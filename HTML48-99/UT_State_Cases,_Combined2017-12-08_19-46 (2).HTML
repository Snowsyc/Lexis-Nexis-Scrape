CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant investigator sought review of the judgment by the trial court of the Third District, Salt Lake County (Utah), which convicted him of tampering with a witness in violation of Utah Code Ann. § 76-8-508 (1978) and official misconduct in violation of Utah Code Ann. § 76-8-201(1978).


OVERVIEW: The investigator sent a report to the fire marshal. The fire marshal did not, at first, produce the report in a civil suit. The grand jury indicted the investigator. The trial court convicted the investigator of tampering with a witness and official misconduct. The court affirmed in part and reversed in part. The court concluded that the record contained sufficient evidence from which the jury could conclude that the investigator knowingly or intentionally attempted to induce the fire marshal to withhold the report from the official investigation. Since the investigator knew he was the target of the grand jury investigation before testifying and was informed of his right to counsel, the court held that his testimony was properly used during trial. The court held the trial court did not err in excluding the juror affidavit.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the trial court's conviction of appellant for witness tampering, but reversed the trial court's conviction of appellant for official misconduct.

OUTCOME: The appeal was dismissed.
