OVERVIEW: Relators sought a writ of mandamus compelling the respondent, a district court judge, to grant a jury trial in an action brought against them under 42 U.S.C.S. § 1983. The court declined to issue the writ because entitlement to a jury trial in a § 1983 action depends on the nature of the underlying action. OUTCOME: Writ of mandamus declined; despite a punitive damage claim, which was neither compensatory or nominal, the nature of the underlying action was equitable in nature. Therefore, relators were not entitled to a jury trial.
