February 24, 1967 


SUBSEQUENT HISTORY:  [**1] Rehearings Denied June 30, 1967.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant taxpayers sought review of decisions by the circuit court (Kentucky), which upheld orders issued by appellee, Kentucky Board of Tax Appeals, which assessed additional income taxes against the taxpayers. The taxpayers attacked the general statutory scheme under which the taxes were imposed, alleging unconstitutionality because the taxpayers were foreign corporations and claimed that they were being unfairly taxed.


OVERVIEW: The basic question presented was whether the dividend income from the taxpayers, wholly owned corporations, must have been included in the taxpayers' taxable net income, for the purpose of apportioning income taxes payable to the State of Kentucky, under the provisions of Ky. Rev. Stat. § 141 in effect during the tax years involved. The taxpayers argued that it was unconstitutional for the state to tax income from property or activities that had no relationship to the doing of business in Kentucky. The only income the taxpayers contended should not have been included for apportionment purposes was the separately identifiable dividend income from wholly owned corporate subsidiaries operating in foreign jurisdictions. The court reversed the decisions of the circuit court, finding that the determining factor was not whether the dividend income may have been characterized as "business" or "nonbusiness," but whether it arose from a source integrated with business carried on in Kentucky. The court found that if properly includable as "gross income," it should have been deductible (before the apportionment formula was applied) as nontaxable dividend income.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the judgments by the circuit court in the taxpayers' challenge to the Board's orders and the orders of the Board were set aside in both cases.

CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant landowners sought review of a judgment entered by the trial court (Kentucky) in favor of appellee sewer construction district awarding the landowners one dollar each for the easements created over the landowners' property for the purpose of building a sewer project.


OVERVIEW: The sewer construction district built a new sewer over the landowners' property. Such was not the case here where the entire cost of the improvement was paid by the landowners through special assessments.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the judgment of the trial court.
