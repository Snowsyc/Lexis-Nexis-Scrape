PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant motor vehicle operators sought review of an order of the State Corporation Commission (Virginia), which assessed the taxpayers with gross receipts road taxes pursuant to Va. Code Ann. § 58-638 (1950).


OVERVIEW: The operators transported United States mail for compensation. The operators appealed the Commission's assessment of gross receipts road tax. The operators contended that since they were engaged solely in the carriage of mail upon terms and conditions fixed by the Postmaster General of the United States, they were engaged in a purely governmental function, and, therefore, not subject to the imposition of the tax. The court found that the operators carried the mail under written contracts between them and the Postmaster General. The court found that they were not officers or employees of the Federal Government or of any of its departments. They court found that the operators were independent contractors doing certain prescribed work for the government at fixed compensation. The court held that however extensively the government may have reserved the right to specify and control the actions of the operators in carrying the mail, neither the reservation nor the exercise of that power gave to the operators the status of a representative or agent of the government.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the final order of the Commission assessing the operators with gross receipts road taxes.

PROCEDURAL POSTURE: The commonwealth challenged a judgment from the Corporation Court of Danville (Virginia), which acquitted defendants of operating a photographer's business without a license in violation of Va. Code Ann. § 58-393.


OVERVIEW: Defendants were acquitted on a charge of operating a photographer's business without a license in violation of Va. Code Ann. § 58-393. The trial court held that § 58-393, if construed to cover defendants' operations, would have been invalid because it imposed an undue burden on interstate commerce in violation of the Commerce Clause, U.S. Const. art. I, § 8. The commonwealth contended on appeal that interstate commerce was not involved. The court affirmed the judgment that found defendants not guilty. The court held that § 58-393 was not applicable to defendants. The court found that interstate commerce was involved in the transaction that was the basis for the charge. The court concluded that the license tax under § 58-393 imposed an unlawful burden on interstate commerce when applied to defendants' activities. The court determined that the act of a cameraman was not subject to state taxation because it was not a local incident sufficiently separate and distinct from the sum of other functions and activities that constituted interstate commerce.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment against the commonwealth that acquitted defendants of operating a photographer's business without a license.
