CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant paralegal sought review of the decision of the Superior Court of Kitsap County (Washington), which convicted him of the unlawful practice of law, a misdemeanor, in violation of Wash. OVERVIEW: Although he did not represent himself as an attorney, defendant performed legal research, drafted pleadings, and performed other such legal work for his clients. OUTCOME: The court affirmed defendant's conviction for the unlawful practice of law and on theft and collection agency violations.

PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed his conviction by the King County Superior Court (Washington) for one count of brandishing a weapon in violation of Wash. Rev. Code § 9.41.270.


OVERVIEW: Subsequent to his conviction for brandishing a weapon, defendant appealed. The court affirmed and held that the restriction in Wash. Rev. The court rejected the claim that the statute was overbroad because the restriction on self-defense was minimal.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the conviction for brandishing a weapon and the sentence.

The court reversed the decision of the superior court, holding that the city had the authority under its police power to enact the ordinance because the Washington Legislature did not expressly preempt the area of contributing to the dependency of minors, the Legislature did not preempt the area by implication, and the ordinance was not in conflict with the state's child abuse reporting statute, Wash. Rev. Code § 26.44.030.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the order of the superior court and directed that defendant's conviction for contributing to the dependency of a minor be reinstated.
