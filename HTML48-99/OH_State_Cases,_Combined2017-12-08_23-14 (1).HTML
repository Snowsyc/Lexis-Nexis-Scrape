CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellants, two trucking business operators and a number of semi-tractor truck owners, sought review of an order of the Warren County Court of Common Pleas (Ohio), which granted partial summary judgment in favor of appellees, a village and a number of village council members, and a village mayor, and dismissed the business operators and truck owners' claim under 42 U.S.C.S. § 1983.


OVERVIEW: A village council enacted an ordinance restricting the location where trucks could operate and banned the parking of trucks within a village. Two trucking business operators and a number of semi-tractor truck owners were fined for breaking that law. They commenced suit against the village, the village council and the village mayor under 42 U.S.C.S. § 1983. The village, the village council, and the village mayor filed a motion for summary judgment, which the trial court granted. The business operators and the truck owners sought review. OUTCOME: The court affirmed the order of the trial court that granted partial summary judgment in favor of a village, a village council members, and the village mayor and dismissed two business operators and a number of truck owners' claims under § 1983.

The trial court resentenced the prisoner as mandated. The director declined to change the prisoner's records to reflect a definite sentence. The prisoner filed a complaint in mandamus. The director filed a motion to dismiss, which was overruled by the court. The director filed an answer and a motion for reconsideration, which was overruled by the court. The court granted the writ of mandamus, ordering the director to modify the prisoner's records. The appellate court's decision was binding on the State under the doctrine of res judicata. Even if it was erroneous, the failure of the State to seek a proper review of the appellate court's judgment made that judgment binding upon the director as an agent of the State. Ohio Rev. Code Ann. § 5145.01 did not confer power to review judicial determinations upon the Director, but only the authority to correct clerical, inadvertent, or ministerial errors.


OUTCOME: The court granted the prisoner's complaint for a writ of mandamus ordering the Director to modify the prisoner's records to reflect the sentences of definite terms of imprisonment imposed by the trial court pursuant to the mandate of the appellate court.
