CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant fathers sought review of a decision of the Jefferson Circuit Court (Kentucky), holding that Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. § 406.021(1), was unconstitutional as failing to afford equal protection to illegitimate children. Appellee commonwealth had appealed on behalf of a mother, and the circuit court had consolidated 153 appeals.


OVERVIEW: A prior version of Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. § 406.031 provided for a three-year limitations period. However, the Kentucky Supreme Court held that the three-year period failed to afford equal protection to illegitimate children and was therefore unconstitutional. The state court had based its decision on prior United States Supreme Court cases, which held that similar limitations periods did not provide illegitimate children with an adequate opportunity to obtain support and did not substantially relate to the state's interest in avoiding litigation of stale or fraudulent claims. The Kentucky General Assembly then adopted a four-year statute of limitations. The court, adopting the circuit court's opinion, held that the four-year period was also unconstitutional. The possible difficulties of proof did not overcome the concrete human rights of illegitimate children. Because invalidation of § 406.031 left Kentucky without a statute of limitations in paternity actions, the five-year time limit set out in Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. § 413.120(2) applied. The five-year time limit did not run during the minority of any person entitled to bring an action pursuant to Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. § 406.021(1).


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the circuit court's decision and adopted its opinion.
