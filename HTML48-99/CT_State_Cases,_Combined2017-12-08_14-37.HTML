CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Movant employer, who employed plaintiff employee who was injured by defendant manufacturer's machine, filed a motion to intervene as co-plaintiff to recover benefits it had paid and might become obligated to pay pursuant to the Workers' Compensation Act. The employee opposed the intervention on the basis of Conn. Gen. Stat. § 52-572r(c) of the Product Liability law.


OVERVIEW: The employee was injured while operating a machine that the employer had purchased from the manufacturer. The purpose of the sought-for intervention was to enable the employer to be reimbursed from any judgment obtained by the employee for amounts it had paid and might become obligated to pay pursuant to the Workers' Compensation Act. In response to the employee's opposition, the employer asserted that the employee was hired well before the effective date of Conn. Gen. Stat. § 52-572r(c). The court held that the employer's right to recover from a third party was derived from the employee, and that the "date of injury rule" determined rights and obligations of parties. The court, therefore, held that intervention by the employer as a co-plaintiff was barred by § 52-572r(c). Alternatively, the employer contended that if § 52-572r(c) precluded intervention, it was unconstitutional because it did not bear a rational relationship to any legitimate purpose for which it was enacted. The court held that the purpose of § 52-572r(c) was a reduction in the number of product liability actions filed, a purpose on which the General Assembly could legitimately act.


OUTCOME: The court denied the employer's motion to intervene as co-plaintiff.

OUTCOME: Defendant's motion to strike plaintiff's prayer for relief requesting double and treble damages under Conn. Gen. Stat. § 14-295 was granted.
