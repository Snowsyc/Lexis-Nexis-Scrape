Proof of his drug use on the day of the crime showed his state of mind during the crime and his condition during the interrogation. Drug use was part of the crime. It was not inadmissible. There was no error in the denial of his challenges to the grand and traverse juries. Excusing four jurors opposed to the death penalty was not error, nor was the refusal to excuse a juror who had heard about the crime but said that she could impartially evaluate the evidence.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed defendant's convictions for rape and aggravated sodomy. The court reversed the murder conviction and the death sentence.

PRIOR HISTORY: Interlocutory appeal. Lowndes Superior Court. Before Judge Horkan.



DISPOSITION: Judgment affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant motor carrier, which was engaged solely in interstate commerce, challenged the decision of the Lowndes Superior Court (Georgia), which allowed plaintiff injured motorist to join defendant, the motor carrier's liability insurer, in the motorist's personal injury action against the motor carrier.


OVERVIEW: The motorist brought a personal injury action against the motor carrier for injuries that he received in a collision in the county of the trial court involving the pickup truck in which the motorist was traveling and a tractor-trailer owned by the motor carrier. The trial court found that the motor carrier's truck was engaged solely in interstate commerce. When the motorist sought to add the motor carrier's liability insurer as a party, the insurer objected on the ground that the motor carrier was not an intrastate carrier, so there could be no joinder under Ga. Code Ann. § 46-7-58(e). The trial court allowed the amendment, and the motor carrier sought review. On appeal, the court affirmed, holding that § 46-7-58(e), which allowed the joinder of a surety where a bond was given, was applicable to interstate as well as intrastate carriers. Although state law was preempted insofar as it might seek to protect interstate passengers and property being transported in interstate commerce, Georgia's interest in protecting the public in using its highways was not preempted by federal law. Because § 46-7-58(e) was enacted to protect the public on Georgia highways, it was not preempted.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the decision allowing the injured motorist to join the motor carrier's liability insurer, despite the fact that the motor carrier was engaged solely in interstate commerce, in the motorist's personal injury action.
