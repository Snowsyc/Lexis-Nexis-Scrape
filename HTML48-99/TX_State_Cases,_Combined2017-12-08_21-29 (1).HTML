Texaco Inc. v. Gideon


Nos. 11063 and 11064


Court of Civil Appeals of Texas, Austin


366 S.W.2d 628; 1963 Tex. App. LEXIS 1993



April 3, 1963 


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant leasehold owner sought review of judgments of a District Court of Travis County (Texas) that adjudicated appellee royalty owner's interest in certain oil producing lands. Appellant contended that pursuant to Tex. Rev. Civ. Stat. Ann. art. 1995, §14, venue for the action was exclusively in the county where the lands were located.


OVERVIEW: Holding that the purpose, and not the form or style, of the action was dispositive for the purpose of venue, the court reversed the judgment and remanded with instructions to transfer the causes. Tex. Rev. Civ. Stat. Ann. art. 1995, §14, provided that suits for the recovery of lands had to be brought in the county in which the land, or part thereof, lay. Section 14 was mandatory, and controlled over Tex. Rev. Civ. Stat. Ann. art. 1995, § 5, 27, even if the latter rules were otherwise applicable. The court noted that royalties were considered interests in land, and therefore § 14 was applicable to suits involving royalty interests. The ultimate or dominant purpose of the suit determined whether a suit falls under § 14, not how the cause of action was described. Thus, notwithstanding that appellee royalty owner's right or interest was evidenced by a written contract, and his suit was for a declaration concerning the rights flowing from the contract and whether appellant had breached it, the action was not one for breach of contract for venue purposes.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the judgments and remanded the causes for transfer to the county of the land in question for trial on the merits. The court held that appellee royalty owner's suit was, at base, one to determine his interest in lands, and therefore venue was proper in the county of those lands.
