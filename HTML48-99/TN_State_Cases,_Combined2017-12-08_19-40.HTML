CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant challenged his conviction for driving under the influence (DUI) from the Rutherford County Circuit Court (Tennessee), arguing that the statutory presumption of intoxication for multiple DUI offenses violated his right to equal protection.


OVERVIEW: Appellant was convicted of driving a motor vehicle while under the influence of an intoxicant (DUI), his third offense. OUTCOME: Appellant's conviction for driving under the influence of an intoxicant (DUI) was affirmed because appellant's right to equal protection of the law was not violated by the statutory presumption of intoxication for multiple DUI offenders as the state had a legitimate interest in protecting its citizens from repeat offenders.
