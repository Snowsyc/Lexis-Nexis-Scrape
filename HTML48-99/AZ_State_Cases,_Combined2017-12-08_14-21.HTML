PRIOR HISTORY: Appeal from the Superior Court of Pima County

Cause No. CR-05915

Honorable Alice Truman, Judge



DISPOSITION: Judgment affirmed; sentence vacated and remanded for resentencing.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed from a judgment of the Superior Court of Pima County (Arizona), which convicted him of second degree burglary, in violation of Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 13-1507, and sentenced him to more than 11 years in prison, to run consecutively to other sentences he was serving.


OVERVIEW: Defendant claimed that the trial court erred in refusing to admit jail medical records in relation to an insanity defense. He also contended that his trial attorney could not waive defendant's presence for the exercise of peremptory challenges and that the trial court erred in failing to state a reason for imposing a consecutive sentence. On review, the court upheld the convictions but not the imposition of a consecutive sentence. The trial judge had directed defendant to select those parts of the jail medical exhibit that were relevant so they could be admitted, but defendant failed to do so. Defendant was entitled to be present at critical stages of the proceedings, and such stages generally included the peremptory challenges. However, the court held that counsel could waive defendant's presence at such proceedings as a matter of trial strategy. The court found no basis for setting aside the jury's unanimous finding of guilt. The court concluded, however, that the trial court erred in failing to explain the reasons for the consecutive sentence, as required by Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 13-708. Thus, defendant was entitled to resentencing.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the conviction, but it vacated and remanded for resentencing. If the sentence imposed was again made consecutive to other sentences, the trial court was instructed to comply with the applicable statute.
