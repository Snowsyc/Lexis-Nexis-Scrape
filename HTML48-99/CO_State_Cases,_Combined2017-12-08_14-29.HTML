CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: The state appealed an order of the Eagle County District Court (Colorado) dismissing the county court convictions of two defendants for kayaking on closed waters in violation of Colo. Rev. Stat. § 33-31-114 (1973), on the grounds that the statute could not serve as a constitutional basis for criminal prosecution of the defendants.


OVERVIEW: For safety reasons, after two deaths, the sheriff closed a river to anyone using tubes, rafts, or kayaks. The sheriff's order was based on Colo. Rev. Stat. § 33-31-114 (1973). Two kayakers were later observed on the closed waters. They were issued a summons and complaint for violating Colo. Rev. Stat. § 33-6-127(2) (1973). The court affirmed. Colo. Rev. Stat. § 33-31-114 gave enforcement officials the authority to close a river to boating when the condition of the water constituted a hazard to human life and safety but it could not be applied constitutionally to convict defendants because it had no express provision making it unlawful to disobey an order closing a body of water. As the General Assembly did not choose to apply criminal sanctions to one who failed to obey a sheriff's order prohibiting boating, defendants were not subject to criminal charges for their kayaking.


OUTCOME: The reversal of defendants' county court conviction for kayaking on closed waters and dismissal of the state's charges against them was affirmed, as the legislature had not chosen to criminalize violations of a sheriff's order prohibiting boating or closing waters for safety reasons. The statute authorizing sheriffs to issue such orders could not alone be used to impose criminal sanctions.
