677 S.W.2d 476; 1984 Tenn. Crim. App. LEXIS 2533



July 26, 1984 


PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] ROANE COUNTY

HON. E. EUGENE EBLEN, JUDGE

Burglary in the Third Degree, Safecracking; Grand Larceny)



DISPOSITION: AFFIRMED.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendants sought review of their convictions entered on jury verdicts in a trial court of Roane County (Tennessee) of burglary and safecracking. Defendants claimed that because they had no intention of opening a safe while in the premises their convictions under Tenn. Code Ann. § 39-3-404(b)(1) should be reversed and that the trial court erred when it answered a question propounded by the jury during deliberations.


OVERVIEW: Defendants and others broke into a store. When they found the store safe could not be moved, they backed a truck up to the store intending to load the safe onto the truck. Defendants claimed that because the safe was not opened on the premises, there was no offense under § 39-3-404(b)(1). Defendants also claimed that the trial court's answer to a jury question regarding an indeterminate sentence was in error because it informed the jury of the role of the parole board in determining whether defendants would serve a minimum or maximum sentence. The court affirmed. Attempts to open a safe were unlawful. The fact that the safe was moved to the truck for transportation elsewhere did not diminish from the fact that an opening was intended. The trial court did err in telling the jury that how long defendants stayed in jail was determined by the parole board. The matter of the future disposition of defendants was not a proper consideration by a jury when it was determining the length of defendants' sentence. However, the court found the error harmless because it was clear by the minimum sentence imposed that the jury was not prejudiced by the erroneous instruction.


OUTCOME: The judgment of the trial court was affirmed.

CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant challenged a judgment of the Criminal Court for Sullivan County (Tennessee), which convicted him of arson and found him to be an habitual criminal. OVERVIEW: Defendant's apartment was set on fire. Two witnesses testified that defendant offered to pay them to pour kerosene in the apartment and to set it on fire.
