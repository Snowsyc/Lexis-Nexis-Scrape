State v. Merritt


Consolidated Nos. 57367, 58896


Court of Appeals of Missouri, Eastern District, Division Two


805 S.W.2d 337; 1991 Mo. App. LEXIS 351



March 12, 1991, Filed


PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] Appeal from the Circuit Court of St. Francois County; Hon. Kenneth Wayne Pratte, Judge, Hon. Stan J. Murphy, Judge.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed from a decision of the Circuit Court of St. Francois County (Missouri), which convicted defendant of felony resisting arrest in violation of Mo. Rev. Stat. § 575.150.


OVERVIEW: A police officer was conducting undercover surveillance of a suspected drug house. The officer observed defendant go into the house, leave shortly thereafter, and then drive to a liquor store. The officer approached defendant when he suspected a drug buy was taking place. The officer observed what he thought was marijuana in defendant's car. When the officer approached and told him he was under arrest, defendant sped off. The court held that there was sufficient evidence to convict under Mo. Rev. Stat. § 575.150. While the evidence may not have supported the finding of an actual drug transaction such a finding was not necessary. The statute only required the officer to contemplate making a felony arrest. The officer clearly believed a drug transaction had taken place and made his decision to arrest based on that legitimate belief. Defendant's resistance was by a means other than flight, as the officer was struck by defendant's vehicle and partially dragged when defendant left the scene. Defendant was clearly aware the officer was from the police because the officer identified himself and showed his badge to defendant.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed.

CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: The State appealed from a decision of the Circuit Court of Jackson County (Missouri) that granted respondent's motion to dismiss a petition for forfeiture under the Criminal Activity Forfeiture Act (CAFA), as contained in Mo. Rev. Stat. §§ 513.600 to 513.645 (1986).


OVERVIEW: The State challenged the trial court's grant of respondent's motion to dismiss a petition for forfeiture under the CAFA. On appeal, the sole issue was whether the time limitations for state action after a seizure of property that had been used or intended for use or derived from criminal activity were mandatory. The court noted that where, as here, a statutory provision did not provide what results should follow a failure to comply with its terms, it was generally held to be directory. The court held that because a forfeiture was involved, even though no punitive means were provided, the time limitations in CAFA were mandatory. The court refused to broaden the scope of the legislative enactment to permit a forfeiture where it was not clearly authorized. The court noted that if the legislature decided to relax the requirements it had seen fit to impose in § 513.607.5(2), then it alone must do so. Accordingly, the court affirmed the judgment of the trial court.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the decision of the trial court that granted respondent's motion to dismiss a petition for forfeiture under the Criminal Activity Forfeiture Act.
