CASE SUMMARY:

The Chief had found that the corporation failed to properly reclaim land that it had strip mined and thereby forfeited a portion of a reclamation performance bond.


OVERVIEW: The corporation strip mined a property consisting of 18.2 acres of land. Following the termination of the strip mining, the Chief found that the corporation failed to properly reclaim the land as required by Ohio Rev. OUTCOME: The court affirmed the trial court's decision upholding the Chief's order that the corporation failed to properly reclaim the strip mined land, and holding that the corporation forfeited a portion of its reclamation performance bond.

NO. 9-105


COURT OF APPEALS, ELEVENTH APPELLATE DISTRICT, LAKE COUNTY, OHIO


1982 Ohio App. LEXIS 12045



November 26, 1982 


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant challenged a judgment of the Mentor Municipal Court (Ohio) finding him guilty of possession of marijuana, a misdemeanor. The assigned error was whether the trial court erred in placing defendant on three years probation in addition to ordering him to pay the maximum fine of $ 100.00.


OVERVIEW: The court had previously held that the Mentor City Ordinance that imposed a more severe penalty for possession of marijuana than that imposed by the state statute was unconstitutional. The court observed that the nature of the city penalty was significantly changed when the local ordinance imposed a jail sentences for possession of less than 100 grams of marijuana when the same offense was treated by state statute as a minor misdemeanor punishable only by a fine. The court also noted that the trial court had subsequently attempted to impose an additional penalty, namely probation of three years in addition to the fine. The court reversed in part, eliminating the sentence of probation, and affirmed in part as to the $ 100 fine. The court held that, in addition to the fact that the city could not impose a more severe penalty for the offense than was provided by state law, the trial court could not impose probation where there was not imprisonment. Ohio law provided for probation in the context of suspending a sentence of imprisonment and providing for supervision by the probation department in lieu of jail time. As there was no imprisonment involved, probation could not be imposed.


OUTCOME: The court modified in part so as to eliminate the sentence of probation, and affirmed in part as to the $ 100.00 fine.
