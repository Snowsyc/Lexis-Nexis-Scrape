CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellants, a state industrial commission and the state board of regents, sought review of a judgment of the Circuit Court for Dane County (Wisconsin), which reversed the industrial commission's decision to deny respondent claimant's request for unemployment benefits.


OVERVIEW: The claimant was employed by a university as an assistant cataloguer. Even though he had the qualifications as a teacher, he did not teach. The industrial commission denied the claimant's request for unemployment benefits pursuant to Wis. Stat. § 108.02(5)(f)4, which provided that teachers in educational institutions supported by public funds were not entitled to such benefits. The court held that (1) the term "teacher" was not defined by the Act and as such, it had to be construed in conformity with and to promote the Act's objects and purposes; (2) the claimant had the qualifications of a teacher and even though he did not teach, he enjoyed all the benefits and status of a teaching member of the faculty; (3) the fact that the claimant did not actually teach was not controlling as he had to accept the disadvantages of a teacher along with the benefits; and (4) the claimant was a teacher for purposes of the Act and as such, he was not entitled to unemployment benefits.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the trial court's judgment and directed the trial court to reinstate the industrial commission's decision.
