October 21, 1971 


SUBSEQUENT HISTORY:  [**1] Rehearing Denied November 17, 1971. Review Denied December 14, 1971.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant, the Arizona State Tax Commission (Commission), challenged a judgment from the trial court (Arizona), which treated plaintiff taxpayer's motion for a judgment on the pleadings as one for summary judgment and granted the motion in favor of the taxpayer after the taxpayer filed a complaint for the refund of taxes, penalties, and interest paid under protest.


OVERVIEW: The taxpayer attacked the application of the taxes in question to a lease of mining equipment. On appeal, the court reversed the judgment of the trial court in favor of the taxpayer and remanded the case after determining that the Commission was entitled to have its motion for judgment on the pleadings sustained and judgment entered in the Commission's favor because the taxpayer's complaint was insufficient. In pertinent part, the court first held that the tax and the exemption the taxpayer sought to apply to itself, pursuant to Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 42-1312.01(A)(2), was a tax on, and an exemption of, businesses selling tangible personal property, not an exemption of businesses leasing or renting such property. In addition, the court held that the classification exempting sales without exempting rentals was not a violation of the equal protection clause where the classification was reasonable and not arbitrary. In this situation, the court noted that the reasonableness of the classifications could be found in the differences between the two categories of business in regard to financing, customer and supplier relationships, and income. Accordingly, the court remanded the case.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the judgment of the trial court in favor of the taxpayer and remanded the case after determining that the Commission was entitled to have its motion for judgment on the pleadings sustained and judgment entered in the Commission's favor.

OVERVIEW: The old high school district was comprised of two elementary school districts. OUTCOME: The court affirmed the trial court's judgment.
