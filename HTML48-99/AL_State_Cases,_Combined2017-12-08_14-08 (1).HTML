CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant employer appealed an order of the Jefferson Circuit Court (Alabama), which found that plaintiff employee had sustained a 31 percent loss of earning capacity and awarded him benefits in a workmen's compensation case.


OVERVIEW: The employee filed a complaint against the employer for workmen's compensation benefits, alleging an on-the-job injury. The trial court awarded benefits. On appeal, the employer contended that the employee did not sufficiently rebut the presumption that he had sustained no loss of earning capacity because he returned to the same job, and his post-injury earnings exceeded his pre-injury earnings. OUTCOME: The judgment of the trial court was affirmed.

PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant mother sought review from a decision of the Houston Juvenile Court (Alabama), which entered an order confirming the findings and recommendations of the referee who's order had restrained the parents from hindering, interfering, or in any way preventing the Department of Human Resources (DHR) from completing its investigation of the allegations of child abuse.

CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant sought review of a judgment of the Circuit Court of Jefferson County (Alabama), which convicted defendant for entering a department store's restaurant and refusing to leave after being warned in violation of Birmingham, Ala., City Code § 1436 (1944).


OVERVIEW: Defendant, together with another person, seated himself at the restaurant and was unable to get service from the waitresses. Defendant was then asked to leave, and he refused. A police officer was called, and the parties were subsequently arrested. Defendant contended on appeal that the complaint was insufficient to support a prosecution and that the complaint was so vague and uncertain as to not apprise defendant of what he was called upon to defend. As a private business, the store had a right to revoke defendant's status at will.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the trial court's judgment.

Hill v. State

COURT OF APPEALS OF ALABAMA


38 Ala. App. 404; 88 So. 2d 880; 1956 Ala. App. LEXIS 196



January 17, 1956 


SUBSEQUENT HISTORY:  [**1] Rehearing Denied February 7, 1956.



DISPOSITION: Affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant challenged the judgment of the County Court of DeKalb County (Alabama), which convicted him under an affidavit or complaint that charged that within 12 months before the making thereof, he did display, handle, exhibit, or use a poisonous or dangerous snake or reptile in such manner as to endanger the life and health of various persons. The county court overruled defendant's motion to exclude the evidence and discharge him.


OVERVIEW: Defendant was a member of a branch of the religious sect which believed in the handling of poisonous snakes as a test of faith. A witness for the prosecution was a member of the branch that did not have that belief. The charges arose when the witness was invited to a meeting of the snake-handling group. Defendant was convicted of violating the statute that provided that it was unlawful for any person to display, exhibit, handle, or use any poisonous or dangerous snake or reptile in such a manner as to endanger the life or health of any person. Defendant argued that the indictment was defective because it did not state the time of the commission of the alleged offense. The court did not sustain defendant's insistence because it was not challenged by a demurrer although demurrer would have been appropriate because time was material when the grade of the offense changed. The statute was not an unconstitutional violation of freedom of religion because the law was enacted for the purpose of restraining and punishing an act which had a tendency to disturb the public peace. It was sufficient to show that the life or health of one person was endangered.


OUTCOME: The evidence was ample to sustain the judgment of conviction and the court's action in overruling the motion for a new trial was without error. Therefore, the judgment was affirmed.
