April 13, 1982 


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: The Family Court (Rhode Island) issued an order pursuant to R. I. Gen. Laws § 14-1-30 to bar petitioners, reporter and newspaper publisher, from a hearing involving a juvenile, and to bar petitioners from an future hearings unless they agreed in advance not to publish the names of juveniles. Petitioners applied for a writ of certiorari to review the order and the validity of § 14-1-30. The petition was granted and the writ was issued.


OVERVIEW: Petitioners published the name of a juvenile who was involved in a family court hearing. Pursuant to § 14-1-30, which excluded all but those with a direct interest in the case from attending hearings involving juveniles, the family court barred the reporter from attending the juvenile's hearings. The family court also barred petitioners from attending any future juvenile hearings unless they agreed in advance not to publish the names of the juveniles. The court granted petitioners' application for a writ of certiorari to review the order. Upon review, the court held that the order was invalid because it barred petitioners for publishing lawfully obtained information; thus, barring them from the proceedings amounted to a penalty for having published information that they were allowed to print. The court further found that the portion that conditioned their attendance at other hearings was impermissibly overbroad and an unconstitutional prior restraint on the press. However, the court held that the invalidity of the order did not extend to the statute, and the fact that members of the press could be excluded under § 14-1-30 did not render the statute unconstitutional.


OUTCOME: The court held that the family court order barring petitioners from the then current and future hearings was invalid. However, the court held that the statute pursuant to which the order was entered was not unconstitutional. Thus, the petition for certiorari was granted in part and denied in part. The matter was remanded to the family court.

CASE SUMMARY:

OUTCOME: The court reversed the judgment, which had been in favor of the retailers, that had held that the Sunday closing law was unconstitutional. The court remanded to the superior court for further proceedings.

This appeal was a de novo appeal with a right to trial by jury.


OUTCOME: The court granted the petition for certiorari, quashed the judgments appealed from, and remanded the case to the district court with direction that defendant be given an opportunity to perfect his claim to a jury trial.
