Lund v. County of Hennepin


File No. TC-3611


MINNESOTA TAX COURT COUNTY OF HENNEPIN, FOURTH JUDICIAL DISTRICT


1985 Minn. Tax LEXIS 26



October 30, 1985 


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Petitioner homeowners sought review of property taxes levied on their homestead by respondent Hennepin County (Minnesota) on the basis that Minn. Stat. § 273.13, subd. 7, Class C, relating to homestead property, unconstitutionally taxed the same class of properties at differing rates.


OVERVIEW: The homeowners challenged the imposition of property taxes based on a graduated assessment of market value for their property. In support of their position, they argued that the assessment of homestead property at a differing rate than rental apartment property violated the uniformity provisions of the Minnesota Constitution. They also contended that a person in possession of property as an owner or renter should be subject to the same assessment and that § 273.13, subd. 7, Class C was a special law that created a preference. The court rejected both arguments, holding that the statute was a constitutional exercise of the state taxing power under Minn. Const. art. IX, § 1. The court found that the state could distinguish between renters and property owners because the ability to pay was not a requirement of the statute, and that the tax was uniform because it treated the same class of property in the same manner, despite the fact that it appeared inequitable.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the county's assessment and tax.

OVERVIEW: The taxpayer was in the business of prefabricating custom-designed home packages. The taxpayer's customers were general contractors. OUTCOME: The court affirmed the Commissioner's order.

CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Petitioner taxpayer challenged the way in which the assessor of respondent County of Hennepin, Minnesota, applied the homestead classification to his apartment building pursuant to Minn. The assessor granted the homestead classification to the unit occupied by the taxpayer and classified the rest of the property as "3d," which was residential real estate having four units or more under § 273.13, subd. 19.


OVERVIEW: The taxpayer owned and resided in one unit of a 13-unit building and rented the other 12 units to the public. The taxpayer contended that inasmuch as he owned the building in which he resided, the entire building should have been accorded homestead status notwithstanding the fact that 12 of 13 distinct units within the building were occupied by others. Thus, as to apartment buildings generally, the legislature clearly expressed its intent that the non-homestead rates applied. The fact that the owner of the apartment building actually resided in one of the apartment units did not, in the court's opinion, justify a departure from the legislative scheme.


OUTCOME: The court determined that the assessor's classification was correct.

Lewis v. Commissioner of Revenue


Docket No. 3770


MINNESOTA TAX COURT


1983 Minn. Tax LEXIS 30



November 1, 1983 


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant retiree sought review of an order of appellee Commissioner of Revenue (Minnesota), which assessed additional income taxes on retirement benefits earned by the retiree from a company prior to his becoming a resident of Minnesota.


OVERVIEW: In computing his Minnesota gross income for the year 1978, the retiree subtracted the retirement benefits paid on behalf of a company. The Commissioner disallowed the claimed subtraction and the retiree appealed. The court affirmed, holding that there was no question that the income in question was received by the retiree as a retirement benefit from a qualifying plan and was compensation defined by Minn. The court awarded neither interest nor penalties.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the order assessing additional income taxes for the year 1978 and held that the retiree was not entitled to subtract any of his pension income for calendar years 1979, 1980, and 1981. The court did not charge interest on the additional taxes due and assessed no penalties.
