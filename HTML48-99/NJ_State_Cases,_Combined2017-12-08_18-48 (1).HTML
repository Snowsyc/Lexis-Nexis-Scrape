CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant township sought review of the decision from respondent Commissioner, Department of Environmental Protection (New Jersey), which granted real estate tax exemptions pursuant to N.J. OVERVIEW: Respondents, nonprofit corporations and their officers, were granted real estate tax exemptions from respondent Commissioner of the Department of Environmental Protection pursuant to the Green Acre Act (act), N.J. Stat. Ann. §§ 54:4-3.63 to -3.71. Appellant township brought suit against respondents, raising five arguments challenging the validity of the tax exemptions. The court affirmed the tax exemption grants. The court held that the act had not violated N.J. Const. art. The court also held that the act's reference to I.R.C. § 501(c)(3) had not violated N.J. Const. art. IV, § 7, para. 5's prohibition against the incorporation of provisions of other laws.


OUTCOME: The decision of respondent Commissioner, Department of Environmental Protection to grant real estate tax exemptions to respondents, nonprofit organizations, was affirmed because the statutory limitation to nonprofit organizations who owned natural open space land had not violated state constitutional provisions prohibiting the enactment of special tax laws that benefited a specific class or effecting the uniform valuation of property.
