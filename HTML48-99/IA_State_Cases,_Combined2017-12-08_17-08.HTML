Seeman v. Iowa Dep't of Human Servs.


No. 275 / 98-1226


SUPREME COURT OF IOWA


604 N.W.2d 53; 1999 Iowa Sup. LEXIS 315



December 22, 1999, Filed


PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] Appeal from the Iowa District Court for Linn County, L. Vern Robinson, Judge. Appellants challenge enforcement of lien for Medicaid payments against uninsured motorist insurance proceeds.



DISPOSITION: AFFIRMED.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: In an action for declaratory judgment, plaintiffs sought review of an order of the Iowa District Court for Linn County, which ruled that the statutory term "third party" in Iowa Code § 249A.6 (1997) included the injured party's own insurance carrier.


OVERVIEW: Plaintiffs sustained severe and permanent injuries when the motorcycle they were riding was hit by an uninsured and insolvent motorist. Plaintiffs' insurance policy provided no coverage for medical expenses or any other health or hospitalization insurance. Plaintiffs settled with their insurance company for the full value of their uninsured motorist coverage. Defendant department of human services filed a lien for Medicaid payments for the medically needy in accordance with Iowa Code § 249A.6. After defendant's filing of a declaratory judgment action, the trial court ruled that the plain language of § 249A.6 authorized a lien against the insurance proceeds. On appeal, the court affirmed the declaratory judgment action for defendant since § 249A.6(1) applied to proceeds for medical expenses whether recovered from a tortfeasor or an injured party's own insurance carrier.


OUTCOME: In defendant department of human service's action for declaratory judgment, the court affirmed the trial court's ruling that a lien for Medicaid payments may be enforced against uninsured motorist benefits covered by an injured party.
