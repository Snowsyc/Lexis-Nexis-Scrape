OVERVIEW: The employee was one of the 84 eligible casual laborers considered for seniority, but because his total rating score was not among the top 44, he was not selected for seniority employment. CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant teacher sought review of the judgment of the Superior Court for Skagit County (Washington), which affirmed the dismissal of the teacher from his employment with respondent college.


OVERVIEW: The teacher challenged his employment discharge and was given a hearing before a hearing examiner and review committee. The examiner and committee recommended dismissal to the board of trustees. The board adopted the recommendation and terminated the teacher's employment for cause. The teacher contended that his dismissal impermissibly resulted from his having exercised his constitutionally protected right to free speech. On appeal, the court affirmed, holding that the grounds for dismissal were not constitutionally protected actions and were supported by the facts. The college showed by a preponderance of the evidence that it would have reached the same decision in the absence of the protected conduct. Accordingly, the teacher's dismissal did not violate his First Amendment right to free speech. The teacher's continued use of profane language and criticism of other instructors after repeated direction by his superiors constituted insubordination.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment of the trial court and the dismissal of the teacher from his employment at the college.

OVERVIEW: Defendant was found guilty under § 9A.45.020, which provided that a person was guilty of harassment if he threatened to cause bodily injury to another person without lawful authority.
