PROCEDURAL POSTURE: The District Court of Appeal (Florida) certified a petition for certiorari directed to a decision affirming the trial court's holding that an amendment to Fla. Stat. Ann. § 46.01 was unconstitutional because the subject of the amendatory statute was not adequately expressed in its title and the statute dealt with more than one subject, contrary to the provisions of Fla. Const. art. III, § 16.


OVERVIEW: Non-resident defendants contended that an amendment to Fla. Stat. Ann. § 46.01 had rendered it inapplicable to non-residents. The trial court held that the amendment was unconstitutional because the subject of the amendatory statute was not adequately expressed in its title and the statute in question dealt with more than one subject, contrary to the provisions of Fla. Const. art. III, § 16. The amendment, 1963 Fla. Laws ch. 63-572, was a revisor's bill. The statutory revision power was not intended to make changes in the substantive law without express legislative action. The court reversed and held that the revisor's notes must give the reason for each recommended change and were an integral part of a revisor's bill; therefore, they must be considered in interpretation. The revisor's note established that the intent of this amendment was to eliminate an affidavit requirement, not to make the section applicable to non-resident defendants. The court held that the constitutional requirements were inapplicable to revisor's bills where many subjects must be dealt with in one bill to accomplish the purpose of continuing revision and that 1963 Fla. Laws ch. 63-572 was validly enacted.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the decision that an amendment was unconstitutional and held that constitutional requirements were inapplicable to revisor's bills where many subjects must be dealt with in one bill to accomplish purpose of continuing revision. The court held that the amendment was designed to eliminate an affidavit requirement, not to make the section applicable to non-resident defendants.
