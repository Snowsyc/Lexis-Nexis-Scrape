Fabich v. Fabich

SUPREME COURT OF NEW HAMPSHIRE


144 N.H. 577; 744 A.2d 615; 1999 N.H. LEXIS 156; 24 Employee Benefits Cas. (BNA) 1467



December 30, 1999, Decided


SUBSEQUENT HISTORY:  [***1] Released for Publication February 14, 2000.


PRIOR HISTORY: Hillsborough-northern judicial district.



DISPOSITION: Reversed and remanded.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed the order of the Hillsborough-Northern Judicial District Superior Court (New Hampshire) distributing the marital property of plaintiff and defendant in a divorce proceeding.


OVERVIEW: Plaintiff wife filed for divorce from defendant husband. Prior to the judgment of divorce, defendant was injured at work and was permanently disabled. Defendant received accidental disability retirement benefits. The court awarded plaintiff one-half of defendant's monthly benefits. Defendant appealed, arguing that the compensation was for lost income and was to be considered only in determining alimony and support. On appeal, the court reversed, holding that the trial court abused its discretion in awarding plaintiff one-half of defendant's entire monthly benefits. Defendant demonstrated that the benefits were intended at least in part to compensate for his lost earning capacity and pain and suffering. To the extent that the benefits compensated defendant for his lost earning capacity and suffering, they were defendant's separate property and were not subject to an equitable property distribution, but were only available to determine support and alimony obligations.


OUTCOME: The court reversed, holding that plaintiff was not entitled to a marital property distribution of one-half of the entire amount of defendant's accidental disability retirement benefits in divorce proceedings.
