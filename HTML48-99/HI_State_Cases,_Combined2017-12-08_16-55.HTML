Thereafter, the government had the burden to prove by a preponderance of the evidence that the illegally seized evidence was contraband. In reversing and remanding, the court found that the state had not met that burden.


OUTCOME: The court reversed and remanded, finding that the government had the burden to prove that the evidence illegally seized was contraband and under the trial court's order should not be released to defendant.
