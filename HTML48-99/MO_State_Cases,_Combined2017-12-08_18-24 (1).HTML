PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant sought review of a judgment from the Circuit Court of Cass County (Missouri), which denied defendant's Mo. Sup. Ct. R. 27.26 motion, convicted defendant of capital murder, and sentenced defendant to death.


OVERVIEW: The trial court denied defendant's Rule 27.26 motion, convicted defendant of capital murder, and sentenced defendant to death. Civil Appeal

Judge James F. McHenry



DISPOSITION: Reversed and Remanded With Directions


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant nursing home contested the dismissal by the Circuit Court of Cole County (Missouri) of its petition for judicial review of a decision of respondent Department of Social Services (DSS), as modified by respondent Administrative Hearing Commission (AHC). The DSS had determined that the nursing home, a participating vendor in the Missouri Title XIX Medicaid program, had been overpaid for one year. The AHC increased the overpayment amount.


OVERVIEW: The nursing home filed a petition for judicial review of the DSS determination of an overpayment and later filed an amended petition against the DSS and the AHC. The court held that the trial court erred in sustaining the motion filed by the DSS to dismiss for lack of subject matter jurisdiction because the nursing home's first petition gave it subject matter jurisdiction and the second petition did not defeat that jurisdiction. As required by Mo. Rev. Stat. § 621.055 (Supp. 1984), the action to be reviewed was the action of the administrative agency, the DSS, as modified by the AHC. Further, Mo. Rev. Stat. § 621.145 expressly provided that, for the purpose of review, the action of the AHC and the order, if any, of an agency were treated as one decision.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the trial court's dismissal of the nursing home's petition and remanded the cause with directions to reinstate the nursing home's petition for judicial review. The court stated that the AHC was to be removed as a party upon a proper motion.

No. KCD 29556


Missouri Court of Appeals, Western District


581 S.W.2d 465; 1979 Mo. App. LEXIS 3276



May 4, 1979 


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant filed a motion to quash the jury panel challenging the exclusion of women from the panel.


OVERVIEW: Following defendant's conviction of first degree robbery and affirmation of that conviction on appeal, the U.S. Supreme Court held Missouri's jury selection statutes which allowed the exclusion of women from jury service on the sole basis of gender were unconstitutional. That decision had retroactive application. The court therefore withdrew its mandate convicting defendant and held the same for naught. The case was remanded to the trial court and defendant was ordered returned to the county jail to await further proceedings.


OUTCOME: Defendant's conviction was vacated and the court remanded the case to the trial court. The court further ordered that defendant be transported from his present place of confinement to the county jail pending further proceedings in the court below.

Hedges v. County Court for Ray County


No. KCD29516


Court of Appeals of Missouri, Western District


581 S.W.2d 73; 1979 Mo. App. LEXIS 3113



April 30, 1979 


PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] From the Circuit Court of Platte County.

Writ Filed in Circuit Court - Mandamus.

Judge John M. Yeaman.



DISPOSITION: Reversed and Remanded With Directions.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: In plaintiff landowners' action for mandamus and other relief arising from an order by defendant County Court, Ray County (Missouri) to close a public road, the landowners sought review from a judgment of the Circuit Court, Platte County (Missouri), which granted defendant developer's motion to dismiss the mandamus action.


OVERVIEW: The developer petitioned to close and vacate a county road. After a hearing, which the landowners had no notice of and did not attend, the trial court ordered the road closed. After their petition to vacate the order was denied by the trial court, the landowners petitioned for a writ of mandamus ordering the trial court's order vacated, as well as other relief. By stipulation, the trial court issued a temporary injunction allowing the landowners to continue to use the road during the mandamus action. The developer filed a motion to dismiss the mandamus action, which the trial court granted. On appeal, the court reversed, holding that: (1) the trial court's order to close the road was improper under Mo. Rev. Stat. § 228.190 because the road had not been abandoned; (2) the order violated the 14th Amendment and Mo. Const. art. I, § 10, because it deprived the landowners of a property right without notice or due process; and (3) mandamus was authorized by Mo. Rev. Stat. § 536.150 (1969), because the hearing on the developer's petition to close the road was uncontested.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the trial court's judgment dismissing the landowners' petition for mandamus and other relief, and remanded the action to the trial court.

State v. Buford


No. KCD29658


Court of Appeals of Missouri, Western District


582 S.W.2d 298; 1979 Mo. App. LEXIS 2854



April 30, 1979 


SUBSEQUENT HISTORY:  [**1] Motion for Rehearing Overruled, Transfer Denied June 11, 1979. Application to Supreme Court for Transfer Denied July 17, 1979.


PRIOR HISTORY: From the Circuit Court of Jackson County

Criminal Appeal

Judge William J. Marsh.



DISPOSITION: Judgments and Convictions Set Aside; Cause Remanded.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed from the judgment of his convictions of the Circuit Court of Jackson County (Missouri). He was charged under a five-count indictment. His motion to quash the jury panel was overruled. The jury acquitted him on two counts. He was convicted on the remaining counts of first-degree robbery, assault with intent to kill with malice, and assault with intent to kill without malice. He was sentenced to concurrent terms of imprisonment.


OVERVIEW: Defendant urged that the jury selection process denied him a fair cross section of the community, as the questionnaire used by the county, under Mo. Rev. Stat. § 497.130 (1969), resulted in a disproportionate number of women being unavailable for jury service. In 1976, 65,207 questionnaires were mailed and 37,339 prospective jurors responded that they were not eligible or elected not to serve. Of the latter number, 21,884 females declined to serve based on the election to be excused on the basis of gender. In 1977, the year in which defendant was tried, the same jury selection process was utilized. Approximately the same number of females declined jury service solely based on the statutory right to do so. The court reversed the judgments of defendant's convictions and remanded the case for a new trial. He showed that the large discrepancy in females available in the jury selection process occurred not just occasionally, which indicated that the cause of the underrepresentation was systematic. The proof sufficiently showed that the selection of juries under § 497.130, as it affected the option of females to serve as jurors, resulted in a constitutionally impermissible jury wheel.


OUTCOME: The court set aside defendant's judgments and convictions, and remanded the case for a new trial.

Miller v. Whaley


No. 40605


Court of Appeals of Missouri, Eastern District


581 S.W.2d 916; 1979 Mo. App. LEXIS 2294



April 24, 1979 


PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] From the Circuit Court of the City of St. Louis.

Civil Appeal.

Judge Richard J. Mehan.



DISPOSITION: Affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant police officers sought review of the decision of the Circuit Court of the City of St. Louis (Missouri), which affirmed a determination by respondent Board of Police Commissioners that the police officers failed to file a required police report of an incident and, when commanded to file a report several days after the incident, filed a false report.


OVERVIEW: The police officers responded to an assist call relating to a sawed-off shotgun within the pool hall. The Board found that while the police officers were on the premises, two patrons were struck and injured, and the glass part of a jukebox was broken. The police officers were charged with violation of Rule 3, § 3.108(i) and Rule 9, § 9.015 of the Police Manual. The court affirmed the decision of the trial court. The court found that the Administrative Procedure Act, Mo. Rev. Stat. §§ 536.073, 536.077 (1969) did not provide for oral motions for production of evidence and therefore, the denial of the police officers' motion for production of the department internal file was proper. The court reasoned that because the police officers apparently knew of the report's contents, they were not prejudiced by the denial. The court found that neither Rule 3 nor 9 were so vague as to be unconstitutional. The court found that it was apparent from the testimony that custom, usage, and experience informed the police officers of the context of the established procedures that guided their reporting practices, which afforded a reasonable degree of specificity to the application of the rules.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the decision of the trial court to affirm the decision of the Board regarding the police officers' failure to timely file a proper police report.

A. v. A.


No. 39801


Court of Appeals of Missouri, Eastern District


581 S.W.2d 889; 1979 Mo. App. LEXIS 2327



April 24, 1979 


SUBSEQUENT HISTORY:  [**1] Motion for Rehearing Overruled, Transfer Denied May 18, 1979.


PRIOR HISTORY: From the Circuit Court of St. Louis County.

Civil Appeal.

Judge Richard T. Enright.



DISPOSITION: Affirmed as Modified.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Respondent former husband appealed the judgment of the Circuit Court of St. Louis County (Missouri), which granted petitioner former wife's request for a dissolution of the parties' marriage.


OVERVIEW: The husband denied that the marriage was irretrievably broken but filed a cross-bill for legal separation. On appeal, the husband contended that the trial court's finding of irretrievable breakdown was against the weight of the evidence or not supported by substantial evidence. The court held that where one party to the marriage denied irretrievable breakdown, the party seeking dissolution must satisfy the court of one of the five facts listed in Mo. Rev. Stat. §§ 452.320.2(1)(a)-(e). Here, by asking for a legal separation while denying irretrievable breakdown, the husband took the position that while the marriage was not irretrievably broken, in the event the trial court should so find, the decree should be in the form of a legal separation rather than in the form of a decree of dissolution of the marriage. The court found that the wife provided sufficient evidence that the marriage could not be preserved. There was little companionship in the marriage and the husband often threatened the wife and the children. There was substantial evidence in the record of a deteriorating relationship. The court affirmed the child support and modified the maintenance orders.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment, modifying the part of the maintenance award that was contingent upon the wife working less than 30 hours a week; it was open to fraud and confusion.
