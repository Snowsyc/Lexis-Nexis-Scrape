After defendant's discharge, the State filed a request for a finding of operation pursuant to Conn. Gen. Stat. § 14-227b, to which defendant filed a plea in abatement, which was overruled, and a finding of operation was made. Defendant appealed.


OVERVIEW: Six days after defendant's discharge, the State filed a request for a finding, limited to the sole question whether defendant was operating a motor vehicle on a public highway on the date when the offenses charged were alleged to have occurred. A finding of operation was a prerequisite to the operation of Conn. Gen. Stat. 14-227b, which made mandatory on the commissioner of motor vehicles the suspension or revocation of an operator's license, even though he had been found not guilty of driving while intoxicated, simply on the ground that he had refused to take one of the tests specified in Conn. Gen. Stat. § 14-227a. Defendant had refused to take one of the tests, but his plea in abatement argued that the court had no jurisdiction to decide judicially the State's request for a finding because defendant was not within the power of the court. On review, the court held that defendant's plea in abatement should have been sustained. The State was not empowered to request a court finding on operation after defendant, at his election, had been tried to the jury, and the jury had been discharged after acceptance of the verdict by the presiding judge. The case was remanded.


OUTCOME: The case was remanded with direction that the plea in abatement be sustained and the finding of operation made by the court be set aside, and that further proceedings be taken to correct the judgment file so that it would accord with the court's decision.
