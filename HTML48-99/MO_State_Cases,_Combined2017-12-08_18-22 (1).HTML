CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiffs, a superintendent and an auditor of a school board, appealed the judgment of Circuit Court of City of St. Louis (Missouri), which granted a motion to dismiss by defendants, a school board and its treasurer, of plaintiffs' petition to restrain the proposed expenditure associated with bringing the school board under the Workmen's Compensation Act of the State of Missouri.


OVERVIEW: Plaintiffs sought to restrain defendants from expending money on workers compensation insurance. Plaintiffs contended that such expenditure was not authorized by statute nor was it constitutional. The court disagreed. The court found that although no constitutional provision expressly authorizes such an expenditure, if payment for workmen's compensation for the school board's employees was a "grant of public money," the proposed expenditure of public school money was one authorized both by the constitution itself and by legislative action not prohibited by the constitution. The court found that the Workmen's Compensation Law was the exercise by the state of its police power and was to be construed liberally. Mo. Const. art. VI, § 25, empowered the legislature to authorize payments from any public funds into a fund or funds for paying benefits upon retirement, disability, or death to persons employed and paid out of any public fund for educational services, and to their beneficiaries or estates.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment of the trial court.
