PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant property owners sought review of a judgment of the Johnson District Court (Kansas), which dismissed the property owners' declaratory judgment action that contended the Kansas eminent domain statutes, Kan. Stat. Ann. § 26-501 et seq., were unconstitutional.

CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant was convicted in the Sedgwick District Court, Division No. 6 (Kansas), of possession of heroin under the Uniform Narcotic Drug Act, Kan. Stat. Ann. § 65-2501 et seq. Defendant appealed his conviction.


OVERVIEW: An officer obtained a warrant to search "the person, place, thing or means of conveyance" relating to opium suspected to be present in a building. In executing the warrant, the officer found heroin, an opium derivative, in defendant's billfold. He was charged with possession of heroin. The trial court denied defendant's motion to suppress evidence directed at the billfold and heroin. Defendant was convicted of the charged offense. On appeal, the court held that: (1) Kan. Stat. Ann. § 22-2509 (Supp. 1971) did not violate the Fourth Amendment or Kan. Const. Bill Rights § 15 where it only authorized a reasonable detention and search of a person under the conditions specified; (2) where probable cause to believe that a drug was kept or concealed on certain described premises was established to the satisfaction of a proper magistrate, the search of a person found on the premises in the execution of a search warrant was reasonable and necessary to secure effective enforcement of the Act; and (3) the officer's search of defendant was reasonable under § 22-2509 where there was probable cause to believe that opium was concealed on the described premises or on the persons of those present.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the trial court's judgment.

DISPOSITION: Affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant state challenged a judgment of the Shawnee District Court (Kansas), which entered judgment in favor of appellee state director of property valuation (director) and upheld the validity of the directives which were issued to the counties regarding the equalization of assessed valuations of property.


OVERVIEW: The director ordered the board of county commissioners to adopt a resolution directing the county assessor to apply valuation per cent changes as shown in the directive. The directives were issued to comply with the legislative mandate to equalize assessed valuations between adjacent counties pursuant to Kan. Stat. Ann. § 79-1446. The state brought an action seeking a declaratory judgment that the statutes under which the director purported to act were unconstitutional and that his directives were void. Judgment was entered in favor of the director and the state appealed. The state contended the statute was constitutionally objectionable because no provision was made for the giving of notice prior to the time the director acted to equalize the assessments. The court held that notice to individual property owners affected was not required where a general increase was made by a board of equalization. Although some inequality in assessed values would exist where counties bordered on opposite sides of a district boundary, there was good reason to believe there would be less disparity and more equality after the directive was put into effect than before.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment.
