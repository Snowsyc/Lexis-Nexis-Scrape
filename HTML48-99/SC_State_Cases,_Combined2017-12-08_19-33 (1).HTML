PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant was indicted for murder in the commission of an armed robbery. The State sought the death penalty pursuant to S.C. Defendant moved for an order prohibiting the State from seeking the death penalty as South Carolina then had no operative death penalty. A trial court of Georgetown County (South Carolina) granted defendant's motion. The State appealed.


OVERVIEW: The sole question on appeal involved the applicability of the death penalty statute to a crime committed prior to its effective date. When defendant allegedly committed the crime, there was no South Carolina statute, which permitted the death penalty for murder. There was, therefore, no "operative fact," to warn defendant that the State would seek to impose the death penalty if he were convicted. OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment of the trial court.
