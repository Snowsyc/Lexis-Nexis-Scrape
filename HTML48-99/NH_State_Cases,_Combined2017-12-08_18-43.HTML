PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiff school board sought review of a decision by the Superior Court, Belknap County (New Hampshire), which denied the school board's requests in its petition for a declaratory judgment against defendant city to require the city council to increase its appropriation in the budget to the school board.


OVERVIEW: The school board filed a petition for a declaratory judgment to require the city to increase its appropriations for the school board in the original budget. The trial court concluded that it should not intervene and denied the school board's requests. On appeal, the court agreed and issued a declaratory judgment, concluding that the city had control over all appropriations for education subject to at least two limitations. The city could not refuse to appropriate money for statutorily mandated service and programs, such as N.H. Rev. Stat. Ann. §§ 189:1, 1-a, 12, 14-a, 24, 39, 198:1-3. The city also could not refuse to appropriate money to meet the minimum standards required by the State Board of Education. Thus, the school board was required to eliminate or reduce other programs that were desirable or optional but not mandatory as the appropriations in the city budget were sufficient for mandatory programs. The court stated that if a situation occurred where funds were not sufficient to meet the mandatory requirements, then it would be entitled to force the city to provide the funds for the required programs.


OUTCOME: The court ordered a declaratory judgment concluding that the city had appropriated funds sufficient to meet the State Board of Education's minimum standards and other statutory requirements and that the school board had the authority to determine the areas of the school district's budget that would be affected by the city council's reduction. If the budget fell below the required minimums, the school board could pursue further action.
