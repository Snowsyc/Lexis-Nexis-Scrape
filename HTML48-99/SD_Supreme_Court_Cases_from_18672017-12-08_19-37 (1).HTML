CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant bank challenged a judgment of the Circuit Court, Minnehaha County (South Dakota), which held that the bank was liable to sales tax claimed due appellee State on food sold to the bank's employees, repossessed merchandise sold, and use tax on food sold.


OVERVIEW: The bank, duly charted and national, made food and refreshments available to its employees and guests for sale in a cafeteria maintained by it for the convenience of those served. The legal incidence of the sales tax rested on the retailer-seller who was made liable for the payment thereof, and the tax was sustained. A national bank was held not liable for similar local sales tax on purchases made by it. The court held that S.D. Codified Laws § 57.4306(3) (Supp. 1960) required the bank, as a user, to pay the tax directly to the state commissioner of revenue because it was a tax proscribed by U.S. Rev. Stat. § 5219. Thus, the court concluded South Dakota could not, as to a national bank, levy and collect a sales tax on the property it sold nor a use tax on the property it bought. The bank was liable to pay a use tax on the food and refreshments it sold, and where collection was not made, the retailer was liable for default in performance of its duty as such. The bank was liable as debtor to the State in the amount of use tax it failed to collect and pay over to it.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment of the trial court denying the bank recovery for sales tax paid. The judgment denying the bank recovery for the use tax and penalty on materials and supplies purchased outside the state for use by it in its banking business was reversed with directions to enter judgment for the bank in that amount.

State ex rel. Dunker v. Spink Hutterian Brethren


File No. 9627.

May 24, 1958, Opinion Filed


SUBSEQUENT HISTORY:  [***1] Rehearing Denied June 24, 1958.


PRIOR HISTORY: Appeal from Circuit Court, Spink County; Hon. Chas. S. Hanson, Judge.

Action by the attorney for the State of South Dakota to vacate the charter of a communal corporation and to annul its existence. From a judgment for the corporation, the state's attorney appeals.

Judgment modified and as modified affirmed.



DISPOSITION: Judgment modified and as modified affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant state government challenged the judgment of the Circuit Court of Spink County (South Dakota), which held that appellee communal corporation's charter could not be revoked since the statute authorizing such revocation was unconstitutional.


OVERVIEW: Communal corporation was formed for the purpose of acting as a communal colony for its members. After being formed, corporation leased 80 acres of land (augmenting the 5000 acres it already owned). Upon learning of that lease, state government moved to revoke corporation's charter on the theory that the purchase of additional land was an expansion of its activities and, therefore, a violation of state corporation law. Corporation argued that the statute cited was unconstitutionally vague, and the judge assigned the case agreed. State appealed. On appeal, the judgment below was modified, and as modified affirmed. In particular, the court held that the state corporation statute's clause permitting the revocation of corporate charters owing to the expansion of their activities was not unconstitutionally vague, did not improperly embrace multiple subjects, did not impair the obligations of a contract, did not infringe upon religious freedom, and did not deprive corporation of due process. However, it upheld that lower judge's decision on a nonconstitutional basis, i.e., corporation's activities in leasing 80 additional acres did not constitute an expansion of its activities.


OUTCOME: The judgment below was modified, and as modified affirmed.

Proceeding by owner of tract of land having no access to a public highway for writ of mandamus to compel County Commissioners to act upon petition for the laying out of right of way across adjacent lands to public highway. Owner of land across which petitioner had alleged lay the only feasible means for giving access to public highway, intervened and moved for dismissal of proceedings. From the entry of judgment dismissing proceedings, plaintiff appeals.

Affirmed.



DISPOSITION: Affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiff landowner sought review of a decision by the Circuit Court, Pennington County (South Dakota), which granted an intervenor property owner's motion to dismiss plaintiff's complaint for writ of mandamus seeking to acquire a right of way across the property owner's land for access to the public highway.


OVERVIEW: The dismissal was granted on the grounds that the statute authorizing the laying out of a right of way over adjoining lands without the consent of the owner was unconstitutional. Plaintiff contended that the property owner had no interest in the mandamus proceeding; thus, he should not have been granted leave to intervene. The court ruled that the property owner had a sufficient interest in the proceeding to permit him to intervene. The interest that entitled a party to intervene was a direct interest, by which the intervening party was to obtain immediate gain or suffer loss by the judgment, which might be rendered between the original parties. To authorize an intervention, the interest was one that should be created by a claim to, or lien upon the property, which was the subject of litigation. The court reasoned that the property owner was accorded an interest in the litigation where plaintiff specifically demanded a right of way across the property owner's land. Consequently, the property owner had an interest in the litigation because he would obtain an immediate gain or suffer an immediate loss by the judgment rendered.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment granting the property owner's motion to dismiss plaintiff's action.

Gene J. Kadinger and C. W. Chapman were convicted of having engaged in the business of contracting for plumbing work without first procuring a license as required by a Sioux Falls municipal ordinance. From judgments of conviction, the defendants appeal, both cases having been by stipulation consolidated for the purpose of appeal.

Judgment reversed.



DISPOSITION: Judgment reversed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendants appealed a judgment from the Municipal Court of the City of Sioux Falls (South Dakota), which convicted them of having engaged in the business of contracting for plumbing work without a license.


OVERVIEW: The trial court convicted defendants of having engaged in the business of contracting for plumbing work without a license. On appeal, defendants contended that the requirement of the ordinance that an applicant shall have served an apprenticeship for at least three years as shown by an affidavit of a master or journeyman plumber imposed an unreasonable and arbitrary restriction upon a lawful occupation. The trial court's conviction was reversed. The court held that the affidavit requirement prevented otherwise qualified applicants from taking an examination and obtaining a license. The provision that no one would be licensed unless he had been employed as an assistant to a licensed undertaker continuously for three years was unreasonable in that it made essential a particular form of acquiring skill and knowledge. Thus, the provisions that limited the right to an applicant to take an examination to an applicant furnishing an affidavit attesting to his apprenticeship could not be sustained as a reasonable and valid exercise of the police power.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the trial court's judgment that convicted defendants of having engaged in the business of contracting for plumbing work without a license.

PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] Appeal from the Circuit Court of Brule County; Hon. R. C. Bakewell, Judge.

The defendant was convicted of unlawfully killing a migratory water fowl and he appeals.

Judgment affirmed.



DISPOSITION: Judgment affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed his conviction from the Circuit Court of Brule County (South Dakota) for unlawfully killing a migratory water fowl, S.D. Codified Laws § 25.0306(9)(1949).


OVERVIEW: Defendant, a nonresident, was refused a license to hunt migratory water fowl, but proceeded to hunt anyway. Subsequently, he killed a wild goose. He was charged with the unlawful killing of a migratory waterfowl. He appealed the conviction, asking the court to consider whether the enactment of this law, S.D. Codified Laws § 25.0306(9), was within the power of the legislature. On review, the court found nothing that made absolute the right of nonresidents of the state to hunt migratory birds in this state on the same basis as residents. Thus, the court believed that no treaty had such a purpose. Rather, treaties were intended to conserve migratory birds. The state was at liberty to give migratory birds further protection than that given by the treaties, federal laws and regulations. The court stated that nonresidents constituted a peculiar source of evil at which this statute was aimed. It held that the trial court determined that the whole setup of the state's hunting laws and the eligibility for licenses was determined by residence, and no requirement of citizenship was involved. Hence, it affirmed defendant's conviction as within the power of the legislature.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed defendant's conviction for the unlawful killing of a migratory water fowl, finding that the legislature had the power to give migratory birds further protection that that given to them by treaties, federal laws, and regulations.
