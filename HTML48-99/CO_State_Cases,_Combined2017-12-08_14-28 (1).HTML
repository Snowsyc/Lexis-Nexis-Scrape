PRIOR HISTORY: Certiorari to the Colorado Court of Appeals.



DISPOSITION: Judgment Reversed and Cause Remanded.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed a judgment of the Colorado Court of Appeals affirming his conviction by a jury of theft by receiving in violation of Colo. Rev. Stat. § 18-4-410 (1986) and conspiracy to commit theft by receiving in violation of § 18-2-201 (1986). Defendant claimed that the trial court erred in refusing to explain its instructions in response to a jury question and in not allowing his counsel to be heard on the response to the jury's question.


OVERVIEW: At defendant's trial for violating §§ 18-4-410, 18-2-201, the jury asked if suspicion was the same as knowing or believing as recited in § 18-4-410(1). The trial judge only referred the jury to his original instructions. Defendant was convicted and the appellate court affirmed the conviction. On a writ of certiorari the court reversed the appellate court judgment, holding that it was presumed that a jury understood and heeded a trial court's instructions. That presumption, however, was rebuttable. Where, as here, a jury affirmatively indicated that it had a fundamental misunderstanding of an instruction, the basis for the presumption disappeared. The instructions as given were proper, but it was clear that the jury did not know if a suspicion was encompassed within the culpable mental state described in the instructions. The court held that a jury was properly referred back to the instructions only when it overlooked a portion of them or when the instructions were clear. The instructions here did not give a clear answer to the question. Because the jury's confusion related to a central element of the crime, it was prejudicial error to fail to respond adequately to the inquiry.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the appellate court's judgment affirming defendant's conviction for theft by receiving and conspiracy to commit theft by receiving. The court remanded the cause and ordered that defendant's conviction be vacated and that he receive a new trial.
