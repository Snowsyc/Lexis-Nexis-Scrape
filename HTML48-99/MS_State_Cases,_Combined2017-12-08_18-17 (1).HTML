DISPOSITION: AFFIRMED IN PART, REVERSED AND REMANDED IN PART.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellants filed an appeal from an order of the Copiah County Circuit Court (Mississippi), granting summary judgment to appellee city, mayor, board, police chief, and police officer, in suit alleging several claims, including violation of appellants' civil rights under 42 U.S.C.S. § 1983, violation of constitutional rights and state law claims. Appellants asserted that there were genuine issues as to material facts to preclude summary judgment.


OVERVIEW: Police officers arrested and charged appellants for disorderly conduct, resisting arrest and disturbing the peace. Appellants proceeded to trial and were found not guilty by the jury. Subsequently, appellants filed a complaint against appellee city, mayor, board, police chief and police officer in their official and individual capacities, asserting several claims, including violation of 42 U.S.C.S. § 1983, and several constitutional rights. Appellees filed a motion for summary judgment, which was granted on the basis of immunity. Appellants filed an appeal. The court affirmed summary judgment for appellees on all claims except the civil rights claims against appellee police chief and police officer in their individual capacities because, based upon sworn affidavits submitted by both parties in response to the motion for summary judgment, there was a genuine dispute as to the material facts of the case. However, the court held that to hold appellee municipality liable under 42 U.S.C.S. § 1983 for the misconduct of its employees, appellants had to allege that an official policy or custom was a cause in fact of the deprivation of rights inflicted, which appellants failed to do.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed summary judgment for appellee city, mayor, board, police chief and police officer in their official capacities on all claims filed by appellants alleging civil rights violations. However, summary judgment for claims against appellee police chief and police officer in their individual capacities reversed because, based upon sworn affidavits by both parties, there existed a genuine dispute as to material fact.

PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant challenged a decision from the Benton County Chancery Court (Mississippi) terminating her parental rights and granting the adoption of her children to her former husband's wife.

The court held that it was important to remember that appellant was neither seeking custody of her children nor asking for custody with her abusive husband present. The parent-child relationship was not destroyed, and she had not relinquished parental claims. Termination of parental rights was not justified under statutory and case law.

COURT OF APPEALS OF MISSISSIPPI


751 So. 2d 1050; 1998 Miss. App. LEXIS 949



November 10, 1998, Decided


PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] COURT FROM WHICH APPEALED: PIKE COUNTY CIRCUIT COURT. DATE OF JUDGMENT: 11/21/96. TRIAL JUDGE: HON. MIKE SMITH. TRIAL COURT DISPOSITION: THIRD OFFENSE OF OPERATION OF MOTOR VEHICLE WHILE UNDER THE INFLUENCE OF INTOXICATING LIQUOR: SENTENCED TO 3 YRS SUSPENDED; PLACED ON PROBATION FOR 5 YRS UNDER SUPERVISION OF MDOC; PAY FINE IN THE AMOUNT OF $ 2,000.



DISPOSITION: AFFIRMED.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: The Pike County Circuit Court (Mississippi) entered a final judgment of conviction after it accepted defendant's plea of nolo contendre to a third-offense operation of a motor vehicle while under the influence of intoxicating liquor (DUI). Defendant appealed.


OVERVIEW: Defendant was driving his vehicle and approached an intersection. He slowed his vehicle when he saw a roadblock and made a left turn on to a street. A police officer was intercepting vehicles trying to evade the roadblock. As defendant made the turn, the officer signaled defendant to pull over to determine why defendant avoided the roadblock. The officer observed defendant was intoxicated. The officer also saw an empty whiskey bottle on defendant's floorboard. Defendant was arrested and charged with felony DUI in violation of Miss. Code Ann. § 63-11-30(2)(c), which required three convictions for a DUI offense to be a felony. After defendant pled nolo contendre and was sentenced, the appellate court found the officer had the reasonable suspicion necessary to stop defendant's vehicle when he saw defendant tried to evade the roadblock and had probable cause to arrest defendant when he observed defendant had been drinking. It further found defendant's ex post facto rights were not violated even though his first DUI conviction required more subsequent DUI convictions for a felony as the statute, in effect before defendant's latest crime, merely stiffened the penalty for DUI.


OUTCOME: The judgment was affirmed.

JLG Concrete Prods. Co. v. City of Grenada


NO. 96-CA-00925 COA


COURT OF APPEALS OF MISSISSIPPI


722 So. 2d 1283; 1998 Miss. App. LEXIS 916



October 27, 1998, Decided


PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] DATE OF JUDGMENT: 7/18/96. TRIAL JUDGE: HON. MELVIN MCCLURE. COURT FROM WHICH APPEALED: GRENADA COUNTY CHANCERY COURT. TRIAL COURT DISPOSITION: VOIDED LEASE, DENIED DAMAGES.



DISPOSITION: AFFIRMED.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: A lessee appealed from a decision of the Grenada County Chancery Court (Mississippi) that declared that no renewal of a 1957 lease granted by the appellee city had ever been properly authorized and that the lessee had no rights to the land. City cross-appealed asserting that the chancellor improperly denied its timely amendment to add a claim for damages to the property.


OVERVIEW: The lower court properly found there was no new lease on city property where no new lease could be found, there was no proof in city council minutes of a renewal being discussed or approved; and the alleged proof of notice and acceptance of the lease could not be produced. There was no evidence that any city official had the authority to negotiate, agree to terms, and enter an agreement to lease real property. There was no hint that the lessee made efforts to assure the validity of a renewal. There was no manifest error in the chancellor's failing to find estoppel arising from 14-year-old improvements. As the city manager had no authority to renew the lease, and with no agreement as to new terms, there was no possible justifiable reliance on such action. No equitable grounds existed to override the absence of city council approval of a lease renewal. The lessee was a tenant at will, obligated to pay rent payments until ousted. There was no abuse of discretion in denying leave to amend two-and-a-half years after a declaratory judgment action was filed by the city where the alleged late discovery of the facts underlying the amendment was implausible.


OUTCOME: The lower court decision was affirmed.

CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: The action was filed in the Chancery Court of Lee County, Mississippi by appellee tax collector, against appellants, two companies seeking to collect ad valorem taxes on tractor truck units owned by one company and leased to the other. DISPOSITION: AFFIRMED.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant pled guilty to, and was convicted of, one count of sexual battery in violation of Miss. Code Ann. § 97-3-95 (1972). Defendant was sentenced to a term of 25 years. Defendant appealed the decision of the Pearl River County Circuit Court (Mississippi), which denied his motion for post conviction relief.


OVERVIEW: On appeal, defendant argued that (1) his indictment was improperly amended, (2) the amended indictment placed him in double jeopardy, (3) the trial court lacked jurisdiction, (4) he was denied his Sixth Amendment right to effective assistance of counsel, (5) unobjected to issues were not procedurally barred, (6) his plea bargain was a contract and he suffered prejudice in sentencing, and (7) the sexual battery statute was ambiguous. Defendant failed to cite authority for several of his arguments, which precluded appellate review.


OUTCOME: The judgment of the trial court was affirmed.

TRIAL COURT DISPOSITION: WREN - CONVICTION FOR RAPE OF A CHILD UNDER FOURTEEN YEARS, LIFE SENTENCE; LESTER - CONVICTION FOR RAPE OF A CHILD UNDER FOURTEEN YEARS, EIGHT YEAR SENTENCE; BUTLER - CONVICTION FOR ACCESSORY AFTER THE FACT, FIVE YEAR SENTENCE.



DISPOSITION: AFFIRMED WITH THE EXCEPTION OF LESTER'S EIGHT YEAR TERM, REMANDED FOR RE-SENTENCING.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendants appealed the judgment of the Panola County Circuit Court (Mississippi), which in a joint trial convicted one defendant of rape of a child under 14 years and sentenced him to life in prison, convicted a second defendant of accessory before the fact to capital rape and sentenced him to eight years in prison, and convicted the third defendant as an accessory after the fact and sentenced her to five years in prison.


OVERVIEW: One defendant stood inside the door of the bedroom where defendant female's three daughters were sleeping while the other male defendant raped the 13-year-old. Defendant female observed the attack and retreated. Following the ordeal, she escorted the victim to the bathroom, where she washed the semen from her daughter's body and bedclothes. Her only comment to the children was that they should have locked the door. Defendants jointly and individually urged eight grounds for reversal. The court affirmed the convictions, but reversed the eight-year sentence for accessory before the fact to capital rape and remanded that defendant for resentencing because: (1) he was an aider and abettor and was subject to the same punishment as a principal, Miss. Code Ann. § 97-1-3 (Rev. 1994); (2) Miss. Code Ann. § 97-3-65(1) required the trial court to impose either a life term or the death penalty; and (3) defendant's claim that a life sentence would be cruel and unusual punishment in his case was without merit. The court held that the trial judge did not abuse his discretion in finding that defendants' race-neutral explanations for their peremptory challenges to white jurors were pretexts.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed defendants' convictions and sentences, with the exception of the eight-year sentence. The court vacated the eight-year sentence and remanded for resentencing in accordance with the mandatory sentencing provisions of the state code, which provided that an adult convicted of raping a child younger than 14 had to be sentenced to death or life in prison.

PRIOR HISTORY: COURT FROM WHICH APPEALED: LOWNDES COUNTY CIRCUIT COURT. DATE OF JUDGMENT: 12/07/95. TRIAL JUDGE: HON. LEE J. HOWARD. TRIAL COURT DISPOSITION: AFFIRMED IN PART, REVERSED IN PART.



DISPOSITION: AFFIRMED IN PART; REMANDED IN PART.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant association sought review of a judgment by the Lowndes County Circuit Court (Mississippi), which held that appellee Lowndes County Board of Supervisors properly adopted zoning regulations to limit the development of property around an air force base.


OVERVIEW: The association challenged the zoning regulation on the ground that it amounted to a taking. On appeal, the court affirmed the adoption of the zoning regulations but remanded the takings issue for determination by the trial court. The court rejected the association's argument that the Board was required to give advance public notice that the Zoning Commission would be established at their March 11, 1994. The court also found that although the Commission's action were not in strict adherence to the requirements of the Open Meetings Act, its actions were in substantial compliance and thus not erroneous. The court also rejected the association's argument that Miss. Code Ann. § 61-7-7 (1) required the Board to identify a particular airport hazard or hazards in the minutes of its meetings in order to have authority to adopt the airport zoning ordinance. Yet, the court ruled that by ordering the Board to conduct evidentiary hearings with respect to each parcel of land affected by the ordinance, it was in effect forcing the Board to gather evidence for the association's takings claim, when the Board was under no legal obligation to conduct such fact-finding endeavors.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the part of the judgment directing the Board to refrain from enforcing the airport zoning ordinance. Yet, the court remanded the association's takings issue and affirmed the judgment in all other respects.

DISPOSITION: AFFIRMED.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed from a judgment of the Panola County Circuit Court (Mississippi), which, following a jury trial, convicted him of the crime of manslaughter.


OVERVIEW: Defendant and a friend were both indicted for capital murder in the beating death of the victim. The friend pled to a lesser charge and testified at defendant's trial. On appeal, the court rejected defendant's five assignments of error and affirmed the judgment. The jury's verdict was not against the weight of the evidence, which was sufficient to satisfy all elements of manslaughter. The court determined that the task of interpreting whether defendant's fist could have been considered a dangerous weapon, as that phrase was employed by Miss. Code Ann. § 97-3-35 (Rev. 1994), was a factual determination for the jury. The court held that the trial court did not err in its instructions to the jury or in its evidentiary rulings. The court also ruled that defendant failed to establish that venue was improper. The sheriff's testimony regarding the location of the body raised a presumption as to the location of the killing and was sufficient to undergird the trial court's finding that venue was proper.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment of the trial court, which convicted defendant of manslaughter.
