PRIOR HISTORY: Appeal from the District Court of the City and County of Denver, Honorable Joseph N. Lilly, Judge.



DISPOSITION: Affirmed in Part, Reversed in Part.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiff appealed an order from the District Court of the City and County of Denver (Colorado), which before trial, ruled that the plaintiff was limited to a maximum recovery of $ 10,000 damages for personal injuries under Colo. Rev. Stat. § 13-10-2 (repealed 1971), even though the jury found in favor of the plaintiff against defendant city in excess of that limitation.


OVERVIEW: Plaintiff filed an action against the city and one of its police officers, alleging that she was injured in a car accident caused by the officer's negligent operation of a patrol car while he was responding to an emergency. Before trial, the trial court had ruled that the plaintiff was limited to a maximum recovery of $ 10,000 damages for personal injuries under Colo. Rev. Stat. § 13-10-2 (repealed 1971). Plaintiff made a timely objection and, after a jury returned a verdict in her favor, appealed the trial court's limitation of damages.On appeal,the court affirmed the judgment on the issue of liability, but reversed on the issue of damages, concluding that both the city's charter as well as the state's statute partially discarded the doctrine of governmental immunity. And, although there was a monetary limitation in § 13-10-2, nowhere in the Denver charter was there a monetary limitation. The court concluded that governmental immunity for tortious acts of municipal police officers was a matter of both statewide and local concern and, in the absence of conflict with § 13-10-2, a home-rule city may extend greater benefits to persons injured by the tortious acts of its officers.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment as to liability and reversed as to damages, and the cause was remanded to the district court, with directions to hold a trial on the issue of damages only, concluding that, because there was no conflict between the statute and the city's charter provisions, the latter controlled and the $ 10,000 limit of the statute was not applicable.
