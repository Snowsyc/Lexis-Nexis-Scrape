Clayton v. Cape Canaveral


No. 76-641


Court of Appeal of Florida, Fourth District


354 So. 2d 147; 1978 Fla. App. LEXIS 15093



January 31, 1978 


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellants, club owner and operator, sought rehearing by the court of its affirmance of the judgment of the trial court (Florida), which granted a directed verdict in favor of appellees, city of Cape Canaveral and its chief of police, in an action by appellants for malicious prosecution.


OVERVIEW: Appellants, club owner and operator, filed an action for malicious prosecution and other related causes of action against appellees, the city of Cape Canaveral and its chief of police, resulting from a series of arrests and prosecutions of appellants. At trial, a directed verdict was granted in favor of appellees on all counts. The court affirmed the judgment of the trial court on all counts except for the malicious prosecution action against appellee city of Cape Canaveral. The court concluded that law enforcement is typically the task of the executive, and thus does not fall within the area of municipal tort immunity reserved for judicial, quasi-judicial, legislative, and quasi-legislative functions. Rather, the conduct of which appellants complained fell into the category of governmental functions for which a municipality can be liable under the doctrine of respondeat superior if the tort is committed against one with whom the municipality employee is in privity, or is otherwise in contact in a direct transaction or confrontation. The court reversed the judgment of the trial court as to the count for malicious prosecution and remanded the cause for a new trial.


OUTCOME: Request for rehearing was granted by the court. The judgment of the trial court granting directed verdicts for appellee chief of police was affirmed on all counts. Directed verdicts for the city of Cape Canaveral were affirmed on all counts except for the count of malicious prosecution, which was reversed and remanded for a new trial, as municipal tort immunity did not apply to the executive function of law enforcement.

S. A. Healy Co. v. Highland Beach


No. 76-1631


Court of Appeal of Florida, Fourth District


355 So. 2d 813; 1978 Fla. App. LEXIS 14882



January 24, 1978 


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant corporation challenged the judgment entered in the trial court (Florida), which upheld the single family zoning imposed on appellant's property by appellee town.


OVERVIEW: Appellant corporation filed suit challenging the constitutionality of the single family zoning imposed on appellant's property. The trial court upheld the zoning field, and appellant challenged. The court held that the fact that most of the land located in appellee town was committed to multi-family use did not prevent appellee from adopting and enforcing single family zoning regulations. Although the highest and best use for appellant's property was multi-family, the constitutional validity of the zoning was not based upon the highest and best use. Rather, in order for the zoning to have been held unconstitutional, the ordinance must have deprived appellant of the beneficial use of the property by precluding all potential uses of the property. In addition, the zoning did not affirmatively appear arbitrary and unreasonable and without substantial relation to the public safety, health, morals, or general welfare. Finally, the court held that appellee did not have the burden of demonstrating a real, material, and substantial public need for the single family zoning. Rather, appellant had the burden of proving it was invalid. Accordingly, the court affirmed the judgment.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment upholding the single family zoning imposed on appellant corporation's property because protecting single family developments was a legitimate objective of zoning and zoning was the exclusive function of appellee town, not the courts.
