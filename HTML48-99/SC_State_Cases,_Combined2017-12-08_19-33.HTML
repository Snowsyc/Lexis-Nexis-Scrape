November 3, 1999, Heard  
December 6, 1999, Filed


PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] Appeal From Richland County. James R. Barber, III, Circuit Court Judge.



DISPOSITION: AFFIRMED.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: On appeal from Circuit Court, Richland County (South Carolina), appellant challenged entry of summary judgment in favor of respondent, South Carolina Department of Revenue, on suit to recover damages allegedly resulting from the loss of video poker revenue.


OVERVIEW: Appellant, convenience store owner, commenced this action against respondent, South Carolina Department of Revenue, to recover for business losses allegedly incurred when cash payouts for video poker were banned pursuant to a local option referendum. The complaint alleged a regulatory taking of appellant's contracts regarding the poker machines and an unconstitutional impairment of contract in violation of the Contract Clause, U.S. Const. art. 1 §10. The trial judge found no taking or impairment of contract and ruled in favor of respondent. The judgment was affirmed. The court found no merit in appellant's contention that a taking occurred when the statute providing for the local option referendum was enacted, in light of the fact no property right could have existed at that time since the contracts had not yet been signed. Furthermore, the court found no substantial impairment of a contract where further regulation regarding cash payouts was foreseeable.


OUTCOME: Judgment in favor of department of revenue affirmed; where appellant had entered into contract after enactment of law that allowed for local option referendum, there was no regulatory taking or unconstitutional impairment of contract.

PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Following a declaratory action brought by appellant sheriff against various respondent county officials, appellant sought review of the decision by the Horry County Circuit Court (South Carolina) denying appellant the custody and control of the county jail.


OVERVIEW: Appellant sheriff sought review of a decision by the lower court denying appellant the custody and control of the county jail.
