PRIOR HISTORY: Appeal from the Fourth Judicial District Court, Ouachita Parish; Honorable Lemmie O. Hightower, District Judge, Presiding.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant was convicted in the Fourth Judicial District Court, Ouachita Parish (Louisiana), of two counts of extortion. He appealed his convictions and sentences.


OVERVIEW: Defendant argued that the trial court erred (1) in overruling his motion to quash the indictment based on alleged unconstitutional vagueness and overbreadth of La. Rev. Stat. Ann. § 14:66; (2) in overruling his challenges for cause of two prospective jurors; and (3) in denying a mistrial, when the foreman of the jury made an allegedly inconclusive report as to the number of jurors who concurred in the verdict. One juror had read about the charges in newspapers, and indicated she would require some evidence on behalf of defendant to acquit. The other had a particular dislike of sexual crimes, and the instant charges involved sex.


OUTCOME: The convictions and sentences were affirmed.

PRIOR HISTORY: Appeal from the First Judicial District Court, Caddo Parish; Honorable William J. Fleniken, District Judge, Presiding.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Two defendants were convicted of aggravated escape in the First Judicial District Court, Caddo Parish (Louisiana). In a joint appeal, defendant's alleged numerous points of error, asserting, in-part, that the applicable statute was unconstitutionally vague, that they were not "legally confined" for purposes of escape, and that the trial court improperly refused to issue subpoenas regarding the disciplinary proceedings of two prison guards.


OVERVIEW: One defendant was confined in the Louisiana Caddo Correctional Institute (CCI) as a result of convictions of misdemeanors, while the second defendant was held under an indictment for murder. They escaped from CCI by using makeshift knives to confine two CCI employees. The appellate court rejected defendants' arguments that the statutory phrase concerning circumstances wherein human life was endangered, under La. Rev. Stat. Ann. § 14:110, was unconstitutionally vague. Defendants' chief defense was that, at the time of their escape, they were not "legally confined" at CCI, because, allegedly, they were lawfully in the custody of the sheriff of the parish. Accordingly, defendants were "legally detained" for purposes of escape. Finally, there was no reversible error in the trial court's refusal to order production of the disciplinary proceedings of the two guards overpowered in the escape, because there was no showing that the evidence was material to the defense.


OUTCOME: Defendants' convictions and sentences were affirmed.

CASE SUMMARY:

PRIOR HISTORY: ON WRIT OF CERTIORARI DIRECTED TO: HONORABLE JAMES A. WYSOCKI, JUDGE AD HOC, CIVIL DISTRICT COURT, PARISH OF ORLEANS, DIVISION "G", NOS. 87-3697, 87-3727, 87-3728, 87-3898.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Applicant, the director of finance for the city, brought a writ of certiorari before the Civil District Court, Parish of Orleans, Division G (Louisiana). He argued that the district court should have granted summary judgment and dismissed the claims of respondents, the owners of real property, because they had failed to timely file their appeal from the decision of the tax commission.


OVERVIEW: The owners of real property sought recovery of ad valorem taxes paid under protest from the director of finance for the city. The district court denied the director's motion for summary judgment, he filed a writ of certiorari, and the court granted it. The court noted that the owners complained that their assessments were unequal and not uniform relative to assessments of similar classes of property in adjacent assessment districts. Thus, the court determined that their complaint was a correctness challenge. As such, the owners were obliged to follow the procedures mandated by La. Const. art. VII, § 18 and provided for in La. Rev. Stat. Ann. § 47:1998: suit must be filed within 30 days of the decision of the tax commission. Yet the owners failed to file their petitions timely. Therefore, the actions of the owners had prescribed, and the director's exception of prescription should have been maintained.


OUTCOME: The court set aside the judgment of the district court, entered judgment maintaining the director's exception of prescription, and dismissed the owners' suits.

Casey v. Southern Baptist Hospital


No. CA-8146


Court of Appeal of Louisiana, Fourth Circuit


526 So. 2d 1332; 1988 La. App. LEXIS 1067



May 12, 1988, Decided


PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] AN APPEAL FROM THE CIVIL DISTRICT COURT, FOR THE PARISH OF ORLEANS, NO. 84-04993, DIVISION "E", HONORABLE GERALD P. FEDOROFF, JUDGE.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiff patient appealed an order of the Civil District Court in and for the Parish of Orleans (Louisiana), which found no constitutional violation in La. Civ. Code Ann. art. 2322.1 and La. Rev. Stat. Ann. § 9:2797 and dismissed the patient's action against the hospital for her alleged contraction of hepatitis from a blood transfusion.


OVERVIEW: The patient had a blood transfusion following a cesarean section delivery of her child. The patient contracted hepatitis. The patient brought an action against the hospital. The patient alleged that La. Civ. Code Ann. art. 2322.1 and La. Rev. Stat. Ann. § 9:2797 were unconstitutional. The district court found no constitutional violation and dismissed the action. On appeal, the court reversed and rendered judgment in favor of the patient, holding that the final versions of the questioned statutes were not germane to the original titles and that the statutes were therefore unconstitutional. The court found that the hospital was liable for the hepatitis contracted by the patient and awarded damages in favor of the patient.


OUTCOME: The court reversed and rendered judgment in favor of the patient.

State v. Oliver


No. KA 7771


Court of Appeal of Louisiana, Fourth Circuit


526 So. 2d 395; 1988 La. App. LEXIS 1068



May 12, 1988, Decided


PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] APPEAL FROM CRIMINAL DISTRICT COURT, PARISH OF ORLEANS, SECTION C, NO. 312-385, HONORABLE JEROME M. WINSBERG, JUDGE.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant was convicted of two counts of armed robbery and sentenced to serve 13 years hard labor on each count without the benefit of parole. The trial judge set aside the sentence and resentenced defendant as a habitual offender. On appeal, the court vacated that sentence and defendant was resentenced by the Criminal District Court, Parish of Orleans, Section C (Louisiana). Defendant appealed.


OVERVIEW: Defendant was charged with armed robbery at an incident that occurred at a bus stop. Defendant was picked out of a photographic line-up. Defendant's first trial for armed robbery ended in a mistrial. Defendant was convicted of two counts of armed robbery on the second trial. Defendant was sentenced to serve 13 years of hard labor on each count without the benefit of parole. The trial judge set aside the original sentence and sentenced defendant as a habitual offender. The trial court held that the minimum statutory sentence was unconstitutionally excessive. The court granted the State's writ application and vacated the sentence. The trial court then resentenced defendant. On review, the court found that the enhanced penalties imposed under La. Rev. Stat. Ann. § 15:529 did not constitute cruel and unusual punishment. The court stated that the trial judge erred in imposing the first sentence because it was lesser than the sentence mandated by Louisiana law. The court determined that the officer's testimony regarding similar robberies did not foster a conclusion that defendant was being sought for any crimes other than those for which he was standing trial.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the convictions.

State v. Briggs


No. CR87-1123


Court of Appeal of Louisiana, Third Circuit


526 So. 2d 297; 1988 La. App. LEXIS 1150



May 11, 1988, Decided


PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] On appeal from the Fifteenth Judicial District Court for the Parish of Vermilion, State of Louisiana, the Honorable Fred A. Blanche, Judge, presiding.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed from a judgment of the Fifteenth Judicial District Court for the Parish of Vermilion, State of Louisiana, which convicted defendant of forcible rape, a violation of La. Rev. Stat. Ann. § 14:42.1, and sentenced defendant as an habitual offender to 60 years at hard labor.


OVERVIEW: Defendant argued that he was entitled to a mistrial because the court clerk at the arraignment mentioned that defendant was present with court-appointed counsel. On appeal, the court held that the trial court's denial of a mistrial was not erroneous because the clerk's statement did not amount to a prosecutorial attempt to argue guilt from poverty. The court determined that the victim's testimony was sufficient to prove beyond a reasonable doubt that a rape occurred and that defendant was the rapist. Although defendant was not informed when he pleaded guilty to forgery that the guilty plea could be used as the basis for the filing of a future multiple offender bill, the court ruled that the prior forgery conviction provided a sufficient basis for the multiple offender bill. The court concluded that La. Rev. Stat. Ann. § 15:529.1(A)(1) of Louisiana's Habitual Offender Law was not unconstitutionally vague because the only interpretation that a person of ordinary intelligence could make of § 15:529.1(A)(1) was that a second felony offender's sentence would be not less than one-third the longest term and not more than twice the longest term prescribed for a first conviction.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed defendant's conviction and sentence.
