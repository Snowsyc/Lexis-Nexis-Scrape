PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant, which had obtained a writ of foreign attachment against appellee's property, filed an appeal from the order of the Court of Common Pleas of Allegheny County, Pennsylvania, ordering appellee's bond discharged and cancelled on the basis of a prior decision, which declared the Pennsylvania foreign attachment procedures unconstitutional.


OVERVIEW: Appellant obtained a writ of foreign attachment, which directed the seizure of appellee's property. The attachment was released upon the posting of a bond by appellee. OUTCOME: Court held it did not have jurisdiction over appeal and transferred the matter to superior court. Court had exclusive jurisdiction of appeals from final orders of trial courts where such courts held a Pennsylvania statute unconstitutional and, in this case, foreign attachment provisions had been ruled unconstitutional some years earlier.
