245 S.W.2d 749; 1952 Tex. App. LEXIS 2226

CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant buyers sought review of the judgment of the trial court (Texas), which granted appellee supplier's petition to fix a materialman's lien and to foreclose on the buyers' property.


OVERVIEW: The owners contracted with the supplier for material's necessary to finish building a house. The supplier was delivering materials at the property in question over two weeks prior to the owners moving onto the property. The owners sold the property to the buyers without paying for the materials. Subsequent to the sale the supplier filed for a materialman's lien. The court upheld the grant of the lien and the foreclosure. OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment of the trial court.

CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant election contestees challenged a judgment of a trial court (Texas), which held that an election for a tax increase carried and that an election for the issuance of bonds for an independent school district was defeated. Respondents were a county attorney and others.


OVERVIEW: The contestees argued that H.B. 344, ch. 333, 49th Leg. (Tex. 1945), which amended Tex. Rev. Civ. Stat. art. 2968, was unconstitutional. Article 2968 required that voters, who were exempt from the payment of a poll tax and lived in cities with a population of 10,000 or more, to obtain a certificate of exemption from the tax collector every year. The trial court held that 45 votes were illegal because the voters had failed to obtain exemption certificates for the current year. On appeal, the court held that H.B. 344, ch. 333, 49th Leg. (Tex. 1945) was constitutional. The caption of H.B. 344 was not misleading when read in connection with Tex. Rev. Civ. Stat. art. 2968. The only change made in Article 2968 was the requirement that a voter should secure a certificate of exemption annually in lieu of a permanent certificate. Article 2968 and H.B. 344, ch. 333, 49th Leg. (Tex. 1945) were not unconstitutional as an excess of the legislature's power to limit the right of suffrage. Tex. Const. art. 6, §4 authorized the legislature to enact laws to preserve the purity of the ballot box and to require registration of voters in cities of 10,000 inhabitants or more.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment.
