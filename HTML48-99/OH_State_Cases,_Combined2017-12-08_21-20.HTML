SUBSEQUENT HISTORY:  [***1] Appeal dismissed, question moot, 166 Ohio St., 147.


PRIOR HISTORY: IN MANDAMUS: Court of Appeals for Montgomery County.



DISPOSITION: Writ allowed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Relator, the People of the State of Ohio on behalf of a voter, filed a petition with the court praying for a writ of mandamus to issue to respondents, the members of a county board of elections, ordering them to take such steps as were necessary to enable them to provide an adequate number of voting compartments for the voters in the county. The board demurred to the petition, claiming that it did not state a valid cause of action.


OVERVIEW: The court reviewed applicable statutory law pertaining to elections in order to render its judgment. It addressed the provisions of Ohio Rev. Code Ann. §§ 3501.29, 3507.06, and 3507.14 and found that there was no repugnancy or conflict between any of the election-related provisions. The court found that § 3506.14 did not place discretion in the board to determine the number of voting machines to be used in each precinct, independently of the duties enjoined by §§ 3501.29 and 3507.06. The board was required to determine the number of machines to be used in each precinct by applying the standard set forth in § 3501.29, which provided for one voting machine for every 100 qualified electors and, on the basis of that ration, to determine the number of machines necessary to accommodate the number of qualified electors in each precinct. The court further found that the legislature intended to apply the same standard respecting voting machines as it prescribed for paper ballots. The court held that the board could use its machines on hand, along with the paper ballots, or provide the use of both in any of the precincts in order to provide adequate means for voting in county elections.


OUTCOME: The court permitted the writ.

OVERVIEW: The 101st General Assembly enacted Amended House Bill No. 164, which provided for the election of a board of directors for the Union County agricultural society. OUTCOME: The court affirmed the trial court's judgment.

DISPOSITION: Judgment affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant, a decedent's natural son, sought review of the judgment of the Probate Court of Cuyahoga County (Ohio), which held that the son was not an heir of the decedent because he had been adopted by another and could not inherit from the decedent. Appellee estate administrator was also a party to the action to determine the decedent's heirs.


OVERVIEW: The administrator commenced an action to determine the heirs of the decedent, who died intestate. The son claimed to be the sole heir at law, notwithstanding the fact that he had been legally adopted by his adoptive father during his childhood. The probate court held that the son was not an heir. On appeal, the court affirmed. The court held that Ohio Gen. Code § 8030, which provided that an adopted child could inherit from its natural parents, did not apply even though it was in effect at the time of the adoption. Instead, the court held that Ohio Rev. Code Ann. § 3107.13, under which the son could not inherit from the decedent because he was adopted, applied because it was in effect at the time of the decedent's death. The court rejected the son's contention that § 3107.13 did not apply to children who had been adopted prior to the effective date of § 3107.13. Section 3107.13 was meant to apply to all adopted children, regardless of the time of their adoption. Applying § 3107.13 was not an improper retrospective application because it did not change or affect existing rights of the son as to inheritance where there were no rights of inheritance until the death of the decedent.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the probate court's judgment.
