August 29, 1990, Heard in the Court of Appeals  
October 2, 1990, Filed


PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] Appeal by defendant from judgment entered 16 October 1989 by Judge Thomas S. Watts in Beaufort County Superior Court.



DISPOSITION: Reversed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant railroad appealed an order of the Beaufort County Superior Court (North Carolina) entering judgment for plaintiff landowner and requiring the railroad to build and maintain a new railroad crossing on the landowner's property at the spot requested by the landowner. The landowner sought to develop property on the western part of his farmland and requested the new crossing to obtain better access to the property.


OVERVIEW: The railroad argued that the evidence did not support the factual findings and that the landowner was not entitled to the new crossing under any state statute, including the cattle guard statute, N.C. Gen. Stat. § 62-226 (1989) or the cartway statute, N.C. Gen. Stat. § 136-69 (1986). The court found that the findings of fact did support the trial court's findings. However, it reversed because there was no legal authority that a landowner, holding land subject to an easement by the railroad, could direct a railroad as to the location of a new railroad crossing. There was no statutory or common-law authority to support that proposition. The railroad did have a duty to keep its crossings safe and if the existing crossing was inadequate for the safe passage of two way traffic, the landowner could file an action directing the railroad to upgrade the existing crossing. The cattle guard statute only applied to completely enclosed land and the cartway statute only applied when a petitioner was seeking access to a public road over the lands of another person.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the judgment.
