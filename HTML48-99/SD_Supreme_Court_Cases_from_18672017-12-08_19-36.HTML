CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellants took an appeal from the order of the Circuit Court of the Third Judicial Circuit, Codington County (South Dakota) granting summary judgment to defendants on grounds that they owed appellants no duty of care for act of child under their care.


OVERVIEW: Appellants, parents of child sexually abused by another child living with foster family under supervision of state social services department, sued appellees, the foster parents and two department employees, for negligent supervision. PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant county appealed the judgment of the Circuit Court of the Eighth Judicial Circuit, Butte County, South Dakota, reversing appellant's property tax assessment of appellee's land as unconstitutional under the South Dakota Constitution, and ordering appellant to assess such lands in a manner similar to other agricultural lands.


OVERVIEW: Appellee landowner brought an action against appellant county challenging its assessment of appellee's property, which was based on a method that classified certain real property as irrigable and valued it as such. CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendants, rodeo owner and sponsor, among others, appealed the judgment from the Circuit Court of the Fourth Judicial Circuit, Davidson County (South Dakota), after plaintiff brought a negligence suit for injuries he sustained while working at defendants' rodeo. The trial court denied defendants' motion for summary judgment and declared that S.D. Codified Laws § 42-11-1(6)(e) violated the "open courts" provision of S.D. Const. art. VI, § 20.


OVERVIEW: Plaintiff was injured while working at a rodeo when a gate struck him in the face. Plaintiff filed a negligence suit against defendants, rodeo owner and sponsor, among others. Plaintiff also sought a declaratory judgment that S.D. Codified Laws ch. 42-11 was unconstitutional. The trial court denied motions for summary judgment filed by all the parties. The trial court also declared that S.D. Codified Laws § 42-11-1(6)(e) violated S.D. Const. art. VI, § 20, the "open courts" provision. The court reversed. The trial court erroneously determined that S.D. Codified Laws § 42-11-1(6)(e) provided defendants with complete immunity. OUTCOME: The court reversed the judgment that declared that a certain state statute violated the open courts provision of the state constitution. PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant sought review of a judgment from the Circuit Court of the Sixth Judicial Circuit; Hughes County (South Dakota), which convicted him of escape and aggravated assault and sentenced him as an habitual offender.


OVERVIEW: Defendant was convicted of escape and aggravated assault. He was also charged with having been convicted of three prior felonies. Defendant pled guilty to the prior felony part of the information and admitted to being an habitual offender. Defendant's convictions for escape and aggravated assault were reversed. PROCEDURAL POSTURE: The State appealed the judgment of the Circuit Court of the Seventh Judicial Circuit, Pennington County (South Dakota), which dismissed a felony drug indictment against defendant based on constitutional infirmity of the statute. Defendant had been charged with keeping a place for use or sale of controlled substances in violation of S.D. Codified Laws § 22-42-10.


OVERVIEW: An undercover police agent allegedly purchased illegal drugs from the co-tenant at defendant's apartment on three occasions, and the State claimed that defendant was present in the apartment on two of these three occasions. OUTCOME: The court reversed the dismissal of the indictment and remanded the case for proceedings consistent with its opinion.
