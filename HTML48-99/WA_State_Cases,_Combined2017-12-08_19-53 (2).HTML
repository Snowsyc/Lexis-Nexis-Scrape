CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiff 20-acre landowners sought a writ of mandamus to compel the prosecuting attorney of King County to institute a quo warranto proceeding seeking the dissolution of the town of Black Diamond as a municipal corporation. The Superior Court for King County (Washington) refused to issue the writ, and the owners appealed.


OVERVIEW: An order of a board of county commissioners declaring a town legally incorporated became final when it was not appealed pursuant to Wash. Rev. Code § 36.32.330, and, implied by the board's finding that the incorporation statutes had been complied with, was a finding that not more than 20 acres of unplatted land belonging to any one person was taken within the corporate limits, Wash. Rev. Code § 35.21.010, notwithstanding that certain persons owned more than 20 acres of unplatted land within the corporate limits and did not expressly consent to such inclusion, where such persons had full notice of the proceedings, made no attempt to enjoin the proceedings, and did not make any objections thereto for nearly three years. In view of the actual notice to the owners and their failure to object or pursue any remedy available to them, the findings of the board that the incorporation statutes had been followed and fully complied with was an implied finding that there were no non-consenting 20-acre tract owners within the boundaries of incorporation. As the board had jurisdiction to make such a finding and order, the order incorporating the city became final when it was not appealed.


OUTCOME: The judgment was affirmed.
