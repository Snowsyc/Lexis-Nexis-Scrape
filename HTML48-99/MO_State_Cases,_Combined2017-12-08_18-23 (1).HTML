OVERVIEW: Defendant allegedly had been drinking at the time he shot his acquaintance. He filed a notice of his intention to rely on the defense of mental disease or defect to exclude responsibility. A psychologist testified that defendant suffered from psychotic episodes and "blackouts due to alcoholism." Instruction 310.50, based upon Mo. Rev. DISPOSITION: Appellant's convictions as to Counts II, III, V and VI are affirmed. His conviction on Count IV for sodomy, § 566.060.3, is reversed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant sought review of a judgment from the Circuit Court of Jackson County (Missouri), which convicted defendant of five counts of sodomy, Mo. Rev. Stat. § 566.060.3.


OVERVIEW: Defendant was charged with five counts of sodomy, Mo. Rev. Stat. § 566.060.3. The trial court convicted defendant of five counts of sodomy. Defendant contended that the trial court erred when it admitted the victim's testimony because she was in the courtroom in violation of the witness exclusionary rule. The court held that: (1) where the testimony of the offending witness was offered for the limited purpose of impeachment and not as part of the offering party's case-in-chief, courts were more receptive to the evidence; (2) when such witnesses testified in rebuttal and not on a matter for the prosecution's case-in-chief, there was no abuse of discretion when a court decided not to exclude the testimony; (3) the victim's testimony related to nonessential issues that did not directly relate to defendant's guilt or innocence of the crimes charged or the elements of the offenses; thus, there was no abuse of discretion when the trial court let the victim testify on rebuttal; and (4) the prosecution presented no direct evidence that that defendant placed his penis in the victim's hand to commit sodomy; thus, there was insufficient evidence to submit count IV to the jury.

PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant Missouri State Highway Patrol (MSHP) sought review of the order of the Circuit Court of Cole County (Missouri), which expunged the arrest record of respondent, an individual seeking employment as a police officer.


OVERVIEW: The individual was arrested for the misdemeanor of stealing under $ 150. The arrest arose out of a markdown error and, as a result, charges were never brought. The records relating to the arrest were closed pursuant to Mo. Rev. Stat. § 610.100.
