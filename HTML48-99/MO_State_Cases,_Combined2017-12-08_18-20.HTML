Goings v. Missouri Dep't of Corrections


No. SC81589


SUPREME COURT OF MISSOURI


6 S.W.3d 906; 1999 Mo. LEXIS 79



December 21, 1999, Filed


PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] APPEAL FROM THE CIRCUIT COURT OF COLE COUNTY. The Honorable Byron L. Kinder, Judge.



DISPOSITION: Reversed and remanded.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed the judgment of the Circuit Court of Cole County (Missouri), which denied his declaratory judgment action challenging the department of corrections' refusal to credit him for time served, pursuant to Mo. Rev. Stat. § 558.031, for time spent in custody prior to his conviction.


OVERVIEW: Defendant violated his parole by stealing and was returned to custody. Thereafter he was sentenced for the theft and sentenced to a term concurrent with his earlier sentence. The department of corrections refused to credit him for time served, pursuant to Mo. Rev. Stat. § 558.031, from time of the parole violation to the time of his conviction. Defendant's action challenging the refusal of credit was denied. On appeal, the court reversed, holding that defendant was entitled to credit because the statute plainly allowed credit for time spent in custody after an offense and before commencement of the sentence, when time in custody was related to the offense. Because his custody and his subsequent conviction were related to the theft, the statute applied. Although he was also in custody for parole violation, the statutory language allowed custody to be "related to" several offenses, and the credit would nevertheless apply.


OUTCOME: Judgment reversed, because statute plainly allowed credit for time spent in custody after an offense and before commencement of sentence when time in custody was related to the offense. Defendant's custody and his subsequent conviction were related to the same theft.

DISPOSITION: The judgment is reversed, and the case is remanded for further proceedings consistent with this opinion.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Respondent state tax commission appealed a writ of mandamus issued by the Circuit Court of Cole County (Missouri) compelling it to file proposed rule revisions to property valuation formula for agricultural and horticultural land.


OVERVIEW: Relaters, coalition of agricultural associations and interest groups, sought a writ of mandamus compelling respondent state tax commission to file its revisions to state regulations governing property valuation formulas for agricultural and horticultural land. State trial court granted the writ, and respondent appealed. State supreme court reversed and remanded, noting that mandamus was inappropriate here, since a court may only compel a government agency to do that which it is obliged to do, not that which it has the discretion to do or refrain from doing. Since state law expressly granted respondent the discretion to withdrawal proposed rule changes as well as to file them, respondent could not be compelled via mandamus to file them. Accordingly, order granting writ reversed, and case remanded for further proceedings.


OUTCOME: Order granting writ reversed, and case remanded for further proceedings; respondent had discretion to withdraw or file proposed rule revisions, and mandamus does not lie to compel a discretionary act.
