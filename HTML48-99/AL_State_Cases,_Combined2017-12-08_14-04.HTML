PRIOR HISTORY: Appeal from Autauga Circuit Court. (CV-95-082). Ben A. Fuller, Trial Judge.



DISPOSITION: AFFIRMED.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appeal from judgment of the Autauga Circuit Court (Alabama) granting partial summary judgment for defendant petroleum retailer and denying plaintiffs oil companies' motion for partial summary judgment in an action concerning the requirements of the Alabama Motor Fuel Marketing Act when motor fuel's wholesale price was on the rise.


OVERVIEW: Disagreement between plaintiffs oil companies and defendant petroleum retailer concerned requirements of the Alabama Motor Fuel Marketing Act (AMFMA) when the wholesale price of motor fuel was on the rise. In affirming circuit court's grant of partial summary judgment for defendant and denial of plaintiffs' motion for partial summary judgment, Supreme Court of Alabama held: violation of the AMFMA did not occur where defendant retailer sold motor fuel at a retail price initially above its statutory cost and equal to one or more of its competitors, but then due to an increase in the wholesale cost of fuel, defendant retailer and one or more of its competitors while matching one another's retail price, were placed in a below-cost selling situation; under the circumstances, a retailer such as defendant could assert the "meeting-competition" defense under Ala. Code § 8-22-8(b) (1975 & Repl. Vol. 1993); and plaintiffs must rely on "self help."


OUTCOME: Judgment was affirmed where violation of the AMFMA did not occur when due to increase in fuel's wholesale cost, defendant retailer and one or more of its competitors while matching one another's retail price, were placed in below-cost selling situation and defendant could assert "meeting-competition" defense against plaintiffs oil companies.

OVERVIEW: Plaintiff filed suit for a judgment declaring that 1998 Ala. CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendants, governor, finance director, and comptroller, challenged the judgments of the Montgomery Circuit Court (Alabama), which entered judgment in favor of plaintiffs, state officials and legislators, in the consolidated actions of the state officials and legislators seeking a judgment declaring that the governor's attempted disapproval of portions of the 1991 educational appropriations bill was unconstitutional.


OVERVIEW: The governor argued, inter alia, that a proper analysis of Ala. Const. § 125 (1901) led to the conclusion that he had the authority to exercise a post-adjournment item veto and that when Ala. Const. § 126 (1901), was read in pari materia with Ala. Const. § 125 (1901), the sections provided that he was entitled to approve or disapprove items within appropriations bills at any time during the 10 days following the legislature's sine die adjournment. The court affirmed the trial court's judgments. The court concluded that the only power that § 125 provided the governor after the legislature's final adjournment was to approve bills presented to him within five days of the legislature's final adjournment. The court held that Ala. Const. § 126 (1901) did not authorize the governor to item-veto an appropriations bill after the legislature had adjourned sine die. The court reasoned that, unless the legislature was in session, the legislature could not, pursuant to § 126, (1) receive a message from the governor, (2) have a bill returned to it, (3) consider the governor's objection, or (4) repass the bill over the governor's veto.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the trial court's judgments, which entered judgment for the state officials and legislators in their actions seeking a judgment declaring that the governor's attempted disapproval of portions of an educational appropriations bill was unconstitutional.

PRIOR HISTORY: Certified Question from the United States District Court for the Southern District of Alabama; Charles R. Butler, Jr.



DISPOSITION: CERTIFIED QUESTION ANSWERED.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: The court heard this case to answer a certified question from the United States District Court for the Southern District of Alabama. Appellant employee was injured while cleaning equipment with high pressure hoses for his employer who had been engaged by appellee corporation. The court was asked whether Ala. Code § 6-11-3 violated Ala. Const. art. I, § 11, art. I, § 13, art. I, § 6, or art. III, § 42.


OVERVIEW: Appellant employee was injured while cleaning equipment with high pressure hoses for his employer who had been engaged by appellee corporation. Appellant won a judgment that included $ 289,000 for future lost wages. Pursuant to the requirements of Ala. Code §§ 6-11-1 through 6-11-7, appellee filed a post-trial motion to have the award reduced to present value so as to account for potential investment incomes. The district court that heard the case certified a question as to the constitutionality of the statute to the court. The district court asked the court to consider whether Ala. Code § 6-11-3 violated Ala. Const. art. I, § 11, art. I, § 13, art. I, § 6, or art. III, § 42. The court answered the question in the affirmative and held that it violated appellant's right to a jury trial, which was guaranteed by Ala. Const. art. I, §§ 11 and 13. The court found that only the jury had the right to reduce damages to present value and held that by delegating this role to the trial judge, the statute violated appellant's right to a jury trial.


OUTCOME: The court answered the question certified by the district court in the affirmative. The court found that appellant employee's constitutional right to a jury trial was violated by an Alabama statute, which permitted the trial court to present value an award for lost future income that was entered against appellee corporation. The court held that only the jury had the right to reduce the damage award.

PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] 
Appeal from Mobile Circuit Court, No. CV-89-1030; Braxton L. Kittrell, Judge.



DISPOSITION: REVERSED AND REMANDED WITH DIRECTIONS.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant patient sought review of a decision of the Mobile Circuit Court (Alabama), which reduced, pursuant to Ala. Code § 6-5-544(b), the amount of damages awarded to the patient by a jury in the patient's medical malpractice action against appellee medical care provider (provider).


OVERVIEW: The patient brought a medical malpractice action as the result of complications resulting from an improper injection of a sedative. Because of a statutory cap enacted as part of the Alabama Medical Liability Act of 1987, 1987 Ala. Acts 261, specifically § 6-5-544(b), the trial court reduced the jury verdict for non-economic damages. The court held that the cap was an impermissible burden to the state constitutional right to a jury trial, under Ala. Const. of 1901 art. I, § 11, because the sole reason to set aside a verdict was a determination that the verdict was flawed by bias, prejudice, corruption, or other improper motive. Further, because the cap created a favored class of tortfeasor and balanced the direct burden placed upon catastrophically injured victims of medical malpractice against indirect and speculative benefits of reducing health care costs, it violated the state constitutional right to equal protection, guaranteed by Ala. Const. art. I, §§ 1, 6, and 22. Therefore, unless the jury award was flawed pursuant to the requirements of remittitur or new trial, the trial court was required to reinstate the damage award.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the trial court's reduction of damages and remanded with directions to reinstate the damage award.

DISPOSITION: AFFIRMED IN PART; REVERSED IN PART; AND REMANDED.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Petitioner state department of revenue sought a writ of certiorari to challenge a judgment of the Court of Civil Appeals (Alabama) that refused to determine whether Ala. Code § 6-5-605 was constitutional on the ground that the issue was not raised before the trial court in respondent utilities' action challenging the department's tax assessments under an exemption provided by § 6-5-605.


OVERVIEW: In six cases, the utilities challenged their tax assessments, contending that the department acknowledged that § 6-5-605 was unconstitutional, that the assessments should have been computed on another basis, and that no interest was due because no tax was due as long as § 6-5-6-5 had not been repealed or declared unconstitutional. In its petition for certiorari, the department contended that the section violated the requirement of Ala. The court reversed in part, affirmed in part, and remanded the action, holding that the utilities raised the issue and that the trial court declined to address it. Accordingly, the question was properly before the court. The court held that Ala. Code § 6-5-605 was unconstitutional because it violated the one-subject requirement. The statute's title revealed on the surface what was within the body, but did not reveal what, in substance, the statute contained. The court otherwise affirmed the judgment.


OUTCOME: The court held that Ala. Code § 6-5-605 was unconstitutional, reversed the intermediate court judgment as to that issue, and remanded the action. As to all other respects, the judgment was affirmed.

PRIOR HISTORY: Appeal from Jefferson Circuit Court, No. CV-84-1554; Donald Stewart, Judge.



DISPOSITION: REVERSED AND REMANDED.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiff law enforcement officers filed a 42 U.S.C.S. § 1983 action against defendant town council members, alleging that the members had reduced their salaries and then had terminated them, depriving them of property without due process of law. The officers alleged that the members violated their right to freedom of speech. The Jefferson Circuit Court (Alabama) entered a judgment on a jury verdict in favor of the officers. The members appealed.


OVERVIEW: The court held that the town did not deprive the officers of property with due process in relation to their termination and that the trial court erred in submitting that claim to the jury. The court noted that instead of pursuing the town's post-termination procedures, which could have sufficiently provided due process, the officers elected to amend their complaint. The court did not address whether the trial court erred in submitting to the jury the officers' claims of a deprivation of property without due process based on the pay cuts. The court held that the officers' speech in turning in councilmen for improper tire purchases was a matter of public concern. Moreover, the officers' and the public's interest in the free speech involved outweighed any purported state interest in promoting the efficiency of public services. The trial court did not err in submitting the free speech claim to the jury because the officers presented substantial evidence that the speech was a substantial or motivating factor in the challenged employment decisions. The court reversed the judgment and remanded for a new trial as to all claims because the jury returned a general verdict.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the judgment in favor of the officers and remanded the action for a new trial.

Hereford v. Jefferson County


No. 1900708


Supreme Court of Alabama


586 So. 2d 209; 1991 Ala. LEXIS 907



August 23, 1991 


SUBSEQUENT HISTORY:  [**1] Released for Publication September 19, 1991.


PRIOR HISTORY: Appeal from Jefferson Circuit Court, No. CV-86-5789; Arthur J. Hanes, Jr., Judge.



DISPOSITION: AFFIRMED. 


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiff robbery victims sought review of an order of the Jefferson Circuit Court (Alabama), which granted summary judgment in favor of defendants, a county, a sheriff, and several deputies, in the victims' negligence action.


OVERVIEW: A prisoner in the county jail serving a sentence of life without parole, was mistakenly released instead of another prisoner. After being released, the prisoner robbed the victims. The court ruled that summary judgment was proper because, for purposes of the county's vicarious liability, the sheriff was an executive officer and employee of the state, rather than the county, based on Ala. Const. art. V, § 112 (1901). As an executive officer, the sheriff had sovereign immunity under Ala. Const. art. I, § 14 because none of the exceptions to such immunity applied in the instant case. Likewise, the deputies had sovereign immunity because the acts of deputies were the acts of the sheriff. Under Alabama law, a deputy was legally an extension of the sheriff. A deputy sheriff was the alter ego of the sheriff. Thus, the court concluded that if the deputies acts were generally considered the acts of the sheriff, it was logical that those acts also enjoyed the immunity covering the sheriff's own acts.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the decision granting summary judgment in favor of defendants in the victims' negligence action.

Moore v. Reeves

589 So. 2d 173; 1991 Ala. LEXIS 850

PRIOR HISTORY: Appeal from Madison Circuit Court, No. CV-90-412; William D. Page, Judge.



DISPOSITION: REVERSED AND REMANDED.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiff campus security guard appealed the order from the Madison Circuit Court (Alabama), which granted summary judgment in favor of defendants, college president, college vice president, safety director, and supervisor, in the campus security guard's workmen's compensation action pursuant to Ala. Code § 25-5-11(c)(2).


OVERVIEW: The campus security guard's supervisor told him to use an old station wagon to patrol the campus because the regular patrol car was being repaired. The station wagon had no safety belts, and the driver door would open unless the driver held it shut with his arm through the window. The guard drove the station wagon, rounded a corner, and fell out, injuring his back. He filed a workmen's compensation action under Ala. Code § 25-5-11(c)(2), and defendants successfully sought summary judgment. On appeal, the court held that the terms "safety device" and "safety guard" in § 25-5-11(c)(2) meant a contrivance intended to protect against injury, and that the door and door closure mechanism of the station wagon constituted such a safety device or safety guard because it served to protect the security guard from injury. The court found that defendants' failure to maintain and/or repair the safety guard or device was actionable because it was tantamount to the "removal of" or "failure to install" the safety guard or device. The court noted that to hold otherwise would allow supervisors to neglect the maintenance and repair of safety equipment provided to protect co-employees from injury.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the order of summary judgment in favor of defendants and remanded the security guard's workmen's compensation case to the trial court.

OVERVIEW: The school employee accused the school officials of improperly withholding students' federal student aid money and notified federal authorities of his accusations.
