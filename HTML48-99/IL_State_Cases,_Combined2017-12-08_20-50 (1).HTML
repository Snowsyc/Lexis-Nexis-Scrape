Appellate Court of Illinois, Third District, Second Division


92 Ill. App. 2d 475; 234 N.E.2d 815; 1968 Ill. App. LEXIS 963



March 6, 1968 


PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] Appeal from the Circuit Court of Iroquois County, Twelfth Judicial Circuit; the Hon. DAVID E. ORAM, Judge, presiding.



DISPOSITION: Reversed and remanded with directions.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiff administratrix appealed the order of the Circuit Court of Iroquois County, Twelfth Judicial Circuit (Illinois), which granted the motion to dismiss filed by defendant tortfeasor on the ground that the mandate of the appellate court's denial of defendant's leave to appeal was not filed in the trial court within one year of when the mandate was issued.


OVERVIEW: In plaintiff's negligence action following a fatal car accident, the jury found for defendant. The trial court allowed plaintiff's motion for a new trial. Thereafter, defendant filed a petition for leave to appeal to the appellate court which denied the petition. The appellate court's mandate denying the petition for leave to appeal was filed in the trial court one year and 35 days following the entry of the appellate court's decision. When the case was redocketed, defendant filed a motion to dismiss. The trial court granted the motion. The court reversed the trial court. It held that the question specifically posed was whether a petition for leave to appeal from an order granting a new trial deprived the trial court of jurisdiction of the case when the petition was filed or whether the trial court lost jurisdiction of the case when the leave to appeal was granted. The court held that the denial of the petition for leave to appeal the order granting the new trial by the appellate court did not at any time deprive the trial court of jurisdiction. The trial court was vested with the right and obligation to proceed to a trial in this cause without the filing of a mandate.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the order of the trial court that dismissing the cause and remanded the cause for a new trial.

Fiore v. Highland Park


Gen. No. 67-130


Appellate Court of Illinois, Second District, Second Division


93 Ill. App. 2d 24; 235 N.E.2d 23; 1968 Ill. App. LEXIS 966



March 6, 1968 


SUBSEQUENT HISTORY:  [***1] Rehearing Denied April 4, 1968.


PRIOR HISTORY: Declaratory judgment appeal from the Circuit Court of Lake County, Nineteenth Judicial Circuit; the Hon. CHARLES S. PARKER, Judge, presiding.



DISPOSITION: Decree affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant city sought review of a judgment of the Circuit Court of Lake County, Nineteenth Judicial Circuit (Illinois), which, on remand of a zoning ordinance challenge, invalidated a subsequently enacted city ordinance purporting to rezone appellee property owners' property from an office and research classification to a single-family classification rather than the multiple-family use for which the owners had successfully litigated.


OVERVIEW: The owners originally brought suit on the ground that the city's zoning of their property for office and research use bore no substantial relationship to the public welfare. The trial court agreed and ordered the city to permit the property to be used for multiple family dwellings. On appeal from that decision, the court held that the trial court did not have the power to rezone the property but only to state that the specific use sought by the owner was reasonable and could be permitted. While a petition for rehearing was pending, the city rezoned the property to a single-family classification. In affirming the trial court's invalidation of that ordinance and decree permitting the owner's contemplated use on remand, the court held that jurisdiction was retained under Ill. Const. art. 6, § 9 until all issues of fact and law were determined. The city's rezoning of the property was not authorized by the separation of governmental powers. The trial court had no choice on remand than to enter a supplemental decree in accordance with the court's mandate. The use specified in the trial court's supplemental decree was not against the manifest weight of the evidence.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the trial court's judgment invalidating the city's zoning ordinance.

People v. Lewis


Gen. No. 50,274


Appellate Court of Illinois, First District, Second Division


92 Ill. App. 2d 463; 236 N.E.2d 417; 1968 Ill. App. LEXIS 962



March 5, 1968 


PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] Appeal from the Circuit Court of Cook County, Criminal Division; the Hon. EDWARD F. HEALY, Judge, presiding.



DISPOSITION: Judgment affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed from a judgment of the Circuit Court of Cook County, Criminal Division (Illinois), which convicted and sentenced him of burglary.


OVERVIEW: Defendant was seen carrying a television set in an alley by an officer. Defendant was later arrested and then identified by the officer. Defendant argued that he was subject to an unconstitutional arrest and yet the fruits of that arrest were allowed to be admitted into evidence, the one-man showup before the officer violated his constitutional rights as his lawyer was not present, and the trial court committed reversible error by allowing the prosecution to present evidence of defendant's prior criminal record. The court concluded that: (1) defendant's argument that his arrest was illegal could not be raised for the first time on appeal; (2) reference at the trial to a police photograph was not improper because the picture was not introduced into evidence and the witness did not refer to defendant's criminal record; and (3) the eyewitness testimony of one identification witness was sufficient for the State to sustain its burden of proof in cases involving a weak alibi as a defense coupled with strong identification testimony.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment which convicted and sentenced defendant for burglary.

Weber v. Skokie


Gen. No. 51,727


Appellate Court of Illinois, First District, Second Division


92 Ill. App. 2d 355; 235 N.E.2d 406; 1968 Ill. App. LEXIS 950



February 27, 1968 


SUBSEQUENT HISTORY:  [***1] Rehearing Denied March 26, 1968.


PRIOR HISTORY: Appeal from the Circuit Court of Cook County; the Hon. THOMAS C. DONOVAN, Judge, presiding.



DISPOSITION: Decree reversed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiff property owners brought an action against defendant village to contest the validity of a village zoning ordinance. The Circuit Court of Cook County (Illinois) held that the standards of the ordinance were unconstitutional as applied to the property owners, amounting to a taking of their property without due process of law, and enjoined the village from enforcing the ordinance against the property owners. The village appealed.


OVERVIEW: In their action, the property owners sought an adjudication of their right to construct a single-family dwelling upon an individual parcel of property held by them in joint tenancy, which parcel, subsequent to its acceptance as part of an overall plat of subdivision in 1924, was rendered inadequate by the 1946 ordinance as to both the width and total area minimum standards made requisite for the construction of a single-family dwelling. On appeal, the issue was whether a subsequently enacted zoning ordinance, which purported to restrict the construction of residential structures to property sites meeting certain minimum space requirements, could have overridden or superseded the width and area specifications of a previously accepted and recorded plat of subdivision, to the detriment of one who claimed by it. Upon review, the court held that the zoning ordinance overrode and superseded the width and area specifications of the previously accepted and recorded plat of subdivision. The ordinance's substantial and rational relationship to the legitimate objectives of restrictive zoning necessitated, as applied to the property owners' lot, the upholding of the ordinance.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the trial court's holding that the zoning ordinance in question was unconstitutional, as well as the injunction enjoining the village from enforcing the ordinance against the property owners.

La Salle Nat'l Bank v. Palatine


Gen. No. 52,169


Appellate Court of Illinois, First District, Third Division


92 Ill. App. 2d 327; 236 N.E.2d 1; 1968 Ill. App. LEXIS 947



February 23, 1968 


PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] Appeal from the Circuit Court of Cook County, County Department, Law Division; the Hon. CHARLES S. DOUGHERTY, Judge, presiding.



DISPOSITION: Reversed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: In a zoning case involving the validity of defendant Village of Palatine's zoning ordinance as it affected certain property held in trust by plaintiff trustee, the Circuit Court of Cook County (Illinois) declared the ordinance unconstitutional and void in its application to the property, and the Village appealed.


OVERVIEW: The trustee of a land trust sought to have the zoning ordinance declared unconstitutional so the land could be sold to an oil company. The court held that the evidence fell short of overcoming the presumptive validity of the ordinance. A covenant restricting the ownership and use of two of the lots to Caucasians was meaningless because it was unenforceable. A covenant declaring that the realty should be known as business lots was not a restriction on the property because it was too vague. The Village's comprehensive use plan, while entitled to consideration but was not controlling, because Ill. Rev. Stats. ch. 24, § 11-12-6 (1965) provided that such plans were advisory only. The court determined that the fact that the property's value would be enhanced if a gasoline station was permitted did not make the ordinance confiscatory. The court reasoned that industrial complexes and a manufacturing plant were so far away that they did not infringe upon the residential complexion of the neighborhood, near-by nonconforming uses had little effect upon the land at issue, and the trustee's testified that the land could be developed for residential use, although that was not be the best use.


OUTCOME: The judgment of the trial court was reversed.
