DISPOSITION: REVERSED AND REMANDED WITH DIRECTIONS TO DISMISS.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiff motor vehicle dealership brought an action against defendant surety company, to recover under a bond issued to a used car dealer. On the parties' motions for summary judgment, the District Court for Cuming County (Nebraska) found that the surety company was liable under that provision of the bond which covered dealers' misappropriation of funds belonging to purchasers. The surety appealed.


OVERVIEW: The used car dealer purchased cars from the dealership and subsequently defaulted on his payments. The appellate court had to determine: (1) if the used car dealer was protected under the bond; and (2) whether the used car dealer misappropriated the funds of any purchasers. The appellate court held that although the 1989 amendments to Neb. Rev. Sat. § 60-1419 (Reissue 1993), were not made part of the bond issued by the surety, they automatically became part of the bond. Consequently, the appellate court considered the case at bar as though the bond provided according to § 60-1419. The surety contended that motor vehicle dealer's bonds issued under § 60-1419 were designed only to protect consumers and not to serve as an insurance policy for the business dealings between car dealers. The appellate court disagreed. The statute protected car dealers as well as other persons, but only from the acts enumerated in the statute, it did not protect against all losses. Upon delivery of the vehicles the dealership did not retain or reserve titles to them. The dealer's failure to remit payment was merely a failure to pay his debts, not a misappropriation of funds of purchasers.


OUTCOME: The judgment of the trial court was reversed and the case was remanded.
