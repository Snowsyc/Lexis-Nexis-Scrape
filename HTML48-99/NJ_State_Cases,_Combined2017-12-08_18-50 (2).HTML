OVERVIEW: Taxpayer, a Michigan corporation with warehouses in New Jersey, was assessed a sales tax deficiency under the New Jersey Sales and Use Tax Act, N.J. Stat. Ann. § 54:32B-1 et seq., on sales of office furniture purchased by out-of-state dealers who sold it to in-state customers. OUTCOME: The challenge to the taxation director's assessment of a sales tax deficiency on the taxpayer's "dropshipment" sales of office furniture was upheld because the transactions were exempt sales for resale, and the attempt to characterize the three-party sale as a direct sale from taxpayer to New Jersey customers departed from the legislative intent to impose tax on the retail consumer at the point of final consumption.
