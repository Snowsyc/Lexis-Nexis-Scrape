PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] Appeal from District Court, Pittsburg County; James B. Martin, Trial Judge.



DISPOSITION: Affirmed in part; reversed in part.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant McAlester Urban Renewal Authority challenged a judgment of the District Court, Pittsburg County (Oklahoma) fixing the value of appellee property owners' land and awarding the owners attorney fees in the Authority's action to condemn the owners' real property.


OVERVIEW: The Authority instituted the action to condemn real property belonging to the owners. The commissioners fixed the value of the property at $ 9,705. The owners demanded trial by jury. The jury awarded $ 15,560 as damages. The owners then filed a motion pursuant to Okla. Stat. tit. 11, § 1663(f) (1971) requesting the trial court to award them a reasonable attorney's fee. The trial court entered an order awarding the owners a $ 2,000 attorney's fee to be taxed as costs and further awarded the owners' interest on the award at the rate of 10 percent per annum. On appeal, the court held that the Authority's contention that § 1663 was unconstitutional was without merit. The court found that attorney fees awarded pursuant to § 1663(f) were to be treated as costs. However, the court then determined that the words "judgment or order" as used in Okla. Stat. tit. 12, § 727 (1971) providing for judgment interest, had reference to the merits of the case and not mere incidental allowances for costs. Thus, the court found that the trial court erred in awarding interest on the attorney's fee award under § 727 as it did not include costs.


OUTCOME: The trial court's order was affirmed insofar as it awarded an attorney's fee to the owners and reversed insofar as it held the attorney's fee was to bear interest at the rate of 10 percent per annum.
