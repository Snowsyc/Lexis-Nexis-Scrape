Supreme Court of North Dakota


79 N.D. 62; 54 N.W.2d 748; 1952 N.D. LEXIS 99



August 28, 1952, Filed


PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] Appeal from the District Court of Ward County, Roy A. Ilvedson, J.



DISPOSITION: Affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant estate executors sought review of an order of the District Court of Ward County (North Dakota), which applied N.D. R. Ct. § 26-1018 (1943) by allowing life insurance proceeds to pass directly to the beneficiaries free of the insured's debt and ordered defendants to give the insurance proceeds to plaintiff.


OVERVIEW: Plaintiff was the legal heir of an insured whose life insurance policy named his estate as the beneficiary of the insurance proceeds. No other contract was made regarding the policy proceeds. Defendants were the executors of the estate who sought dismissal of plaintiff's suit claiming plaintiff had no right to the proceeds, as N.D. R. Ct. § 26-1018 (1943) authorized the proceeds to pass to the beneficiaries free of the insured's debts was unconstitutional. The trial court ordered defendants to give the proceeds to plaintiff and defendants appealed. On appeal, the court affirmed, holding where the legislature reenacted the statute, the legislature was presumed to have been informed of the prior judicial construction of the former statute that allowed the proceeds to remain free of an insured's debts. The legislature therefore intended the law to be that the proceeds pass untouched to the beneficiaries. Nothing in the statutory amendments indicated the legislature intended to change the statute's meaning.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the trial court's judgment in favor of plaintiff, holding the statute that allowed the insurance proceeds to pass directly to the beneficiaries was constitutional and construing the legislature's notice of prior judicial construction of statute to mean the legislature intended the proceeds to continue to remain free of the insured's debts.

F. W. WOOLWORTH CO. v. GRAY


No. 7184


Supreme Court of North Dakota


77 N.D. 757; 46 N.W.2d 295; 1951 N.D. LEXIS 114



February 5, 1951, Filed


PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] Appeal from the District Court of Burleigh County, Geo. Thom, Jr., Judge, plaintiff appeals from an order sustaining a demurrer to the complaint.



DISPOSITION: Affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiff company appealed an order from the District Court of Burleigh County (North Dakota) that sustained a demurrer by defendant Tax Commissioner of the State of North Dakota to the company's suit to recover taxes paid under the Retail Sales Tax Act, 1949 N.D. Laws 341.


OVERVIEW: The company sold merchandise in six North Dakota cities. The company claimed that a change in Section 6 of the Act exempted or excluded from the sales tax all sales for 24 cents or less. Affirming, the court said that the trial court was correct in sustaining the demurrer. The complaint did not state facts sufficient to constitute a cause of action. The Commissioner required the company to pay a tax of two percent on its gross receipts from all sales, including gross receipts from sales with sale prices of 24 cents or less. The Commissioner was right in so doing. This was what the company was required to pay under the Act. The only change in Section 6 was in the first three items to be applied in the collection of the tax. The remaining items were left as they had been. The Act had uniform operation. It applied uniformly and generally throughout the state and operated similarly upon all persons similarly situated and equally affected. All who were similarly situated were similarly treated. The burden of the tax was on the purchaser, but the obligation to pay the tax was on the seller. The obligation of the purchaser was to the retailer. The tax became a part of the sales price.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment that sustained the demurrer.

PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendants, states attorneys and the attorney general, sought review of the judgment of the District Court of Cass County (North Dakota), which granted plaintiff railway company's request for an injunction prohibiting defendants from enforcing or attempting to enforce the penalty provisions of N.D. OVERVIEW: N.D. Cent. Code § 49-1313 provided that a railway company was prohibited from operating a self-propelled engine or machine upon its tracks. N.D. Cent. Code § 49-1315 provided that the penalty for a violation of N.D. Cent. Code § 49-1313 was a fine not less than $ 100, and not to exceed $ 500. The railway company operated a power ballaster, which was designed for tramping ballast under railroad ties. The railway company filed an action seeking to enjoin the states attorneys and the attorney general from enforcing or attempting to enforce the penalty provisions of N.D. Cent. Code § 49-1515. The trial court granted the railway company's request for the injunction, and defendants sought review. On appeal, the court found that the issues raised by the railway company were ripe for declaratory judgment. The court held that N.D. Cent. Code § 49-1313 was applicable to the railway company's operation of the power ballaster because the size, weight, power and self-propulsion of the ballaster placed the machine in the class of hazardous obstacles covered by § 49-1313. The court remanded the case to the trial court with instructions to modify its judgment by dissolving the restraining order.


OUTCOME: The court remanded the case to the trial court with instructions to dissolve the restraining order entered against defendants.

Plaintiff appeals from an order sustaining a demurrer to the complaint.



DISPOSITION: Affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: The District Court of Burleigh County (North Dakota) entered an order sustaining a demurrer filed by defendants, a state government, its treasurer, a bar association, and its president. Plaintiff litigant appealed the order.


OVERVIEW: The litigant alleged that it became necessary for him to file an action in the office of a district court clerk and that he was required to pay a fee. The litigant later took and perfected an appeal to the supreme court from a judgment of the district court and was required to pay and did pay to the supreme court's clerk another fee. The litigant filed an action against the government, the treasurer, the association, and the president, claimed that the statutes that provided for the fees, which was increased, were unconstitutional and wholly void. The trial court entered an order sustaining the demurrer filed by the government. On appeal, the court found that the fees charged by the clerk of the district court were not dependent upon or connected with the fees to be charged by the clerk of the supreme court and vice versa. The legislature could provide for the collection of reasonable fees for services such as those performed by the clerks of the respective courts. The court litigant had no interest in the fees.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the trial court's order.

OZARK-MAHONING CO. v. STATE


No. 7112


Supreme Court of North Dakota


76 N.D. 464; 37 N.W.2d 488; 1949 N.D. LEXIS 67



April 26, 1949, Filed


PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] Appeal from the District Court of Divide County, A. J. Gronna, Judge.

Action to determine adverse claims and to quiet title. From a judgment for the plaintiff the defendant, the State of North Dakota, appeals.



DISPOSITION: Affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant, the State of North Dakota, sought review from a judgment entered by the District Court of Divide County (North Dakota) in favor of respondent corporation in an action to determine adverse claims and to quiet title to a lake bed.


OVERVIEW: The corporation purchased the lots surrounding the lake from the patentees or their grantees, acquiring all but two. The patents contained no mineral or other reservations or restrictions. As owner of the lots, the corporation, as a riparian owner, claimed title to practically all of the bed of the lake. The State on the other hand denied the claim and asserted ownership in itself on the ground that the lake was navigable when North Dakota was admitted to the Union, when the survey by the government was made, and when the patents through which the corporation derived its title were issued. As measured by the federal rule as to navigability, the court determined that it was certain that the lake was not navigable in fact at the time of the admission of the State into the Union or at the time the lands abutting on its meander lines were patented. Accordingly, the title to the land under the waters of the lake did not pass to the State when it was admitted into the Union, but remained in the United States subject to such disposition as the latter saw fit to make of it. The rights of the corporation under the patents issued by the United States government were fixed and vested.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment of the trial court.
