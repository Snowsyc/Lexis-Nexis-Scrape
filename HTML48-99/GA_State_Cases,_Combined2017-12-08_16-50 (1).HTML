SUBSEQUENT HISTORY:  [***1] Rehearing Denied November 30, 1990.


PRIOR HISTORY: Child molestation. Floyd Superior Court. Before Judge Salmon.



DISPOSITION: Judgment affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant challenged a decision of the Floyd Superior Court (Georgia), which entered a conviction for child molestation.


OVERVIEW: Defendant sexually molested his two stepdaughters, "H," aged three years, and "S," aged four years. Witnesses testified the little girls told them that defendant had molested them. A pediatrician testified she examined both girls and found evidence that both girls had been molested. When called to testify, H refused to answer any questions and the trial court found that she was not a competent witness. On appeal, defendant argued that the verdict was against the weight of the evidence. The court affirmed the conviction, holding that the testimony of the pediatrician and of S, which established that both girls were molested, was sufficient to support the verdict. The court also held that S's testimony was properly admitted under the Child Hearsay Act, Ga. Code Ann. § 24-3-16, and that her testimony that she saw defendant molest H was sufficient to convict defendant. Thus, the motion for a directed verdict as to H was properly denied. The court also held that the trial court properly denied a mistrial because the testimony regarding defendant's drug problem was relevant to address defendant's statement that he could only have committed the crimes if he were on drugs.


OUTCOME: Defendant's conviction was affirmed.

Blackmon v. State


No. A90A1441


Court of Appeals of Georgia


197 Ga. App. 133; 397 S.E.2d 728; 1990 Ga. App. LEXIS 1217



October 2, 1990, Decided


PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] Aggravated assault, etc. DeKalb Superior Court. Before Judge Tillman.



DISPOSITION: Judgment affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed a judgment of the DeKalb Superior Court (Georgia), which entered a jury verdict that convicted him of three counts of aggravated assault and one count of criminal damage to property in the first degree and denied his motion for a new trial.


OVERVIEW: The evidence showed that defendant poured gasoline onto a bed occupied by his wife and two stepchildren and that he then ignited the gasoline. A jury convicted him of three counts of aggravated assault and one count of criminal damage to property in the first degree. The court affirmed, determining that the trial court did not err in discounting defendant's testimony and finding that defendant's custodial statement was freely and voluntarily made. The trial court was not required to charge the jury on the subject of the voluntariness of defendant's confession where defendant did not make a specific request for such a charge. The trial court's jury instruction on defendant's flight could have reasonably been read as a "permissive inference" rather than a "mandatory presumption" where the charge did not require the jury to infer guilt from defendant's flight but merely suggested that guilt may be taken into consideration as having a tendency to establish guilt. Consequently, the trial court's charge on flight was not unconstitutionally burden shifting. The trial court's charges on aggravated assault were accurate statements of the law.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed defendant's convictions for three counts of aggravated assault and one count of criminal damage to property in the first degree.

CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendants sought review of their conviction by the Bibb Superior Court (Georgia) for aggravated assault upon a peace officer and mutiny in a penal institution.


OVERVIEW: Defendants were convicted of aggravated assault upon a peace officer and mutiny in a penal institution. The court held that a comparison of the elements of mutiny under O.C.G.A. § 16-10-54 and aggravated assault under O.C.G.A. § 16-5-21 revealed that a conviction for mutiny required proof of one element that was not necessary for the aggravated assault conviction. Therefore, the aggravated assault charge was an offense included within the mutiny charge pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 16-1-6(a) and was vacated.


OUTCOME: Defendants' convictions as to mutiny were affirmed. Defendants' convictions as to aggravated assault were vacated.
