SUBSEQUENT HISTORY:  [**1] Rehearing Denied June 27, 1984.


PRIOR HISTORY: Appeals from the Circuit Court for Alachua County. Elzie Sanders, Judge.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendants appealed their conviction in the Circuit Court for Alachua County (Florida) for conspiracy to traffic in cannabis, in violation of Flat. Stat. ch. 777.04 (1981) and Fla. Stat. ch. 898.135 (1981).


OVERVIEW: As a result of an undercover operation, defendants were charged with conspiring to possess in excess of 100 pounds of cannabis, and convicted of conspiracy to traffic in cannabis in violation of Fla. Stat. ch. 777.04 (1981) and Fla. Stat. ch. 893.135 (1981). On appeal, the court affirmed defendants' convictions, holding that the trial court did not err in denying defendants' motion for a mistrial because the jury was instructed to disregard the undercover officer's characterization of defendants as partners, and that there was sufficient evidence of defendants' agreement and intent to possess the cannabis to convict defendant, who acted as a "go-between," of conspiracy. Finally, the court rejected defendants' constitutional challenge to ch. 893.135 because defendants were charged with conspiracy and thus not subject to a mandatory minimum sentence, and because their equal protection argument was unavailing.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed defendants' convictions because the undercover officer's description of them as partners was cured by jury instruction, the evidence was sufficient to convict "go-between" defendant of conspiracy, no element of the crime conspired to was to be committed solely by the undercover agent, and defendants' constitutional challenge to the statute allowing a reduced sentence for cooperation was groundless.
