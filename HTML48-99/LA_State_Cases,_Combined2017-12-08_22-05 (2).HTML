CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant savings account owners appealed an order of the Third Judicial District Court, Parish of Lincoln (Louisiana), which forfeited seven savings accounts that were seized pursuant to a search warrant of a residence that was used for unlawful drug activity.


OVERVIEW: Pursuant to the Louisiana Drug Racketeering Act, La. Rev. Stat. Ann. § 15:1351 et seq., the State filed a petition for forfeiture in rem against nine savings accounts seized in a drug dealer's residence. Because two owners of the accounts were not served, the State did not proceed to judgment against two accounts. The trial court entered a judgment of forfeiture. The court affirmed. The court held that defendants were properly served and that La. Rev. Stat. The court found that it could not address defendants' arguments that the State failed to prove that the accounts were derived from drug activity because they failed to provide a transcript of the record, and therefore, it presumed that the trial court's judgment was supported by sufficient evidence. La. Rev. Stat. Ann. § 32:1550A(7)(c) was not applicable to defendants' situation.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the order of the trial court, which decreed a forfeiture of seven savings accounts seized as a result of unlawful drug activity.
