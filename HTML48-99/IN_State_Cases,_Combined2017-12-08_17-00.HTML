PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] Appeal from Hendricks Superior Court No. 2, The Honorable John C. Mowrer, Judge.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Through a criminal petition to transfer, appellee challenged a judgment from the First District Court of Appeals (Indiana), in which that court sustained appellant state's request for review and reversed a previous ruling granting appellee's motion to suppress evidence obtained at a roadblock of appellee's driving while intoxicated.


OVERVIEW: The state police conducted a roadblock, stopping five consecutive cars at a time, and waving others through for the time it took to conduct the check on the five cars being inspected. After each group of five was inspected and released, the next five consecutive vehicles were detained for the same procedure. All stopped motorists were asked to produce licenses or registrations. If a violation was suspected or alcohol consumption detected, the driver was then pulled over to an adjacent parking lot. Upon its granting transfer, the court and reversed. The court held the roadblock constitutional under a three-factor test: 1) the state's legitimate concern in checking for automobile registration and certification and society's grave concern in apprehending and deterring drunken driving, 2) the high success rate of the roadblock in detecting drunken driving and the deterrent effect of its visibility, and 3) the low degree of interference with individual liberty, given the two to three minute stops per vehicle. The court concluded by holding that to the extent McLaughlin v. State, 471 N.E.2d 1125 (Ind. App. 1984), conflicted with the instant court's holding, it was overruled.


OUTCOME: The court granted the petition for transfer, reversed the suppression of the evidence in question, and ordered the trial level judge to proceed with the case.
