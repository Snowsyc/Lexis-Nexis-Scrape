No need existed to enact additional legislation because the use and sales tax complemented each other. The court agreed with the television company's argument that television broadcasting involved interstate commerce and that it might not be burdened by state privilege taxes for if the levy was directly on commerce. However, the court held that the rule was not applicable in the case. The tapes and films, upon reaching the station, had finally come to rest for the purpose for which they were sent.


OUTCOME: The order of the lower court in favor of the Commissioner in a suit involving the television company was affirmed.
