CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant bank sought review of the order from the Administrative Hearing Commission, which denied the bank's claim for a state tax refund.


OVERVIEW: When a 1982 Missouri case declared the intangible personal property tax imposed on holders of accounts in banks by Mo. Rev. Stat. § 148.480 (1978) unconstitutional, the bank sought refunds from the Director of Revenue for the intangible tax it had paid during the years 1975 through 1981 under § 136.035 (1986). OUTCOME: The court affirmed the order denying the bank a refund for intangible personal property taxes it had paid.

PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] From the Circuit Court of St. Louis County

Civil Appeal

Judge Franklin Ferriss



DISPOSITION: Reversed and Remanded With Directions.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiff purchaser challenged a final order of the Circuit Court of St. Louis County (Missouri) finding that Mo. Rev. Stat. § 140.170 was unconstitutional as it did not require the tax collector to give notice by mail to persons owning and residing on property sought to be sold by a tax sale. The trial court voided the tax sale and the collector's deed and upheld co-defendant plumbing company's previously obtained judgment lien on the property.


OVERVIEW: The purchaser filed an action to quiet title to realty that he had purchased at a St. Louis County collector's delinquent tax sale, third offering, published and conducted under the Jones-Munger Law, Mo. Rev. Stat. ch. 140 (1978). On appeal, the purchaser sought a reversal of the order, reinstatement of his tax collector's deed, and extinguishment of the judgment lien on the property. On appeal, the court reversed and remanded with directions to quiet the purchaser's title and adjudge that defendants had no right, title, or interest in the real estate, and to award the purchaser whatever reasonable monthly rents and profits were recoverable. The court held that under the Missouri system of taxation, there were ample provisions for notice and opportunity for hearing throughout the whole period of assessing, levying and equalizing taxes. Hence, viewing the Jones-Munger Tax Law as a part of the whole taxation system, and taking into consideration its provisions for notice of sale, the court held that it was not in conflict with the constitutional provisions of Mo. Const. art. II, § 30, and that it was a valid legislative act.


OUTCOME: The court reversed and remanded the trial court's final order with directions to quiet the purchaser's title and adjudge that defendants had no right, title, or interest in the real estate, and to award the purchaser whatever reasonable monthly rents and profits were recoverable.

Supreme Court of Missouri


639 S.W.2d 606; 1982 Mo. LEXIS 406



October 7, 1982 


PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] From the Circuit Court of Cole County

Civil Appeal

Judge James F. McHenry



DISPOSITION: Reversed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiff unincorporated association (association) challenged a decision of the Circuit Court, Cole County (Missouri), which found in favor of defendant Secretary of State in the association's action challenging the constitutionality and application of Mo. Rev. Stat. § 116.110 (1981), which authorized withdrawal of signatures from an initiative petition after the deadline set by the Missouri Constitution for filing initiative petition signatures.


OVERVIEW: The association timely filed an initiative petition with the Secretary of State requesting a proposition be placed on the ballot. Intervenor-defendant group had circulated withdrawal petitions allegedly in compliance with Mo. Rev. Stat. § 116.110, which provided for the withdrawal of signatures from the initiative petitions after its filing with the Secretary of State. The group submitted 1857 withdrawals to the Secretary of State, which resulted in a deficiency. The Secretary of State, acting in compliance with Mo. Rev. Stat. § 116.110, denied certification of the initiative petition. The circuit court allowed the group to intervene, granted summary judgment in denial of the association's petition, and enjoined the Secretary of State from certifying their initiative petition as sufficient. On appeal, the court reversed finding Mo. Rev. Stat. § 116.110 interfered with and impeded the initiative power. The court held that proponents of an initiative petition had to be able to rely upon signatures they had obtained. The Secretary of State was ordered by the court to take all steps necessary to assure that the initiative petition would be on the ballet at the general election.


OUTCOME: The judgment of the trial court was reversed and the Secretary of State was directed to take all steps necessary to assure that the initiative petition would be on the ballot throughout the State of Missouri at the general election on November 2, 1982.

Supreme Court of Missouri


637 S.W.2d 670; 1982 Mo. LEXIS 401



August 31, 1982 


PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] From the Circuit Court of Stone County (Juvenile Division)

Civil Appeal

Special Judge Thomas K. McGuire



DISPOSITION: Affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant parents sought review of the order of the Circuit Court of Stone County, Juvenile Division (Missouri), which terminated their parental rights, under Mo. Rev. Stat. § 211.447 (2)(h)(a-b), upon the petition of respondent Stone County Juvenile Officer. The parents contended that § 211.447 was unconstitutional and that there were constitutional violations under U.S. Const. amend. IV and Mo. Const. art. I, § 2.


OVERVIEW: After the parents were arrested for theft, the Department of Family Services (DFS) investigated the children's homes. The children were removed from the trailers after the DFS found that there was no edible food, no hot water, and unsanitary conditions. The parents subsequently failed to comply with the DFS's service agreements. The court affirmed the termination of parental rights. The court found that (1) where the evidence showed that the environment from which the children were removed was injurious to their welfare, the action by the juvenile court in removing the children was proper under Mo. Rev. Stat. § 211.031 (1978); (2) although reunification of the family was the desired outcome under ch. 211, the best interest of the child was controlling; (3) there was no duty on the DFS to provide copies of their report to the parties in a termination hearing; (4) the trailers were inspected in compliance with § 210.145, which called for a thorough investigation of the children's home environment; and (5) the constitutionality of the statutes could not be presented for the first time on appeal because nothing was preserved for review.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment of the juvenile court.
