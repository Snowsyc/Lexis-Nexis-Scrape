PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] Appeal from Traffic and Ordinance Division of Recorder's Court of Detroit, Wood (Andrew C.), J.



DISPOSITION: Reversed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed the judgment of the Traffic and Ordinance Division of Recorder's Court of Detroit (Michigan), which convicted defendant of loitering, contrary to Detroit, Mich., Municipal Code § 28-8-8 (1964).


OVERVIEW: An arrest warrant was issued for a third party, who was found in an apartment in which narcotics paraphernalia was observed in every room. Defendant was in the apartment at the time and was charged with loitering in violation of § 28-8-8. Defendant was convicted of the offense, but the court reversed the conviction and ordered that defendant be discharged. The court held that defendant's conduct in visiting the borrower of her hair rollers in another apartment of the same building where defendant resided was patently innocent. To be convicted of violating the ordinance, there had to have been some evidence that defendant knowingly loitered in an apartment where illegal narcotic paraphernalia was kept or sold. Such evidence was not presented, because defendant testified that she was only in the apartment for a few minutes before the police arrived, and she was unaware of the drug paraphernalia.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the judgment convicting defendant of loitering in an apartment where illegal narcotic paraphernalia was kept or sold.
