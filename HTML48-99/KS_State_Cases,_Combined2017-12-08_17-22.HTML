PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiff quadriplegic filed a personal injury action in diversity against defendants, driver, employer, and insurance company. OVERVIEW: The quadriplegic argued that a statute violated the state constitutional guarantee of the right to jury trial and of a remedy by due course of law for injured persons. The court determined that its resolution of the certified question applied equally to Kan. Stat. Ann. § 60-19a02 (Supp. 1988) because § 60-19a02 gave rise to no new issues. OUTCOME: The court determined that the legislature's limitation of recovery for pain and suffering or noneconomic damages in personal injury actions did not violate the state constitution.
