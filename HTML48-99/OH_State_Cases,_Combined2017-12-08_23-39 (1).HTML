CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendants appealed an order of the Lakewood Municipal Court (Ohio), which ordered them to forfeit a certain amount of money obtained from the criminal operation of gambling and a gambling house. Plaintiff city cross-appealed as to the amount of the criminal forfeiture.


OVERVIEW: Defendants were convicted of various gambling offenses. Based on that, the city commenced forfeiture proceedings to seize profits derived from the unlawful activity. The lower court determined the amount of money linked to the gambling operation and ordered the city to return the rest of the money that was seized. On appeal, the court affirmed the lower court's judgment, concluding that Ohio Rev. Code Ann. § 2933.41(C) applied to defendants' case because it was passed before the date of the hearing. The date of the seizure was irrelevant. According to § 2933.41(C), defendants' profits were subject to forfeiture if it was unlawful for them to possess the funds or the money was the subject or used in connection with a criminal offense. The latter applied here presuming that the statute operated prospectively, which was the case absent express contrary language. There was no error in the return of some of the seized funds to defendants. The determination of the amount subject to forfeiture was reasonable given that the law authorized the taking of funds upon a showing that they were produced by the gambling operation.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed.
