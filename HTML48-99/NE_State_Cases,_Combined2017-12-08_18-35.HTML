CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant voter sought review of a judgment from the District Court for Sarpy County (Nebraska) that dismissed his petition that challenged the validity of appellee city's redistricting plan. The district court held that the city did not violate Neb. Rev. Stat. § 5-108 (Reissue 1943) by using other methods then the most recent federal census to determine population sizes because § 5-108 was not mandatory.


OVERVIEW: Section 5-108 provided that when any city elected members of any governing board of districts, such districts must have been substantially equal in population as determined by the most recent federal census. The court held that the statute was enacted to ensure compliance with decisions of the United States Supreme Court relating to the one man, one vote principle. Finally, the language "most recent federal census" in § 5-108 included a special census.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the judgment that dismissed the voter's petition and remanded for a new trial to determine if, according to the most recent federal census, the election wards of established by the city were substantially equal in population in accordance with the principle of one man, one vote.
