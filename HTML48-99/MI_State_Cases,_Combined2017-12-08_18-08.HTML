May 9, 1972, Submitted Division One  
January 17, 1973, Decided

DISPOSITION: Reversed and remanded.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant former city employee appealed a decision of the Wayne Circuit Court (Michigan), which affirmed his dismissal, after a hearing, by plaintiff Civil Service Commission of the City of Hamtramck, Mich. The employee was arrested on a drunk-driving charge, and his license was suspended after he refused to take a breathalyzer test. The employee was terminated because he had violated Hamtramck, Mich., Civ. Serv. R. & Reg. XII, § 5(e).


OVERVIEW: By driving a city truck when his driver's license was suspended, the employee was guilty of "conduct unbecoming an officer or employee of the city," as prohibited by Rule XII, § 5(e). On appeal, the court reversed. The court held that the Commission exceeded its authority in formulating the "rule" that the employee was accused of violating. The Hamtramck, Mich., City Charter clearly imposed on the Commission the duty to act by establishing rules and regulations for the guidance of civil service personnel. However, Rule XII, § 5(e) did not comport with the charter's mandate. The court noted that it was not enough for an administrative body to label a purported standard a rule. The rule proscribing "conduct unbecoming an officer or employee of the city" presented a standard so impalpable as to be no standard at all, as it did not provide meaningful guidance concerning the required mode of conduct. The court concluded that the employee had been illegally discharged and that he was entitled to immediate reinstatement.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the decision and remanded the case. The court held that the employee was entitled to back pay for the entire period of the wrongful discharge, amounting to normal wages that he would have received while working in his regular employment, less any wages actually earned during that period.
