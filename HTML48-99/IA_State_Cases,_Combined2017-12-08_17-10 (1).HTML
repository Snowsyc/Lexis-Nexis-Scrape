PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] Appeal from Linn District Court. Ansel J. Chapman. Plaintiffs appeal from order sustaining motion to dismiss their petition filed under chapter 25A, The Code, 1971.



DISPOSITION: AFFIRMED.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiffs sought review of a decision of the Linn District Court (Iowa) to dismiss plaintiffs' action against defendant state highway commission, in behalf of the State of Iowa, under the State Tort Claims Act (Chapter 25A, The Code, 1971). The court construed § 25A.14(4).


OVERVIEW: Plaintiffs alleged that the State attempted wrongfully to evict them from their home. The motion to dismiss was asserted on the grounds that the action was against the state highway commission, and not against the State, and that the alleged conduct was an abuse of process, which was within the exceptions to State liability under § 25A.14(4). On appeal, the court affirmed. The petition asked for judgment against the State alone, and it was quite clear that plaintiffs did not seek redress from anyone except the State. The court disregarded the misleading manner in which plaintiffs entitled their suit. Plaintiffs conceded that their action was one for abuse of process, but that such an action was not barred under the Act, as § 25A.14(4) barred actions for an "abuse or process." The court found that its intervention was required to change the word "or" to "of" under § 25A.14(4). The court found that § 25A.14(4) contained a patent clerical error. The court's action was warranted in order to give effect to legislative intent. The Act was lifted from the Federal Tort Claims Act, and the counterpart to § 25A.14(4), 28 U.S.C.S. § 2680(h), contained the phrase "abuse of process."


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment of the trial court.
