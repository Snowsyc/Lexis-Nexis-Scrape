DISPOSITION: Affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiff son appealed the judgment of the Wake County Superior Court (North Carolina), which ruled in favor of defendant beneficiaries and found that the son was not entitled to share in his father's estate because he was illegitimate and his father had not recognized him by any of the ways set forth in N.C. Gen. Stat. § 29-19.


OVERVIEW: The illegitimate son of the deceased claimed that he was entitled to a share of his intestate father's estate. The superior court found that the son had not complied with N.C. Gen. Stat. § 29-19, so he was not entitled to share in the estate. The son appealed and argued that the statute was unconstitutional. The court affirmed. Since a father had to act to include an illegitimate child in his estate, strict compliance with the statute was required.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment of the superior court.

OVERVIEW: The court dismissed the appeal filed by the State for lack of jurisdiction. The court held that 15A-979 was to be read in conjunction with N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-1448(a)(1), which stated that a case remained open for appeal for a period of 10 days after the entry if judgment. The burden was upon the State to show that it had fully complied with the statutory requirements for an appeal, and the State failed to prove that the appropriate certificate was timely filed. Because the State failed to properly perfect its appeal, the court held that the State's appeal was not authorized by statute and the court had no jurisdiction. The court dismissed defendant's cross-appeal because he could only appeal a denial of a motion to suppress after a conviction.


OUTCOME: The court dismissed the appeal of the State and the cross-appeal of defendant.

OVERVIEW: On appeal, defendant argued that § 14-177 did not include the act of a consensual fellatio between an adult male and an adult female. OUTCOME: The court affirmed the superior court's judgment of conviction for a crime against nature.
