167 W. Va. 911; 280 S.E.2d 816; 1981 W. Va. LEXIS 708



July 29, 1981, Decided



DISPOSITION:  [***1] Remanded.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: The Circuit Court of McDowell County (West Virginia) certified questions to reexamine the doctrine of sovereign immunity as it related to the State and county boards of education, growing out of a dispute involving the misapplication of funds raised by a special county election, intended to supplement the wages of county non-teaching school employees. The employees brought a declaratory judgment action in the circuit court, which denied relief.


OVERVIEW: The county voters approved a school levy to supplement the monthly wages of county school employees. The board paid the supplement to eligible non-teaching employees until a State bill established a minimum pay scale for auxiliary and service employees of county schools. The board then ceased paying non-teaching employees the supplement. The court held that the board improperly treated two classes of employees differently. The board continued to pay teaching employees a supplement even when their salaries were also increased by the legislature. Such a difference in treatment was not permissible. The board had no discretion in the expenditure of the levy funds and thus was required to pay the monthly supplements to the non-teaching employees. The board was not entitled to assert the protection of sovereign immunity, and therefore, the circuit court had jurisdiction to hear the claim. The State board's involvement was as a third-party defense. The circuit court of a county other than Kanawha County lacked jurisdiction to hear claims naming a state agency or official. However, McDowell County had jurisdiction over the employees' complaint against the county board.


OUTCOME: The court answered the certified questions and remanded the case for further proceedings.

OVERVIEW: At the instance of relator, two previous indictments for the same murder were held invalid by the circuit court. Therefore, the trial on the third indictment was not precluded by the provisions of the "three term" statutory provision under § 62-3-21.


OUTCOME: The court denied relator's petition for a writ prohibiting respondents from proceeding to try relator upon an indictment for murder.

OUTCOME: The court awarded the writ of mandamus.
