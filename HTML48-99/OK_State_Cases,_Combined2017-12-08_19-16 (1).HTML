January 20, 1982 


PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] An Appeal from the District Court of Oklahoma County, Oklahoma. The Honorable Raymond Naifeh, District Judge. WILLIAM R. BURROWS, appellant, was convicted of Murder in the First Degree, in the District Court of Oklahoma County, Case No. CRF-77-2769, was sentenced to death, and he appeals.



DISPOSITION: The Judgment and Sentence is Modified from death to life imprisonment, and AND AS SO MODIFIED, the Judgment and Sentence is AFFIRMED.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant sought review of his conviction by the District Court of Oklahoma County (Oklahoma) for the first degree murder of his pregnant wife. Defendant also appealed the death sentence imposed by the trial court based on the jury's finding that the murder was especially heinous, atrocious or cruel, an aggravating circumstance pursuant to Okla. Stat. tit. 21, § 701.12(4).


OVERVIEW: Defendant argued, inter alia, that the admission of evidence of his wife's pregnancy was reversible error. The court agreed that the fact that the victim was pregnant at the time of her death was irrelevant during the first stage of the trial, and that defendant's motion to exclude that evidence should have been sustained by the trial court. The court found, however, that the introduction of the evidence in this case did not contribute to the jury's finding of guilt. With regard to the sentence, the court found that the evidence amply supported the finding that the murder was aggravated by the circumstances of being an especially heinous killing. In addition, the court held that the second aggravating circumstance found by the jury, that the accused knowingly created a risk of death to more than one person, Okla. Stat. tit. 21, § 701.12(2), was constitutional. The court ruled, however, that under Okla. Stat. § tit. 21, § 701.11 (Supp. 1980), the death penalty could not be imposed if mitigating circumstances outweighed aggravating circumstances. Here, two of the three judges found that there were sufficient mitigating circumstances to warrant modification of the sentence.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed defendant's conviction for murder in the first degree and modified the sentence from death to life imprisonment.
