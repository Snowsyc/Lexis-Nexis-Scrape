Chenault v. Bexar County


No. C-7446


SUPREME COURT OF TEXAS


782 S.W.2d 206; 1989 Tex. LEXIS 126; 33 Tex. Sup. J. 132



December 20, 1989 


SUBSEQUENT HISTORY:  [**1] This Opinion substituted by Court for Withdrawn Opinion dated April 19, 1989.


PRIOR HISTORY: FROM BEXAR COUNTY, FOURTH DISTRICT.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Petitioner justices of the peace challenged the decision of the Court of Appeals for the Fourth District of Texas, which affirmed the trial court's judgment that found to be constitutional the order of respondent, the Bexar County Commissioners Court, abolishing petitioners' positions.


OVERVIEW: Respondent, the Bexar County Commissioners Court, redistricted all of the county's justice of the peace precincts and created new justice precinct places. Respondent then abolished one of the justice of the peace places in each precinct that had two justices. Petitioner justices of the peace were elected to two of the places that were abolished. Petitioners challenged respondent's order, contending that the order violated Tex. Const. art. XVI, § 65. The trial court and the court of appeals found the order to be constitutional. On appeal the court reversed the court of appeals. The court held that the order was unconstitutional and declared it void because the effect of abolishing the justice of the peace positions was to leave the timing of the remaining justice of the peace position in each precinct on an non-staggered election cycle, contrary to the scheme mandated in the first part of art. XVI, § 65.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the court of appeals' judgment in favor of respondent, the Bexar County Commissioners Court, because the order abolishing a justice of the peace position in three precincts left the remaining justice of the peace position in each precinct on a non-staggered election cycle and, thus, disrupted the staggered election scheme mandated in the Texas Constitution.

777 S.W.2d 391; 1989 Tex. LEXIS 129; 33 Tex. Sup. J. 12



October 2, 1989 


PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] FROM TRAVIS COUNTY THIRD DISTRICT.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Petitioners, school districts, children enrolled in those districts, and parents, sought review of an order of an appeals court in Travis County, Third District (Texas), which reversed a trial court judgment in favor of petitioners declaring that Texas's school financing system violated Texas Const. art. I, §§ 3 and 19, and art. VII, § 1, and entered judgment for respondent state officials.


OVERVIEW: Petitioners, school districts, children enrolled in those districts, and their parents, sought a declaration that Texas's school financing system, set forth in Tex. Educ. Respondent state officials sought review, and the court of appeals reversed, holding that the issue was political and not suitable for judicial review. While efforts had been made to lessen those disparities, the court found that those efforts did not satisfy the constitutional mandate, in Tex. Const. art. VII, § 1, on the state to provide an "efficient," meaning "productive" or "effective," system of public schools, and affirmed the trial court judgment of unconstitutionality.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the trial court's finding that Texas's property tax-based system for financing public education violated the state constitution, because the system did not address disparities in the relative abilities of various school districts to raise revenues, and the system did not insure that all children would receive an "efficient," meaning "productive" or "effective," education.

OVERVIEW: During a political campaign, petitioner mayoral candidate allegedly made defamatory statements against respondent mayor that were published by radio advertisements and by a magazine article.
