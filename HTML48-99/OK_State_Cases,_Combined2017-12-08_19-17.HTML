PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] An appeal from District Court, Stephens County; P. C. Largent, Jr., Judge. J.D. Varner was convicted for the crime of Operating a Motor Vehicle under the Influence of Intoxicating Liquor, Second and Subsequent Offense. He was sentenced to a term of three (3) years in the state penitentiary, and he appeals.



DISPOSITION: Judgment and Sentence affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed his conviction for operating a motor vehicle under the influence of intoxicating liquor from the District Court, Stephens County (Oklahoma) and his sentence to three years in prison.


OVERVIEW: The arresting officer testified that he had observed defendant backing his truck up on a highway late at night and, when approached, defendant appeared intoxicated. Another officer trained in administering a breathalyzer test testified that defendant's blood alcohol was above the legal limit. Various relatives and friends testified that defendant was sober the night he was arrested. On appeal, defendant argued that the trial court committed fundamental error in instructing the jury that the breathalyzer test results were presumptive evidence of intoxication because such an instruction shifted the burden of proof from the State to defendant. The court disagreed, holding that the presumption created by Okla. Stat. Tit. 47, § 756 was rebuttable, not conclusive. The presumption did not shift the burden to defendant to prove that he was not under the influence of intoxicating liquor when driving, nor did it change the ultimate burden of proof. The court noted that the presumption was relevant only to the issue of intoxication and was not a presumption that the accused was guilty of the offense.


OUTCOME: The judgment was affirmed.
