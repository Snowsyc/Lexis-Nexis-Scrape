DISPOSITION: The case is remanded, with all reserved questions answered, for further proceedings consistent with this opinion.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiffs, a fire fighters local and its president, brought a mandamus action in the District Court of Albany County, Wyoming, to compel defendants, a city and its officials, to comply with 1965 Wyo. Sess. Laws 197. Defendants argued that the statute sought to be enforced was unconstitutional and void. Without resolving the case and entering a final judgment, the district court elected to reserve the constitutional questions to a higher court.


OVERVIEW: Plaintiffs sought to compel defendants to enter into arbitration to determine the terms of a collective bargaining agreement dealing with wages, hours of service, and working conditions. The court remanded plaintiffs' action for mandamus back to the district court and held that the provisions of 1965 Wyo. Sess. Laws 197 did not violate the state constitution. CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant insurance company sought review of an order from the trial court (Wyoming), which found that plaintiff provided primary automobile insurance coverage rather than appellee insurance company and that appellant had to pay a judgment that was obtained in a wrongful death action.


OVERVIEW: Appellant and appellee both provided automobile insurance coverage to an employer. An employee was involved in an accident while driving a leased vehicle, which resulted in a wrongful death action being filed against his estate and the insurance companies. A judgment was obtained in the wrongful death action and subsequently the trial court ordered appellant to pay the judgment on the basis that it provided primary coverage to the employer. Upon review, the court reversed and remanded. The court found that both insurance companies' policies limited coverage for a hired automobile to excess insurance. The court held that the trial court improperly concluded that appellant's policy constituted the primary coverage where the excess insurance policies of both insurance companies were mutually repugnant. The court determined that it was immaterial which policy was issued first because liability was to be ascertained form a construction of the language of both policies. The court concluded that the liability of the insurance companies needed to be prorated in proportion to the liability limits provided by their respective policies.


OUTCOME: The court reversed an order that found plaintiff provided primary coverage and was required to pay a judgment that was obtained in a wrongful death action. The court remanded for further proceedings not inconsistent with its opinion.

National Tailoring Co. v. Scott


No. 2392


SUPREME COURT OF WYOMING


65 Wyo. 64; 196 P.2d 387; 1948 Wyo. LEXIS 16



August 3, 1948, Decided


PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] ERROR to District Court, Big Horn County; C. D. MURANE, Judge.

Suit by J. E. Scott against National Tailoring Company and Don C. Parker, Sheriff of Big Horn County, Wyoming, to restrain defendants, their agents, attorneys, or deputies, from foreclosing mortgage on plaintiff's realty and to have mortgage adjudged void and canceled. To review a judgment for plaintiff the defendants bring error.

Reversed with instructions.



DISPOSITION: Reversed with Instructions.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendants, the company, and the sheriff, sought review of a judgment from the District Court, Big Horn County (Wyoming) rendered in favor of plaintiff purchaser in an action whereby he sought to restrain defendants from foreclosing mortgage on the purchaser's realty and to have the mortgage adjudged void and canceled.


OVERVIEW: The purchaser bought a clothing company from the seller, who was the mortgagor of the company. The agreement between the parties was that the seller would continue to make the scheduled mortgage payments. Thereafter, the seller died, and several years later, the company sought to foreclose on the mortgage. The purchaser filed an action against the company and the sheriff seeking to enjoin them from proceeding with the foreclosure sale, and to have the mortgage declared void and canceled. The trial court rendered a judgment in favor of the purchaser, and the company and the sheriff appealed the decision. The purchaser reiterated his argument that the action taken by the company was barred by the statute of limitations. The court noted that the statute of limitations barred the cause of action, and not the remedy. In addition, the statute of limitations intended as a retrospective law must have given a person reasonable time to enforce a remedy available to him before the bar of the statute applied. Therefore, the decision of the trial court was erroneous.


OUTCOME: The judgment of the trial court was reversed with instructions.
