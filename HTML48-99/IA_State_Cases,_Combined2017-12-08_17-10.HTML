CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: After defendants were convicted, after trial in a police court, of drag racing in violation of § 321.284, The Code, 1971 (Iowa), they appealed to the Johnson District Court (Iowa). After a bench trial de novo, the district court convicted defendants of drag racing. Defendants appealed, claiming that the State failed to prove they were "competing for speed," as required under § 321.284. They also argued they were improperly denied a jury trial.


OVERVIEW: Defendants filed their demand for a jury trial two days after such demand was permitted under District Rule 4.7 (Iowa). The district court denied defendants' request for a jury trial due to their failure to file such request within the time required under Rule 4.7. At trial, two officers testified that they saw two vehicles driven by defendants on a road at a speed in excess of the posted speed limit. The element of competition was established by the evidence that defendants rapidly accelerated their vehicles and that one cut in front of the other at high speed.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed defendants' convictions.

Moreover, the court believed that the rule making authority of the APCC could withstand the challenge that it was an unconstitutional delegation of power under the state constitution's separation of powers clause.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the trial court's decision abating the action and remanded for further proceedings.
