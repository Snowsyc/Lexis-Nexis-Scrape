PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendants, insured and his wife, sought reversal of a summary judgment entered by a Utah trial court in favor of plaintiff insurer. The trial court held that the family exclusion provision in the policy was valid and enforceable, that the insurer was therefore not required to defend the insured in a civil action brought by his wife, and that the insurer was not required to pay any judgment she might obtain in her action.


OVERVIEW: The insured was the driver in an automobile accident in which his wife was injured. She filed a civil action for damages against her husband. OUTCOME: The court reversed the summary judgment that held the family exclusion provision in the policy was valid and enforceable, that the insurer was therefore not required to defend the insured in a civil action brought by his wife, and that the insurer was not required to pay any judgment she might obtain in her action.

CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: From the trial court (Utah), defendant appealed his conviction for aggravated sexual assault, which crime was a violation of Utah Code Ann. § 76-5-405. The trial court ruled on a motion in limine that defendant's prior convictions for rape and escape were admissible to impeach defendant's credibility. The rape conviction was 10 years old at the time of trial, and the escape convictions were 5 years old.


OVERVIEW: Defendant was convicted of aggravated sexual assault. Aggravated sexual assault was a violation of Utah Code Ann. § 76-5-405. Remoteness in time, per the court, reflected on probative value.


OUTCOME: The court reversed defendant's conviction and remanded the case for a new trial. The reversal was based on the failure to exclude the prior convictions.
