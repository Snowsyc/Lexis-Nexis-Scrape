OUTCOME: The court denied the owner's appeal.

COMMON PLEAS COURT OF NORTHUMBERLAND COUNTY, PENNSYLVANIA


1979 Pa. Dist. & Cnty. Dec. LEXIS 189; 12 Pa. D. & C.3d 1



June 20, 1979, Decided


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant, the president of a volunteer fire department, was arrested and charged with violating Pa. Cons. Stat. Ann. §§ 5512 and 5513, the gambling statutes of the Crimes Code of Pennsylvania. At the hearing on his omnibus pretrial motion, he admitted gambling on its premises. However, he contended that the statutes were unconstitutional under both the Pennsylvania and United States Constitutions.


OVERVIEW: The power of the court was limited to the narrow issue of determining whether the state gambling laws passed constitutional muster. The president contended that both Pa. Cons. Stat. The court disagreed because the fire department had other sources of revenue and did not depend only on gambling. The court also held that the state had the power to suppress gambling and that the legislature had to power to allow certain forms of gambling by legislation.


OUTCOME: The court denied the motion to quash information and found Pa. Cons. Stat. Ann. §§ 5512 and 5513 of the Pennsylvania Crimes Code constitutional.
