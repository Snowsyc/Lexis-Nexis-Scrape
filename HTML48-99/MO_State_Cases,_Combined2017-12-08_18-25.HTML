Civil Appeal From Review of Administrative Proceeding-Industrial Commission

Judge Alvin J. McFarland



DISPOSITION: Affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant claimant challenged a judgment of the Circuit Court of the City of St. Louis (Missouri), which affirmed the decision of the Industrial Commission crediting respondent employer for payments made to the employee after a job-related accident under its employee disability and death benefits plan. The claimant asserted a "vested right" to such payments and that the provisions of Mo. Rev. Stat. §§ 287.270 and 287.160 (1959) were misconstrued.


OVERVIEW: The claimant suffered a compensable injury and was paid for permanent partial disability under the plan, initially receiving full pay for 26 weeks and thereafter receiving one-half pay for 26 weeks. The claimant filed a claim with the Commission for additional benefits, and the Commission credited the employer for the payments already made under its plan. On appeal, the court held that: (1) the claimant elected benefits payments under the plan, which barred further claims against the employer arising from the accident; (2) the claimant's election to receive payments under the plan provided him with no vested right to such payments, which would have permitted recovery of additional workmen's compensation benefits once the election was made; (3) although Mo. Rev. Stat. § 287.160(3) (1959) required that the employer be given credit for the sums paid to the employee as a result of his injury.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment of the circuit court, which affirmed the decision of the Commission allowing the employer a credit for benefits paid to the claimant under the employer's plan, against the claimant's application to the Commission for additional benefits, after the claimant was injured in a job-related accident.

PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] From the Circuit Court of Dunklin County

Civil Appeal From Action for Damages (Monetary Recovery)

Judge Arthur U. Goodman, Jr.

Affirmed


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant driver appealed a decision of the Circuit Court of Dunklin County (Missouri), which entered judgment in accordance with a jury verdict in favor of plaintiff passenger, in the passenger's action to recover damages for personal injuries she sustained when the driver lost control of the vehicle in which the parties were travelling. The accident occurred in Arkansas; as such, Arkansas substantive law applied to the action.


OVERVIEW: The driver argued that the passenger had no cause of action under the Arkansas guest statutes, Ark. Stat. Ann. §§ 75-913 - 75-915, because the passenger was a "guest" and could not establish that the vehicle was wilfully and wantonly operated. On appeal, the court disagreed. A passenger was a guest if the transportation conferred a benefit only on the passenger; guest status did not arise if the transportation was primarily for the driver to attain an objective purpose. Evidence established that the driver requested the passenger's assistance in making a trip for the sole purpose of purchasing furniture for the driver's home. Thus, the jury could have reasonably inferred that the trip was undertaken to attain an objective of the driver and that a tangible, substantial benefit accrued to her by virtue of the passenger's assistance. As such, the passenger's status was an issue for the jury, and the driver's directed verdict motion was properly denied. Finally, references to willful negligence in the passenger's complaint did not render the complaint insufficient to state a simple negligence claim, because those references were considered surplusage and, accordingly, were disregarded.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the trial court's judgment in favor of the passenger in her negligence action against the driver.
