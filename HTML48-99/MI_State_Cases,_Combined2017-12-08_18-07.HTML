CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed an order of a trial court (Michigan), which convicted him upon his guilty plea of robbery armed, a violation of Mich. Comp. Laws § 750.529 and possession of a firearm during commission of a felony, a violation of Mich. Comp. Laws § 750.227b. On appeal, defendant contended that his felony-firearm conviction had to be set aside because it violated double jeopardy under U.S. Const. amend. V and Mich. Const. art. 1, § 15 (1963).


OVERVIEW: Defendant pled guilty to robbery armed and possession of a firearm during commission of a felony. On appeal, the court affirmed. Mich. Comp. Laws § 750.227b provided that one possessing a firearm when he committed or attempted a felony, except for carrying a concealed weapon or unlawful possession of a pistol by a licensee, was guilty of a felony. The court was not required to resort to double jeopardy tests because the legislative intent of § 750.227b was apparent. The statute applied by its terms to all felonies except those explicitly excluded. "Felony" included robbery armed under Mich. Comp. Laws § 750.529, and the legislature had attempted to make certainty of minimal punishment the standard where firearms were involved in the commission of felonies. The minimal punishment purpose applied with equal force to robbery armed crimes as to other crimes. The legislature could have amended the robbery armed statute to add as a proviso that there be a minimal punishment where the dangerous weapon was a firearm without offending the double jeopardy clause. Thus, there was no double jeopardy impediment to its attainment of the same goal by separate statute.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed defendant's convictions for robbery armed and possession of a firearm during commission of a felony.
