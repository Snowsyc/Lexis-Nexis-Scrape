PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] Appeal from Dallas County


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: The state requested a rehearing after the court set aside appellant's conviction for harassment pursuant to Tex. Penal Code § 42.07(a)(1) by the trial court, Dallas County (Texas).


OVERVIEW: When appellant's conviction for harassment pursuant to Tex. Penal Code Ann. § 42.07(a)(1) was set aside, state requested a rehearing. The court granted the rehearing, set aside the reversal and affirmed the conviction. It concluded that § 42.07(a)(1) required a communication via telephone or writing in a course and offensive manner with the intent to alarm. Appellant argued that § 42.07(a)(1) was unconstitutional because it was vague and overly broad in that the sender of the message could not anticipate whether or not the receiver would find the communication to be course and offensive. The communication in question involved purchasing baby caskets and was sent to parents of a six-day old baby. While the words were seemingly harmless, the court found that appellant clearly intended the writing to alarm the victims.


OUTCOME: The court granted state's rehearing, set aside the reversal, and reinstated appellant's conviction finding that the applicable statute required that a communication occur with another via telephone or writing in a course and offensive manner with the intent to alarm, and that the nature of appellant's letter, a letter about baby caskets when the victims' had a six-day old baby, was clearly intended to alarm them.
