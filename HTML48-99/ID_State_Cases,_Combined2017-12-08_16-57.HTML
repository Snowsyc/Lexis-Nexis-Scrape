CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendants, in a consolidated appeal, sought review of a judgment from the trial court (Idaho) convicting them of committing an infamous crime against nature, a violation of Idaho Code § 18-6605.

OVERVIEW: Defendant telephoned children and discussed AIDS, the means of its transmission, use of condoms, and other matters of a sexual nature. The court disagreed and affirmed defendant's conviction. The statute was not overbroad because it did not prohibit the mere expression of ideas or information. Telephone calls made with a legitimate intent to communicate were not criminalized. The statute prohibited only telephone contacts made with a specific and exclusive intent to annoy, terrify, threaten, intimidate, harass or offend. Defendant also argued that the statute was void for vagueness because it did not define words such as "obscene" and "indecent." The court disagreed. The vagueness doctrine did not require that every word used in a criminal statute be statutorily defined.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed defendant's conviction and sentence for violation of a state telephone harassment statute.
