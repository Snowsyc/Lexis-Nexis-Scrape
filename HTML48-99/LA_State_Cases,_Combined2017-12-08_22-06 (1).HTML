DISPOSITION: AFFIRMED.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant wife appealed from that portion of a judgment of the Civil District Court for the Parish of Orleans (Louisiana), which classified certain property of plaintiff husband as separate property.


OVERVIEW: After a hearing for the partition of property held after the husband and wife were divorced, the trial court found that two assets, a piece of real estate and an automobile, were the husband's separate property. The document of purchase of the piece of real estate contained a declaration by the husband that he was purchasing the property with his separate and paraphernal funds and that the property was to be his separate and paraphernal property. The wife acknowledged that declaration. The court affirmed the trial court determination that the property was the husband's separate property because the rule that a husband would not be heard to contradict his prior acknowledgment that property purchased by his wife was her separate property applied equally to a wife who acknowledged that property purchased by her husband was his separate property. The determination that the automobile was the husband's separate property was also affirmed because the husband had purchased the automobile when the couple were domiciled in Pennsylvania, which was not a community property state, and the law of Pennsylvania governed the classification of the vehicle.


OUTCOME: The judgment classifying certain property of the husband as separate property was affirmed.
