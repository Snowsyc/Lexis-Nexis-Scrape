STATE v. OLSEN

Court of Appeals of Ohio, Ninth Appellate District, Medina County


1987 Ohio App. LEXIS 5709



January 28, 1987, Decided


PRIOR HISTORY:  [*1] Appeal from Judgment entered in the Municipal Court of the County of Medina, Ohio Case No. 86 TRD 00236.



DISPOSITION: The judgment of conviction of the trial court is affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant sought review of an order of the Municipal Court of the County of Medina (Ohio), which convicted her on a charge of knowingly failing to obey a lawful order of a police officer at the scene of an emergency, a violation of Ohio Rev. Code Ann. § 2917.13.


OVERVIEW: While defendant was driving home from work she came upon an accident and a line of cars waiting to pass by the accident. Defendant crossed into the opposing lanes of traffic and drove around the line of cars. A police officer stopped her and instructed her to go back. Defendant refused, claiming that the road was slippery, and was later arrested. Defendant claimed that Ohio Rev. Code Ann. § 2917.13 was unconstitutionally vague and that her refusal was valid because her interest in safety outweighed the State's interest in controlling the accident scene. On appeal, the court affirmed, holding that there was nothing unlawful about the officer's order for defendant to move out of the wrong traffic lane and back into the correct lane of traffic after she proceeded in the wrong lane to avoid an accident scene. Further, defendant's subject fear about backing down to the line of cars did not make the officer's order objectively unlawful. Moreover, § 2917.13 was not vague and did not give law enforcement personnel unbridled discretion in controlling the scene of an accident. Finally, defendant's safety interest did not outweigh the officer's interest in controlling the accident scene.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed defendant's conviction for knowingly failing to obey a lawful order of a police officer at the scene of an emergency.

PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiffs, a taxpayer and board of education, appealed an order from a trial court of Montgomery County (Ohio), which found that it lacked jurisdiction to grant injunctive relief against defendants, county commissioners and housing officer, because the taxpayer and board of education had not exhausted their administrative remedies.

November 4, 1986, Decided


PRIOR HISTORY:  [*1] Civil Appeal from the Court of Common Pleas, Case No. 83CIV-172.



DISPOSITION: Reversed and Remanded.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant, a plaintiff in whose favor judgment was entered, sought review of a decision of the Court of Common Pleas of Delaware County (Ohio), which overruled his motion for an award of pre-judgment interest pursuant to Ohio Rev. Code Ann. § 1343.03(C).


OVERVIEW: After a lengthy history of litigation, a judgment was awarded in favor of the plaintiff, and he sought pre-judgment interest pursuant to Ohio Rev. Code Ann. § 1343.03(C). The trial court, pursuant to the statute, held the required hearing, found that the plaintiff had failed to make a good-faith effort to settle, also found that the defendant did not fail to make a good-faith effort, and denied the motion. On appeal, the court reversed, holding that failure to make a good-faith effort to settle did not require a showing of "bad faith." Rather, the term "good-faith effort to settle" meant an honest, purposeful effort, free of malice and the design to defraud or to seek an unconscionable advantage. A lack of good faith was not the equivalent of bad faith. The trial court's denial of the motion solely because it did not "see any bad faith," was contrary to law. The trial court, pursuant to § 1343.03(C), was minimally required, in determining the motion, to state with specificity its findings as to whether the defendant had not failed to make a good-faith effort and/or whether the plaintiff had failed to make a good-faith effort.


OUTCOME: The judgment denying the plaintiff's motion for pre-judgment interest was reversed and remanded.
