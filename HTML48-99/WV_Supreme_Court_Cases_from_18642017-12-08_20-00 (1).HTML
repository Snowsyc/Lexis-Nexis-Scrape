DISPOSITION:  [***1] Reversed and remanded with directions.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant taxpayer sought review of a decision of the Circuit Court of Kanawha County (West Virginia), which sustained an assessment by appellee state tax commissioner of privilege taxes under Code, 1931, 11-13-2 and 11-13-2h. The Pennsylvania taxpayer contended that the nature of its business operations in West Virginia was such that it was not subject to any part of the tax assessed against it.


OVERVIEW: The taxpayer's principal office and place of business was in Pennsylvania, and it was engaged in the business of furnishing managerial services and advice to coal mining companies. Its meager business in West Virginia was limited to providing services and advice to one West Virginia corporation. No deduction was made from the West Virginia corporation's state taxes on its gross receipts for the amount paid to the taxpayer under the managerial contract, and the taxpayer paid a Pennsylvania corporate income tax on its earnings under the managerial contract. The taxpayer maintained no office and owned no property in West Virginia. A relatively small portion of the West Virginia managerial services resulted from the physical presence of the taxpayer's representatives in West Virginia. Tax statutes were construed strictly against the taxing power and liberally in favor of the taxpayer. The court concluded that the taxpayer was not subject to the privilege tax on its meager business in West Virginia that was incidental to its Pennsylvania business and that the taxpayer was not engaged in a service business or calling "within this State" under Code, 1931, 11-13-2 and 11-13-2h.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the circuit court's judgment and remanded to the circuit court with directions to dismiss the proceeding pending therein.

CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiff in error accused appealed a judgment of the Circuit Court of Ohio County (West Virginia), which denied his petition for a writ of prohibition restraining defendant in error judge from proceeding with a murder trial against the accused.


OVERVIEW: The accused was charged with killing a woman on whom he performed an unlawful abortion in violation of Code, 1931, 61-2-8. He filed a plea in abatement alleging that a trial on his indictment in the county of the forum court would have constituted a violation of Article III, § 14 of the Constitution of West Virginia, which assured a defendant the right to be tried in the county where the offense was committed. Thus, he accompanied the agency to the county of the forum court, the point where the crime of murder became effectual. Thus, the offense charged in the indictment was committed in the county of the forum court.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the circuit court's judgment.
