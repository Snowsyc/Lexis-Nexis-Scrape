91 Ill. App. 2d 290; 234 N.E.2d 66; 1968 Ill. App. LEXIS 885



January 24, 1968 


PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] Appeal from the Circuit Court of LaSalle County; the Hon. WALTER DIXON, Judge, presiding.



DISPOSITION: Reversed and remanded with directions.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant claimants sought review of an order of the Circuit Court of LaSalle County (Illinois), which found in favor of appellee county in an escheat action.


OVERVIEW: An order was entered escheating the estate to the county and the order placed title to the real estate involved in the name of the county. Following the escheat order, numerous heirs appeared. The claimants traced their entitlement to the estate through the children of the decedent's aunt. The decedent's aunt never married and therefore the claimants claimed through the aunt's illegitimate children. The county argued that the statute in effect at the time the deceased passed away barred the claimants as the descendants of illegitimate heirs. The court concluded that: (1) the legislature in 1939 intended by the amendment to restate the probate act to provide for inheritance by representation by the illegitimate child and thereby intended merely to restate the law as it existed prior to 1939; (2) those claiming through illegitimate heirs should be allowed to inherit under the terms of the probate act enacted in 1939; and (3) the claimants were entitled to inherit through the first cousins of the decedent.


OUTCOME: The court reversed and remanded with directions the order in the escheat action.
