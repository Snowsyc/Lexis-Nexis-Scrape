CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Relators, a political party and an individual, sought through an application for a writ of mandamus to require respondents, State of Missouri and the Missouri Secretary of State, to review relators' nominating petition and to determine whether the petition contained a sufficient number of signatures of "qualified voters" to warrant inclusion on the ballot for the 1974 general election.


OVERVIEW: Relators filed the nominating petition in an attempt to comply with Mo. Rev. Stat. §§ 120.160, 120.200; the petition contained a sufficient number of signatures to comply with the statute if all of the signatures were valid. Respondents ruled that only 6,722 signatures were those of registered voters; 17,844 such signatures were necessary in order to comply with the statutory requirements. Respondents refused to certify the petition for inclusion of relators on the ballot, and the current action followed. Stat. §§ 120.160, 120.200 included a requirement for registration in addition to other constitutional and statutory qualifications.


OUTCOME: The court quashed relators' application for a writ of mandamus.

The defense contended at trial that in one jury instruction, that the trial judge by referring in several places to "a" mental disease or defect, limited the jury's findings to one of these and not to the combination which in fact constituted the causative defect. The court affirmed and stated that no ordinary intelligent jury would have construed the term as used so narrowly, but would have considered "a mental disease or defect" as embracing all the symptoms and classifications that might enter into the aggregate mental condition. The defense also contended that the trial judge should have instructed the jury that if defendant was acquitted because of mental incompetency, that he would not have been turned loose on the streets. The court held that the instruction had no legitimate bearing on any fact issue which the jury was required to decide and it would have diverted the jury from the real merits of the insanity issue by the introduction of this extraneous consideration.


OUTCOME: The judgment was affirmed.

Civil Appeal From Action for Equitable Relief

Judge James S. Rooney

Reversed and remanded


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiffs, property owners challenging defendant city's purported annexation of their land, appealed the judgment of the Circuit Court of Clay County (Missouri) that granted the city's motion to dismiss.


OVERVIEW: In response to the city's annexation an incorporated area, including land belonging to the property owners, the property owners instituted proceedings seeking a declaration that the annexation was null and void based upon the city's failure to comply with the provisions of the Sawyers Act, Mo. Rev. Stat. § 71.015 (1959). The property owners further sought to enjoin the city from exercising any jurisdiction over the area. The city filed a motion to dismiss, claiming that the Sawyers Act did not apply to it. The circuit court granted the motion, and the property owners appealed the dismissal of their lawsuit. In reversing, the court concluded that the Sawyers Act, by its very terms, was applicable to the city, and once the city announced its intention to annex an unincorporated area of land, the requirements of the Sawyers Act became mandatory and constituted a condition precedent to the authority to annex the unincorporated land. The purported annexation was not valid because the Sawyers Act precluded a city from proceeding beyond the expression of the intent to annex until it obtained a declaratory judgment.


OUTCOME: The judgment dismissing the property owners' suit was reversed, and the matter was remanded for further proceedings.
