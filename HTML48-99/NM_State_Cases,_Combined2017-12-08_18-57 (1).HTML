CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiff passenger sought review of a judgment of the District Court of Guadalupe County (New Mexico), which granted summary judgment in favor of defendant driver in the passenger's negligence action.


OVERVIEW: The passenger was a "guest" in an automobile owned by the driver, within the contemplation of N.M. Stat. Ann. § 64-24-1 (1953). While being so transported as such guest in the driver's automobile, a collision occurred between the driver's automobile and another automobile. The passenger suffered personal injuries as a proximate result of the accident and brought an action against the driver. The trial court granted summary judgment in favor of the driver and the passenger sought review. On appeal, the court upheld the judgment. The court ruled that § 64-24-1 was constitutional because the classification on the basis of ownership was clearly reasonable, and it could not be said to be devoid of reason and to be wholly arbitrary and capricious. The court rejected the passenger's argument that § 64-24-1 was unconstitutional and void in that it violated N.M. Const. art. II, § 18 and the Fourteenth Amendment. The court concluded that a statute would not be declared unconstitutional unless the court was satisfied beyond all reasonable doubt that the legislature went outside the constitution in enacting the challenged legislation.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment of the trial court.
