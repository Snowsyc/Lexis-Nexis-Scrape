October 20, 1999, Filed


PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] Appeal from the District Court of Burleigh County, South Central Judicial District, the Honorable Thomas J. Schneider, Judge.



DISPOSITION: AFFIRMED.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed her driving under the influence conviction, entered in the District Court of Burleigh County, South Central Judicial District (North Dakota).


OVERVIEW: Defendant was charged with driving under the influence. Defendant had failed to request a jury trial until the 28 day time period had expired, and was found guilty in a bench trial in municipal court. The district court denied defendant's motion for a jury trial and found her guilty. Defendant appealed from that judgment. The court concluded that N.D. Cent. Defendant should have requested a transfer to district court within 28 days of arraignment to get a jury trial, under N.D. Cent. Code § 40-18-15.1. In failing to do so, defendant waived her right to a jury trial.


OUTCOME: Conviction affirmed; defendant should have requested a transfer to district court within 28 days of arraignment to get a jury trial.

PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] Appeal from the District Court of Cass County, East Central Judicial District, the Honorable Lawrence A. Leclerc, Judge.



DISPOSITION: AFFIRMED.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Criminal defendant appealed a judgment from the District Court of Cass County, East Central Judicial District (North Dakota), which convicted him, upon a jury verdict, of driving under the influence of alcohol.


OVERVIEW: A criminal defendant was administered a preliminary breath test, arrested for driving under the influence, and taken to a hospital for a blood test. At a suppression hearing, plaintiff prosecution stated that it never raised breath test evidence at trial. Yet, during trial testimony was admitted that defendant had consented to such a test. Then, during deliberations the jury sent out a question asking whether a breath test was administered and for the results. Defendant was found guilty and appealed. The court held that since defendant conceded that the officer had probable cause to arrest him, the result of the breath test was inadmissible under N.D. Cent. Code § 39-20-14. Therefore, evidence that defendant consented to take such a test was irrelevant and improperly admitted. However, the erroneous admission was not prejudicial. Because defendant failed to submit a full transcript of the proceeding, including the trial court's response to the jury question, the court was unable to determine whether the irrelevant evidence changed the final result. Therefore, the judgment was affirmed.


OUTCOME: The lower court's judgment convicting defendant of driving under the influence of alcohol was affirmed. While the admission of the irrelevant evidence that defendant consented to a blood test was improper, the court was unable to determine from the record submitted whether that evidence changed the final result and the result of the blood test alone supported the jury's verdict.

Saari v. North Dakota Workers Compensation Bur.


No. 980342


SUPREME COURT OF NORTH DAKOTA


1999 ND 144; 598 N.W.2d 174; 1999 N.D. LEXIS 173



July 29, 1999, Filed


PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] Appeal from the District Court of Ramsey County, Northeast Judicial District, the Honorable Donovan Foughty, Judge.



DISPOSITION: REVERSED.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant, the North Dakota Workers' Compensation Bureau, sought review of a District Court of Ramsey County (North Dakota) decision that appellee, injured employee, should not have had his permanent partial injury (PPI) award calculated under N.D. Cent. Code § 65-05-12.2, which had been enacted in 1996. Court held appellee's rights had vested and §§ 65-05-12 and 13, statutes in effect on date of injury, should have been applied.


OVERVIEW: In 1987, appellee was injured while working for appellant employer and appellant North Dakota Workers' Compensation Bureau (Bureau) provided appellee with total disability benefits. In 1997, appellee was evaluated for a permanent partial injury award (PPI) and appellant Bureau, applying N.D. Cent. Code § 65-05-12.2, effective in 1996, determined his award. District court reversed, holding appellee's PPI award should have been calculated under §§ 65-05-12 and -13, statutes that had been in effect before 1996, because appellee's rights vested on the date of his injury. Appellate court reversed and reinstated appellant Bureau's determination. Legislature specifically provided the new law would apply to all PPI awards determined after effective date, regardless of the date of injury. Moreover, such a statutory enactment could operate retrospectively because any right to PPI benefits did not vest on the date of injury, but on the date the impairment was determined to be permanent and such awards were not contractual, but statutory. In addition, appellant Bureau properly evaluated award under American Medical Association Guides in effect on date of statutory enactments.


OUTCOME: Decision that appellee should not have had his permanent partial injury (PPI) award calculated under N.D. Cent. Code § 65-05-12.2 reversed. Statute applied to all PPI awards determined after effective date and operated retrospectively because rights to PPI benefits vested on date impairment determined to be permanent and such awards were not contractual. Also, appellant properly evaluated award under correct American Medical Association Guide.

PROCEDURAL POSTURE: The state appealed the decision from the District Court for Barnes County (North Dakota), which dismissed the state's criminal complaint against the defendant, a parish priest, for lack of subject matter jurisdiction. The trial court determined its exercise of jurisdiction over the prosecution would require excessive government entanglement in religious affairs in violation U.S. Const. amend. I and N.D. OVERVIEW: The state's complaint charged the defendant with knowingly taking and exercising control over money in excess of $ 100,000 belonging to the church and spending the money on personal matters. OUTCOME: The court reversed the trial court's dismissal of the criminal theft charges against the defendant for lack of subject matter jurisdiction. The court remanded for a trial on the merits.

No. 10,963


Supreme Court of North Dakota


371 N.W.2d 321; 1985 N.D. LEXIS 354



July 11, 1985 


PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] Appeal from the District Court of Stutsman County, Southeast Judicial District, the Honorable Robert L. Eckert, Judge.



DISPOSITION: REVERSED.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellants, a state historical society and a state historical board, sought review of a decision by the District Court of Stutsman County (North Dakota), which entered judgment in favor of appellee county and reversed the board's decision to list a county courthouse on a state historical sites registry.


OVERVIEW: After the board heard the nomination for and entered an order to place the courthouse on the historical registry, it informed the county that the courthouse had been so listed. The county then filed a notice of appeal from the order of the board. The trial court reversed the order of the board, concluding that the board had no authority to place the courthouse on the registry under N.D. Cent. Code § 55-10-02(4). On appeal, the court held that the legislature's failure to define the term historical value as used in § 55-10-02(4) did not deprive the board of the authority to add new sites to the registry. The court stated that if the board were deprived of such authority because of the omission of the definition of historical value, § 55-10-02(4) would be entirely surplusage and would have no effectiveness. The court stated that such a construction would be inconsistent with applicable rules of statutory construction. The court concluded that the authority to put historical sites on the registry was a proper delegation of power by the legislature, noting that the board was not given the authority to make law, but to execute a law enacted by the legislature.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the judgment of the trial court and affirmed the state historical board's decision to place the courthouse on the state historical sites registry.

CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant sought to vacate his convictions in the Stark County Court (North Dakota), for eight counts of issuing checks without sufficient funds in violation of N.D. Cent. Code § 6-08-16 (1981).


OVERVIEW: At an appearance before the trial court without counsel, defendant waived his right to a jury trial. Defendant failed to appear for trial and was arrested. After additional continuances at defendant's request, defendant and his court-appointed counsel appeared for trial but requested a jury trial. The trial court refused the request and found defendant guilty. At the trial, defendant's sole defense was his testimony that when issuing the eight checks he was under the impression there were sufficient funds deposited in his account. The court held that: (1) N.D. Cent. Code § 6-08-16 (1981) was not violative of the equal protection clause of U.S. Const. amend. XIV or N.D. Const. art. OUTCOME: The court affirmed the convictions.
