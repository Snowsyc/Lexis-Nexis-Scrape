Guillot v. Nunez


No. 41596


Supreme Court of Louisiana


225 La. 301; 72 So. 2d 513; 1954 La. LEXIS 1215



March 22, 1954 


SUBSEQUENT HISTORY:  [***1] Rehearing Denied April 26, 1954.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellants, Clerk of Court of St. Bernard Parish, Louisiana, and the Superintendent of the Division of the State Police, challenged the preliminary injunction issued by the trial court (Louisiana), which restrained the Clerk from destroying appellee owner's slot machines or delivering them to the Superintendent on the basis that 1928 La. Acts 231 was repealed by 1948 La. Acts 6, and that the former effected an unconstitutional taking of property.


OVERVIEW: On appeal, the court pointed out that the taxpayer's contention that 1948 La. Acts 6 repeals by implication 1928 La. Acts 231 was a matter of no consequence in the ultimate determination of the case for the reason that both acts were expressly repealed, and the substance of those acts was incorporated as part of the Revised Code of laws as La. Rev. Stat. Ann. § 47:375 and La. Rev. Stat. Ann. § 15:26.1, respectively. When these provisions were embodied in the Revised Statutes, they stood on a parity with each other and, this being so, it was impossible to hold that one provision had the effect of repealing the other. Further, the design of the Louisiana State Legislature was explicit; by levying a tax on the illegal business of operating gambling slot machines, it was not intended to lift those devices from the category of contraband to the status of property capable of private ownership. Because there was no status of ownership recognized by law in the illegal gambling machines, there was no merit to the contention that by ordering the summary destruction of slot machines, § 15:26.1 effected an unconstitutional taking of the owner's property without due process of law.


OUTCOME: The court annulled and reversed the trial court's judgment, and the owner's suit was dismissed at his costs. The court further ordered the Clerk to deliver to the Superintendent, his agents and employees, the slot machines in his possession for destruction in accordance with the laws of the state.
