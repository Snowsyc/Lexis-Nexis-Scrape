CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Petitioner owner of a bar sought review of the decision of the District Court of Washoe County (Nevada), which denied him a writ of mandate after respondents, a city and its council members (council), denied him licenses to conduct gambling and dispense liquor on the premises even though the location was zoned for such activities.


OVERVIEW: The city council adopted a resolution limiting the maximum number of bar licenses on each city ward without announcing its policy. The owner alleged that the council could not deny licenses upon considerations other than the city's ordinances, and that the action was arbitrary, discriminatory, and confiscatory. The court denied the writ and found that the owner's investments in anticipation of the licenses were not confiscatory because the owner knew the risks and hazards of regulation.


OUTCOME: The court denied the owner's petition for writ of mandate against the city and its council members.

OUTCOME: The court denied respondent's motion to dismiss the appeal.

The issue was whether the defaults were properly entered and whether they required respondent court to exercise a judicial power different from that of its power to relieve of default. Although questioning the propriety of the action of the court clerk in erasing the entries of default as a usurpation of judicial authority, the court held that respondent court had not exceeded its jurisdiction. On petition for writ of certiorari, the test was whether jurisdictional requisites had been met, Nev. Comp. Laws § 9231 (1929), and none were shown to be lacking. If the order granting the manufacturers' motion to set aside default was error, the court could not review it on the merits on a writ of certiorari.


OUTCOME: The court granted the motion of the manufacturers and respondent court to vacate and set aside the writ of certiorari to review the removal of the entry of default against the manufacturers in the action of the injured parties.

King v. Board of Regents


No. 3532


Supreme Court of Nevada


65 Nev. 533; 200 P.2d 221; 1948 Nev. LEXIS 72



November 17, 1948 


PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] Appeal from Third Judicial District Court, Lander County; W. R. Reynolds, Judge.



DISPOSITION: Judgment reversed and case remanded with instructions.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiff taxpayer appealed an order of the Third Judicial District Court for Lander County (Nevada), which dismissed the taxpayer's action to enjoin defendant board of regents of the state university (elected board) from nominating an advisory board pursuant to Nev. Stat. § 68 (1947) to counsel it. The taxpayer claimed that § 68 violated constitutional provisions vesting control of the state university (university) in the elected board.


OVERVIEW: The state constitution provided for the creation of the elected board to control and manage the affairs of the university, under such regulations as might be provided by law. Section § 68 was later enacted to create an advisory board of regents (advisory board) to counsel the elected board. The taxpayer brought an action seeking to enjoin the elected board from nominating the advisory board, claiming that § 68 violated constitutional provisions vesting control of the university in the elected board. The district court dismissed the action and dissolved a temporary restraining order, and the taxpayer appealed. The court held that § 68 was unconstitutional, because it altered the constitutional powers and functions of the elected board. Although the advisory board did not have a formal vote on issues, it was allowed to participate in decisions as to curriculum, functions and salary of the faculty, and other issues, with all of the rights and privileges of the elected board. Section 68 was not merely an exercise of the constitutional power to define the duties of the elected board, because it created another entity with all the rights of the elected board except voting rights.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the order dismissing the action by the taxpayer and dissolving the temporary restraining order. The court remanded the action to the district court, with instructions to overrule the elected board's demurrer and to issue a permanent injunction to prevent nomination of the advisory board by the elected board.

Kress v. Corey

January 12, 1948 


PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] Appeal from Eighth Judicial District Court, Clark County; George E. Marshall, Judge.



DISPOSITION: Reversed and case remanded with instructions.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiff tenant appealed an order and judgment of the Eighth Judicial District Court, Clark County (Nevada) dismissing his complaint for a declaratory judgment, pursuant to the Uniform Declaratory Judgments Act, of the validity of a contract. The district court sustained the demurrers of defendants, lessors and assignees of the original lessee. The tenant had agreed to buy a cafe from the assignees and take over their unexpired lease.


OVERVIEW: The lessors claimed the lease could not be assigned without their consent. After serving a notice of termination upon the assignees, the lessor served a notice to quit upon the tenant. The district court enjoined defendants from interfering with the tenant's occupation. It later sustained defendants' demurrers to the tenant's amended complaint and dismissed the action, thus dissolving the temporary injunction. In its order, the district court released funds that the tenant had deposited under the prior order and denied his application for a writ of supersedeas. On appeal, the court noted that the case was the first under the Act. The court reversed, holding that the conditions for declaratory relief had been met. The controversy between the tenant and the assignees and the controversy between the tenant and the lessors both operated upon the right to possession. Finding that the lessors' notice to quit was considered a threat, the court stated that the trend was to extend the benefit of a declaratory judgment to the interests of parties that were challenged even before a cause of action accrued. Further, injunctive relief could be coupled with a prayer for a declaratory judgment.


OUTCOME: Because there was no appeal from an order sustaining a demurrer, the purported appeal from the order sustaining the demurrers was dismissed. The court reversed, with costs, the part of the order and judgment appealed from that purported to dismiss the action. The court remanded the case with instructions to overrule the demurrers and to permit defendants to answer, subject to the right in the tenant to further to amend his petition if desired.
