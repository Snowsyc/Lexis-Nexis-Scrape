CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiff Planning and Zoning Commission of the Town of Middlebury (Connecticut) filed an action against defendants, Zoning Board of Appeals of the Town of Middlebury (Connecticut) and the applicant, in which the Commission alleged that the Board exceeded its authority in granting the applicant's request for set back variances.


OVERVIEW: The Board alleged that the Commission was not aggrieved under Conn. Gen. Stat. § 8-8(b). The court dismissed the Commission's appeal, finding that the Commission lacked standing and that a change in the zoning laws many years after the applicant acquired the realty, a possible partial taking, and financial problems justified granting the variances. The court concluded that a statutory right of aggrievement was not established because Middlebury, Conn., Zoning Regs. § 72, p. 72-1 placed the enforcement responsibility solely with the zoning enforcement officer and that the court could not have enlarged the legislative classification of parties with automatic standing. The court also reasoned that the Commission was not classically aggrieved because Middlebury, Conn., Zoning Regulations § 71, p. 71-1entrusted the Board with deciding applications for variances. The court further found that the later zoning change as to the set back lines made developing the realty in compliance with the zoning ordinance impossible, that the applicant conceded that her financial need alone did not entitle her to the variances, and that any taking exacerbated the preexisting hardship.


OUTCOME: The court dismissed the Commission's appeal of the Board's grant of the applicant's request for set back variances.

PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendants, Ellington Planning and Zoning Commission and others, filed a motion to dismiss for lack of subject matter jurisdiction and mootness an appeal by plaintiff applicant, which challenged the Commission's grant of a special permit to the applicant for the operation of a solid waste fill in the town subject to conditions that the applicant alleged were illegal, arbitrary, and an abuse of the Commission's discretion.


OVERVIEW: The Commission granted the applicant an extension of a one-year special permit to operate a solid waste land fill, which had been previously granted pursuant to Ellington, Conn., Zoning Reg. § 7.7(f), subject to 32 conditions. Before the special permit expired by its terms, the applicant sought another extension of the special permit. The Commission granted the application subject to the same conditions. The applicant then filed an appeal challenging the imposition of the conditions on the first extension of the special permit. OUTCOME: The court denied defendants' motion to dismiss the applicant's appeal from the conditions the Commission placed on a special permit.
