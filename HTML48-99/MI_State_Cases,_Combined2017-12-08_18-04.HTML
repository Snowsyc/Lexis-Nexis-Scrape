OVERVIEW: Defendant was a passenger in an automobile stopped for a headlight violation. The officers conducted a search of the vehicle and discovered defendant's pistol, which he had no permit to carry. The court held that the search was unreasonable, even if the issuance of a citation to the driver could be considered an arrest. The search was not incident to a lawful arrest where it was not designed to reach the fruits of a crime, the means by which a crime was committed, or instruments calculated to effect escape from custody. The court held, however, that the evidence of defendant's weapon was admissible under Mich. Const. art. 2, § 10, which abolished the exclusionary rule with respect to firearms. The court observed that the language of the Fourth Amendment, U.S. Const. amend. IV, did not directly bear upon the administration or exclusion of evidence; hence, the question of admissibility of evidence was not within the concept of ordered liberty protected by the due process clause, U.S. Const. amend. XIV. The court held that evidence of a weapon, even if obtained in an illegal search, was not subject to suppression.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the suppression order.
