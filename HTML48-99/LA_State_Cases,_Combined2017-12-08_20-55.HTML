256 So. 2d 808; 1972 La. App. LEXIS 6928



January 10, 1972 


SUBSEQUENT HISTORY:  [**1] Rehearing Denied February 7, 1972. Writ Granted March 15, 1972.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant judgment creditor sought review of an order of the trial court (Louisiana) that refused to apply a new provision of La. Rev. Stat. Ann. § 13:3881 to a garnishment judgment rendered against appellee debtor rendered prior to the act for payment due on merchandise purchased from appellant.


OVERVIEW: Appellee debtor failed to pay for merchandise purchased from appellant judgment creditor. The trial court issued a garnishment judgment against appellee under the then existing statute, La. Rev. Stat. Ann. § 13:3881, as amended in 1960, which allowed for an exemption of 80 percent of appellee's wages from seizure. Appellant stopped receiving any of appellee's wages after the passage of Act 242 of 1970, which amended § 13:3881 and altered the amount exempt from garnishment. The trial court refused to apply the new provision and the court affirmed. Appellant showed that it had a vested right to seize appellee's salary prior to the passage of the Act and that its retrospective application adversely affected this right. A statute depriving a judgment creditor of the right to seize a portion of a debtor's salary by reason of a change in the amount of the exemption was a deprivation of a vested right. Once judgment of garnishment was rendered, appellant had a vested right to the non-exempt portion of appellee's salary, including unearned future wages and earned wages, and it could not permit the application of a statute which would destroy that right.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the order of the trial court that refused to apply a new provision of an act, which amended a statute that existed at the time that appellant judgment creditor obtained a garnishment judgment against appellee debtor. Appellant had a vested right to the non-exempt portion of appellee's salary and the retroactive application of a statute that deprived appellant of that right was not permissible.
