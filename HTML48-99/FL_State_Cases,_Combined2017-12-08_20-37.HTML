CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: The relator filed a suggestion for the issuance of a rule nisi in prohibition against respondent to prohibit respondent from further prosecution of relator after he was charged with grand larceny by the trial court (Florida). Respondent contended that he was immunized from prosecution pursuant to the state's immunity statute, Fla. Stat. ch. 932.29.


OVERVIEW: Relator was a twenty-year city councilman, when a grand jury was convened to investigate certain business irregularities by city officials. Relator was subpoenaed to appear before the grand jury. After relator was brought before the grand jury, the states attorney asked if he was willing to waive his immunity; whereupon, relator executed a written waiver of immunity. Relator filed a suggestion, claiming that he was entitled to immunity pursuant to Fla. Stat. ch. 932.29, and that his waiver of immunity was a nullity because it was executed without benefit of the Miranda warnings. Denying the suggestion for issuance of a rule nisi, the court found that there was no contention by relator that the grand jury or its agents had in any way coerced his waiver. The court held that Miranda was applicable to interrogations in the isolated setting of the police station and not those occurring in courts or other official investigations where a court reporter and a broad representative group of citizens were present to serve as impartial observers to guard against intimidation or trickery.


OUTCOME: The court denied relator relator's suggestion for issuance of a rule nisi because relator's waiver of immunity did not require that Miranda warnings be given because the interrogation was not in a police setting.

OUTCOME: The court reversed the trial court judgment that denied defendant's motion to dismiss because charter provisions only explained right of municipality to sue and be sued generally and municipal corporation such as defendant city must be sued in the county in which it was located.
