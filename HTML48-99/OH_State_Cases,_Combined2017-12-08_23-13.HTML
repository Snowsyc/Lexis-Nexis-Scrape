PRIOR HISTORY:  [*1] CHARACTER OF PROCEEDING: Civil appeal from Court of Common Pleas. Domestic Relations Division. Case No. D-198532



DISPOSITION: JUDGMENT: MODIFIED, AND AFFIRMED AS MODIFIED.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant husband challenged a judgment of the Cuyahoga County Court of Common Pleas, Domestic Relations Division (Ohio), which was entered in favor of plaintiff wife in an action for divorce wherein the wife was awarded the marital home, spousal support, and additional alimony for attorney fees, and the husband was awarded his pension from his employer.


OVERVIEW: The husband argued that the trial court erred in its division of property between the parties, which resulted in an inequitable distribution of assets. He also argued that the trial court erred in ordering him to pay medical expenses incurred by their son after the son reached adulthood and erred in its calculations of the value of his pension, which resulted in its calculations of excessive marital assets. On appeal, the court held that the wife had only the portion of her inheritance from her stepfather that she used to purchase and pay for the marital home and that was directly traceable to the marital home as separate property. However, the court noted that it did not find the trial court's overvaluation of her separate property to be prejudicial in light of the court's equitable distribution of the assets. Further, the court held that the trial court erred in ordering the husband medical expenses for the son because the trial court's conclusion that this expense was incurred prior to their son's emancipation lacked support. Thus, that portion of the trial court's order was vacated. Finally, the pension was properly valued as a marital asset.


OUTCOME: The court modified the trial court's judgment by vacating the provision that ordered husband to pay for the son's medical expenses and affirmed the judgment as modified.

1993 Ohio App. LEXIS 1251

PRIOR HISTORY:  [*1] CHARACTER OF PROCEEDING: Criminal appeal from Court of Common Pleas. Case No. 265125



DISPOSITION: JUDGMENT: Affirmed


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed from a judgment of the Cuyahoga County Court of Common Pleas (Ohio), which convicted him for felonious assault under Ohio Rev. Code Ann. § 2903.11, with a firearm specification and two aggravated felony specifications. The jury found him not guilty of possession of criminal tools.


OVERVIEW: Defendant challenged his conviction, arguing that his prior convictions were improperly revealed. However, the court found that defendant failed to object to the evidence. Furthermore, defendant opened the door on some of the information. Defendant was not denied due process because the case was allegedly rushed to trial. Moreover, the trial court did not err in charging the jury that it was not necessary to prove that defendant used a deadly weapon at trial. The trial court committed harmless error in its failure to instruct the jury as to the statutory definition of a firearm. Defendant waived the issue when he did not object at trial. The overwhelming evidence of the firearm rendered the court's failure to give the statutory definition harmless error. Finally, defendant was not denied due process when the court would not instruct upon the lesser included offense of assault. The evidence supporting defendant's conviction for felonious assault was too solid to support an acquittal of that crime. Because the evidence presented would not support an acquittal, the trial judge was not required to instruct the jury on the lesser included offense.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment. The court ordered that defendant pay costs. The court remanded the case to the trial court for execution of sentence.

CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed from a judgment of the Cleveland Municipal Court (Ohio), which convicted him of soliciting in violation of Cleveland, Ohio, Codified Ordinance § 619.09. He was sentenced with a fine of $ 1,000 and a term of 180 days. The trial court suspended $ 500 of the fine and 120 days of incarceration and placed defendant on two years' probation, with conditions. The trial court granted a stay of execution of sentence pending his appeal.


OVERVIEW: Defendant was convicted for soliciting an undercover police officer stationed in an area known for prostitution. He offered her $ 40 and told her he wanted to "get laid." Another officer witnessed the event prior to defendant's arrest. The trial court denied his motion to suppress statements to the officers and his motion for acquittal. On appeal, he argued that his statements should have been suppressed based on entrapment. The court affirmed, rejecting defendant's arguments based on the entrapment defense. The court found ample evidence of defendant's predisposition to commit soliciting, which showed that he failed to establish entrapment. The trial court thus did not err in failing to give an entrapment instruction to the jury. The court also held that the trial court did not err in denying the motion to suppress, finding unpersuasive defendant's argument that affording him an opportunity to commit the crime violated his Fourth Amendment rights. Nor did the trial court abuse its discretion in admitting evidence or in refusing to recommend defendant for a pretrial diversion program. Finally, the sentence was within the statutory guidelines and was not an abuse of discretion.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed defendant's conviction for one count of soliciting in violation of the municipal ordinance, and terminated any bail pending appeal. The court remanded the case to the trial court for execution of sentence.

OVERVIEW: At trial five people testified that defendant offered them money to burn his house, and his cell mate testified that defendant had told him that he had paid someone to set the fires and offered him payment to "take care of" the people who would testify against him. OUTCOME: The court reversed and remanded for further proceedings.

February 10, 1993, Decided


PRIOR HISTORY:  [*1] APPEAL FROM JUDGMENT ENTERED IN THE COMMON PLEAS COURT. COUNTY OF SUMMIT, OHIO. CASE NO. CV88-8-2737


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiffs, a restaurant patron and his spouse, filed a suit for personal injuries against defendant restaurant owner for back injuries sustained by the patron when a chair at the restaurant collapsed and caused him to fall. The Common Pleas Court of Summit County (Ohio) entered judgment for plaintiffs and offset the award pursuant to Ohio Rev. Code Ann. § 2317.45 with any collateral benefits received by the patron. The owner appealed.


OVERVIEW: The patron, a sales representative, was taking a business client to lunch when he was injured. Although the trial court originally held a hearing to determine the amount of collateral workers' compensation benefits the patron received, it held that § 2317.45 was unconstitutional. This ruling was reversed on appeal. On remand, the trial court held a second hearing to determine the amount of collateral benefits. The restaurant owner contended in part that it was error to hold this second hearing. The court disagreed, but held that the trial court erred in deducting attorney fees from the gross amount of collateral benefits. Under § 2317.45(B)(2)(i), the trial court was required to determine, as best as possible, the amount of collateral benefits actually received and "reasonably certain" to be received by the patron over the next 60 months. The court found no abuse of discretion in the trial court's decision to take additional evidence to insure the accuracy of these calculations. Further, the court held that § 2317.45(B)(2)(c)(ii) did not authorize the trial court to deduct attorney fees from collateral benefits. The term "costs" generally did not include attorney fees.


OUTCOME: The judgment of the trial court deducting attorney fees from the amount of the patron's collateral benefits was reversed. In all other respects the judgment was affirmed. The case was then remanded with instructions.

Code Ann. § 45174.50(A), thus depriving the franchisee of its due process rights.

OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment.

PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant attorney general appealed from the judgment of the Court of Common Pleas of Richland County (Ohio) declaring sua sponte that Ohio Rev. Code Ann. § 311.01 was unconstitutional in plaintiff candidate's declaratory judgment action to determine whether he satisfied the qualifications for his candidacy for county sheriff under § 311.01.


OVERVIEW: The candidate filed a declaratory judgment action against the state attorney general to determine whether he satisfied the qualifications of Ohio Rev.
