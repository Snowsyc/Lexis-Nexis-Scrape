PROCEDURAL POSTURE: The state appealed from an order of the Superior Court of New Jersey, which suppressed evidence of the results of a breathalyzer test given to defendant, who was charged with driving while under the influence of intoxicating liquor. The state contended that defendant's test should not have been suppressed for his failure to consent, as no search and seizure was involved.


OVERVIEW: Defendant was taken to a police station for driving while under the influence of intoxicating liquor, and was given a breathalyzer test that showed a positive finding of alcohol. Defendant filed a motion to suppress the results of the test pursuant to R.R. 3:2A-6(a). The court affirmed the municipal court's determination that the test should be suppressed due to lack of consent.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the order of the court below, holding that defendant's breathalyzer results were properly suppressed, as defendant had not consented to the test as required by statute.
