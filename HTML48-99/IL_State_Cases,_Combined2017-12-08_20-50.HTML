PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] APPEAL from the Circuit Court of Cook County; the Hon. F. EMMETT MORRISSEY, Judge, presiding.



DISPOSITION: Reversed and remanded.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiffs, a contract purchaser, a developer, and property owners, sought review of the decision from the Circuit Court of Cook County (Illinois), which granted judgment in favor of defendant county and intervenors, an adjacent property owner and two school districts, in a declaratory judgment action.


OVERVIEW: Plaintiffs filed an application with the county's zoning board of appeals to have a tract of land reclassified from a single family district to a general commercial district and to obtain a special use for a mobile home park, a sewage plant, and a community well. The board denied plaintiffs' request. Plaintiffs filed a declaratory judgment action that sought to have the single family zoning classification declared invalid and unenforceable. The trial court granted judgment in favor of the intervenors and the county. On appeal, the court held that plaintiffs had met their burden of proving by clear and convincing evidence that the zoning ordinance, as applied to the property, was invalid. Therefore, the trial court's decision was against the manifest weight of the evidence. The court found that, under the totality of the evidence, plaintiffs sufficiently established that the property was not economically suitable for development as it was zoned and that the highest and best use of the property was not the development of single family residences. The court concluded that the hardship to plaintiffs did not outweigh nor justify the unreasonable and arbitrary restrictions.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the trial court's judgment and remanded the case for further proceedings.

People v. Freeman


No. 58938


Appellate Court of Illinois, First District, Fourth Division


26 Ill. App. 3d 443; 326 N.E.2d 207; 1974 Ill. App. LEXIS 1614



January 16, 1974, Filed


SUBSEQUENT HISTORY:  [***1] Rehearing denied March 17, 1975.


PRIOR HISTORY: APPEAL from the Circuit Court of Cook County; the Hon. MARVIN ASPEN, Judge, presiding.



DISPOSITION: Judgment affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant sought review of the judgment of the Circuit Court of Cook County (Illinois), which dismissed his petition for a new trial based upon newly discovered evidence.


OVERVIEW: Defendant was convicted of armed robbery. Defendant filed a petition pursuant to Ill. Civ. Prac. Act § 72, Ill. Rev. Stat. ch. 110, par. 72 (1971), for a new trial on the basis of newly discovered evidence. Defendant provided the trial court with affidavits from individuals stating that two other people had confessed to them that they had committed the offense for which defendant was convicted. One of the alleged culprits testified that the affidavits were not true. The trial court dismissed the petition for a new trial. On appeal, the court affirmed finding that a petition pursuant to Ill. Civ. Prac. Act § 72 did not afford a means of relief on the basis of newly discovered evidence. The court rejected defendant's arguments that the rule prohibiting a new trial on the basis of newly discovered evidence was unconstitutional because although § 72 did not provide for a new trial, other means of obtaining the same relief were available.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed.
