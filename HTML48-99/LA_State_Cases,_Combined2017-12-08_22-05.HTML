373 So. 2d 170; 1979 La. LEXIS 6876



June 25, 1979 


PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] Appeal from the Fourth Judicial District Court, Parish of Ouachita, Louisiana. Honorable William Norris, III, Judge.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant sought review of the judgment of the Fourth Judicial District Court, Parish of Ouachita, Louisiana, which convicted him of possession of a controlled dangerous substance (marijuana) with intent to distribute, in violation of La. Rev. Stat. Ann. § 40:966, sentenced him to a suspended term of six years at hard labor and placed him on supervised probation conditioned on the service of one year in the parish jail.


OVERVIEW: Defendant arrived home with a suitcase to find the police executing a search warrant of his house and vehicle. He was later arrested and charged with drug possession when the suitcase yielded a large quantity of marijuana. On appeal of his conviction, defendant argued that the trial court erred at the suppression hearing when it denied him the opportunity to test the veracity of the informants and the affiant on the probable cause affidavit and that the search of the suitcase was illegal. In affirming in part, the court held that the trial court erred in refusing defendant's testimony that he was out of the state at a time when the informant alleged that he observed defendant in his home. The court ruled that the informant's credibility was not at issue but that the affiant's credibility could be traversed by convincing allegations of fact which, if proven, would have established the falsity of the affidavit, whereupon the issue was whether the informant lied and the affiant reasonably believed him or whether the affiant lied. With regard to the suitcase, the court held that when defendant left the suitcase in the home, it became subject to the house search under the warrant.


OUTCOME: The court remanded to the trial court for a rehearing on defendant's motion to suppress, reserved to defendant the right to appeal an adverse ruling on the motion, and affirmed the conviction and sentence in lieu of such further appeal.

OVERVIEW: On the date of the offense, the first degree murder statute provided that whoever committed the offense must be punished by death.
