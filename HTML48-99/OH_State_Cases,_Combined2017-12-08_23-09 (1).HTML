PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellants, an organization and a landowner, challenged a judgment from the Lucas County Court of Common Pleas (Ohio), which, in their administrative appeal, granted summary judgment for appellee city and affirmed the city council's grant of a special use permit to a church, and, in their action for writ of mandamus, a declaratory judgment, and injunctive relief, granted summary judgment to appellees, the city and its planning commission.


OVERVIEW: A church sought a special use permit for a building for an emergency shelter for homeless women. The commission staff opined that the location did not conform to the group home requirement and exceeded the quota for group homes and similar facilities, and the commission approved the staff's recommendation granting a permit for an institution and waiving the five-acre site requirement of Toledo, Ohio, Municipal Code § 1167.01(b)(7). The city council then passed an ordinance granting the permit and waiving the requirement. The organization and the landowner filed an administrative appeal against the city and an action against the city and the commission for a writ of mandamus and injunctive relief. On appeal from summary judgments for the city and the commission, the court held that: (1) the organization did not have standing to file an administrative appeal on behalf of its members, who were not directly affected by the decision; (2) the city council could only grant a special use permit subject to the requirements of § 1167.01(b)(7), and it could not waive the five-acre or any of the other requirements thereunder; and (3) hence, the ordinance granting the waiver was illegal.


OUTCOME: The court reversed trial court's judgment, which had granted summary judgments in favor of the city in the administrative appeal by the organization and the landowner, and in favor of the city and the planning commission in the original action for writ of mandamus, a declaratory judgment, and injunctive relief by the organization and the landowner.
