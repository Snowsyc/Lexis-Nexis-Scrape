January 5, 1977 



DISPOSITION:  [**1] AFFIRMED.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant was convicted in the Circuit Court of Lowndes County (Mississippi) for murder, and he appealed.


OVERVIEW: The evidence was to the effect that defendant's girlfriend had asked a man to give her and a friend a ride home from a cafe. While driving them home, defendant drove up behind the man while he was stopped at a light. The girlfriend asked the man to pull over so that she could talk to defendant. Defendant got out of his car and walked over to the man's car. Defendant had a gun, and he shot the man in the head, killing him. The women testified that the man was unarmed. Defendant pleaded self-defense. He testified that the man had slid across the car seat and was coming up with a gun. On appeal, defendant argued that the trial court erred in granting the State's request for a jury instruction on manslaughter because the instruction improperly shifted the burden of proof to defendant. The court found that on the evidence it was proper for the trial court to submit to the jury instructions defining both murder and manslaughter, which was done. The definition of manslaughter in the criticized instruction was neither incorrect nor misleading and stated with sufficient clarity the burden of proof that must be met by the State in order to justify conviction.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed defendant's conviction for murder.
