PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant sought review of his conviction by the Ashtabula County Court of Common Pleas (Ohio) on the State's charge that he violated Ohio Rev. Code Ann. § 959.15, the Ohio animal fights statute.


OVERVIEW: Defendant was convicted of violating the Ohio animal fights statute, Ohio Rev. Code Ann. § 959.15. He sought review of the conviction, arguing that the statute was unconstitutionally vague and overbroad. The court upheld that conviction. The court ruled that although one portion of the statute was overly broad, that portion was severable from the remainder. OUTCOME: The court affirmed defendant's conviction for having knowingly witnessed an animal fight in violation of Ohio law. The court reversed the trial court's judgment to the extent that it found that a portion of the statute was not unconstitutional.
