PRIOR HISTORY: APPEAL from a judgment of the circuit court for Kenosha county: JOHN E. MALLOY, Judge. Affirmed. 



DISPOSITION: By the Court. --Judgment affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant pro se appealed a judgment from the Circuit Court for Kenosha County (Wisconsin), convicting defendant of misdemeanor selling without a seller's permit contrary to Wis. Stat. § 77.52(12).


OVERVIEW: Defendant, a merchant who had a furniture cleaning business, lost his seller's permit after failing to pay a retail sales tax imposed under Wis. Stat. § 77.52(2). Defendant challenged his conviction for selling without a seller's permit on the ground that the statute was unconstitutional because it imposed a tax on certain services while others were exempt. The court affirmed defendant's conviction. Where it was alleged that a statutory classification violated the Equal Protection Clause, it was required to be proved beyond a reasonable doubt that the legislature had abused its discretion. Because the statute involved a tax measure, the presumption of constitutionality was strong. The legislature had broad authority to determine classifications and exemptions, and its decision was sustainable if substantial differences existed between the occupations or services separately classified. The court concluded that Wis. Stat. § 77.52(2) was a tax that was based on rendering of services and was not tied to the incidental transfer of personal property by a serviceman. A rational basis existed for exempting certain professional services, such as those provided by doctors and lawyers.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed.
