No. 41876


Supreme Court of Georgia


254 Ga. 335; 329 S.E.2d 136; 1985 Ga. LEXIS 692; 49 A.L.R.4th 311



April 30, 1985, Decided


SUBSEQUENT HISTORY:  [***1] Rehearing Denied May 21, 1985.


PRIOR HISTORY: Certiorari to the Court of Appeals of Georgia -- 173 Ga. App. 62.



DISPOSITION: Judgment affirmed in part; reversed in part.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant insured challenged a judgment from the Court of Appeals of Georgia, which found in favor of appellee uninsured driver and held that an automobile insurance policy provision excluding liability coverage while an insured was "attempting to avoid apprehension or arrest" was unenforceable under the Motor Vehicle Accident Reparations ("no-fault") Act (MVAR), Ga. Code Ann. § 33-34-1 et seq.


OVERVIEW: The court granted certiorari to decide whether an exclusion in the insurer's automobile insurance policy, which excluded liability coverage while an insured was "attempting to avoid apprehension or arrest," was unenforceable as a matter of public policy in view of Ga. Code Ann. § 33-34-1. The court held that the exclusion was directly contrary to the public interest of accident victims to have access to insurance funds to satisfy their judgments and that the MVAR was enacted to serve that very interest. The court held that unless a passenger in a fleeing vehicle could demonstrate to the trier of fact by a preponderance of the evidence that he did not encourage or participate in the attempt to avoid apprehension or arrest, the exclusion was enforceable as to the passenger.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed in part and reversed in part. The court held that the exclusion was unenforceable on the ground of public policy as to the deceased who was merely driving when another driver who was fleeing the police struck him. However the court held that the exclusion was unenforceable only to the extent of insurance required by the MVAR. The public policy would not protect passengers who were willing participants in the fleeing vehicle.

DISPOSITION: Judgment affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed his conviction for aggravated assault by the Bibb Superior Court (Georgia). Defendant challenged the constitutionality of Ga. Code Ann. § 16-5-21(a)(2), which provided a person commits the offense of aggravated assault when he assaults with a deadly weapon or with any object, device, or instrument which, when used offensively against a person, is likely to or actually does result in serious bodily injury.


OVERVIEW: Defendant was convicted of aggravated assault after he struck a friend in the face during a fight at a party. Defendant challenged his conviction on two grounds. First, he should have been charged only with simple battery, under Ga. Code Ann. § 16-5-23. The court therefore held that defendant's contentions were without merit and affirmed the conviction.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment of the trial court which had convicted the defendant of aggravated assault.

September 23, 1980, Decided


PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] Bribery; constitutional question. Clayton Superior Court. Before Judge Ison.



DISPOSITION: Judgment reversed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant sought review of a judgment by the Clayton Superior Court (Georgia) that convicted him of bribery based on proof that he had received money from a bar proprietor to allow the bar to remain open beyond the time that it was required to be open by the city ordinance of the town for which defendant was the mayor.


OVERVIEW: Defendant claimed that his indictment did not allege an offense, Ga. Code Ann. § 26-2301, which established the crime of bribery, was unconstitutionally vague, and that the trial judge should have disqualified himself, because he was the district attorney when the investigation was conducted that resulted in defendant's indictment. The court reversed defendant's conviction for bribery and held that the indictment sufficiently notified defendant of the charge against him to meet constitutional requirements of notice, but that the trial judge, who was the district attorney when the investigation was conducted that resulted in defendant's charge, was disqualified to try the case against defendant.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the conviction of defendant of bribery.
