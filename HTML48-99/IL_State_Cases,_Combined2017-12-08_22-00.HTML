No. 81-349


Appellate Court of Illinois, Third District


103 Ill. App. 3d 538; 431 N.E.2d 716; 1982 Ill. App. LEXIS 1406; 59 Ill. Dec. 215



January 28, 1982, Filed


PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] Appeal from the Circuit Court of Peoria County; the Hon. CHARLES WILSON, Judge, presiding.



DISPOSITION: Reversed and remanded.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiff contractor sought review of a decision of the Circuit Court of Peoria County (Illinois), which granted a motion to dismiss filed by defendant State and dismissed the contractor's breach of contract action on the grounds of sovereign immunity.


OVERVIEW: The contractor entered into a contract with the State. It later brought an action against the State for breach of contract. The trial court granted the State's motion to dismiss, pursuant to the State's sovereign immunity. The contractor sought review, and the court found reversible error. The court held that the doctrine of sovereign immunity did not bar the action because the State had consented to being sued by entering into a validly authorized contract with the contractor. The court found that, in order for a contract to exist at all, there had to be mutuality. That meant that the contract had to be enforceable by both contracting parties. Therefore, the State's claim that it could sue on a contract, but could not be sued in return was untenable. The court held that when the State entered into a contract with a private party, it was acting in a business or proprietary capacity. In so doing, it laid aside whatever privilege of sovereign immunity it otherwise possessed and bound itself to performance, just as any private citizen would do by so contracting.


OUTCOME: The court reversed and remanded the findings of the trial court for further proceedings.

CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: The State sought review of a decision of the Circuit Court of Lake County (Illinois), which granted defendant's motion to suppress and bar testimony of arresting officers finding that the officer's testimony was inadmissible under Ill. Rev. Stat. ch. 111, para. 2701 (1979). The State claimed that the officers were exempt from the provisions of § 2701.


OVERVIEW: Defendant learned that for every 10 driving while under the influence arrests made, an officer would receive one day off with pay. The motivation could have a bearing on the weight or credibility of the evidence, however, the court could not say defendant suffered a deprivation of due process as a result of the day-off policy.


OUTCOME: The judgment of the trial court was reversed and the cause was remanded.

CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed from his conviction in the Circuit Court of McHenry County (Illinois) for the offense of child pornography (Ill. Rev. Stat. ch. 38, para. 11-20a (1979)), contending that the statute was unconstitutionally overbroad, unconstitutionally vague, and violated due process requirements.


OVERVIEW: Defendant was convicted of child pornography, and in affirming his conviction the court held that Ill. Rev. Stat. ch. 38, para. 11-20(a) (1979) was not unconstitutionally vague or overbroad. Defendant relinquished any constitutional protections when he removed the child pornography from the confines of his own home and exhibited it to another. Unlike other forms of obscenity legislation, the evil sought to be prevented by the child pornography statute was not any alleged "antisocial behavior" which might result from the viewing of such material. The commonly understood meaning of the word "exhibit" implied both public and private displays, so that defendant was properly prosecuted thereunder. Since § 11-20a clearly incorporated the obscenity standards articulated by the Supreme Court, defendant's contention that the statute was unconstitutionally vague was rejected.


OUTCOME: The court found no constitutional infirmity with the child pornography statute under which defendant was prosecuted, and his conviction was affirmed.
