Appeal denied by People ex rel. T-Mobile USA, Inc. v. Vill. of Hawthorn Woods, 968 N.E.2d 1067, 2012 Ill. LEXIS 914, 360 Ill. Dec. 314 (Ill., May 30, 2012)


PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] 
Appeal from the Circuit Court of Lake County. No. 09-MR-26. Honorable Christopher C. Starck, Judge, Presiding.



DISPOSITION: Affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: The Circuit Court of Lake County, Illinois, granted judgment in favor of plaintiff communications company's suit against defendant village, alleging that two of the ordinances that forcibly annexed land adjacent to the village were invalid because they improperly used as a boundary a water feature that did not constitute a "creek," in violation of 65 ILCS 5/7-1-13 (2008). The village appealed.


OVERVIEW: The appellate court noted that the question was what constituted a "creek" under 65 ILCS 5/7-1-13. The village argued that the statute permitted both natural and unnatural boundaries, such as municipal and state boundaries. The appellate court found that although the statute itself did not contain the word "natural," the plain meaning of the word "creek" required a natural water feature. The term "creek" as used in § 7-1-13 unambiguously connoted a natural rather than man-made water feature. The village had never previously relied on the use of the water feature or its characterization as a creek. The trial court's finding that the water feature was man-made was not against the manifest weight of the evidence. The question boiled down to a batter of the experts, and it was the trial court's role to resolve conflicts in the evidence, assess witnesses' credibility, and determine what weight to give to their testimony. The trial court's acceptance of the expert's opinion that the water feature did not have the characteristics of a creek was not arbitrary or unreasonable. The water feature's straight lines lent additional support to the conclusion that it was man-made.


OUTCOME: The judgment was affirmed.

PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] 
Appeal from Circuit Court of Macoupin County No. 09L23. Honorable Patrick J. Londrigan, Judge Presiding.



DISPOSITION: Affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiffs, claimant and spouse, filed a two-count complaint against defendant property owner to recover damages for injuries sustained by the claimant at the owner's residence. A jury for the Circuit Court of Macoupin County, Illinois, found in favor of the owner and against plaintiffs. The plaintiffs appealed.


OVERVIEW: The claimant went to the owner's home to put up Halloween decorations and fell while standing on a wooden wire spool. The claimant alleged the owner failed to provide a safe and stable platform from which he could work, and failed to warn him that the spool he stood upon was or could be unstable and easily tipped. The claimant acknowledged the spool was designed to hold wire and not for a person to stand on. The claimant stated he did not check the stability of the spool before stepping on it, and also did not ask or look for a ladder. Plaintiffs argued that the trial court improperly denied admission into evidence a statement made by the owner that he offered to pay all of the claimant's expenses pertaining to the incident. The appellate court ruled that the statement at issue was barred by 735 ILCS 5/8-1901 (2004) and Ill. R. Evid. 409. Also, there was no err in admitting the shed photographs into evidence. The photos of the shed were relevant. The pictures showed the interior of the shed where the accident took place, as well as the white-painted spool that the claimant stepped on before falling.


OUTCOME: The judgment was affirmed.

SUBSEQUENT HISTORY: Released for Publication April 13, 2012.
Appeal granted by People v. Cardona, 968 N.E.2d 1068, 2012 Ill. LEXIS 890, 360 Ill. Dec. 315 (Ill., 2012)
Affirmed by People v. Cardona, 2013 IL 114076, 2013 Ill. LEXIS 305 (2013)


PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] 
Appeal from the Circuit Court of Lake County. No. 07-CF-2036. Honorable Fred L. Foreman, Judge Presiding.



DISPOSITION: Affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed a judgment of the Circuit Court of Du Page County, Illinois, that required him to register as a sex offender, arguing that the circuit court could not logically have found his unlawful restraint conviction to have been sexually motivated where the circuit court acquitted him of the more traditional sex crime with which he was charged, indecent solicitation of a child.


OVERVIEW: At a discharge hearing under 725 ILCS 5/104-25 (2008), defendant was acquitted of indecent solicitation of a child, but a judgment of nonacquittal was entered on a charge of unlawful restraint. Defendant appealed a subsequent judgment requiring him to register as a sex offender. On review, the court held that the inquiry of whether a person was proven guilty beyond a reasonable doubt of indecent solicitation was entirely different from the inquiry of whether an unlawful restraint was sexually motivated. The former required a finding beyond a reasonable doubt that defendant, with the intent that the offense of predatory criminal sexual assault of a child be committed, knowingly solicited a child under age 17 to perform an act of sexual penetration pursuant to 720 ILCS 5/11-6(a) (2008), while the latter required a factual finding that the facts underlying the offense of unlawful restraint indicate conduct of a sexual nature or show an intent to engage in sexual behavior pursuant to 20 ILCS 4026/10(e) (2008). That the State was unable to meet the high burden of proving the former did not preclude a court from making the factual finding that the latter, lower standard was satisfied.


OUTCOME: The judgment of the circuit court was affirmed.

PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] 
Appeal from the Circuit Court of St. Clair County. No. 09-CF-596. Honorable John Baricevic, Judge, presiding.



DISPOSITION: Affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant was found guilty of armed robbery with a firearm in violation of 720 ILCS 5/18-2(a)(2) (2008). The Circuit Court of St. Clair County, Illinois, sentenced him to 22 years' imprisonment to be followed by a 3-year period of mandatory supervised release. Defendant appealed.


OVERVIEW: In a criminal appeal, defendant argued the trial court erred by applying a 15-year sentence enhancement to his sentence pursuant to 720 ILCS 5/18-2(b) (2010) based on the use of a firearm during the commission of the robbery. According to defendant, the 15-year sentence enhancement could not be applied against him because it was declared unconstitutional. The Appellate Court of Illinois upheld his sentence. The legislature revived the 15-year sentencing enhancement in the armed-robbery statute by enacting 720 ILCS 5/33A-2(a) (2008); therefore, the trial court correctly applied the sentence enhancement against defendant at sentencing.


OUTCOME: The judgment was affirmed.

PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] 
Appeal from the Circuit Court of Cook County. No. 09 CR 1693. The Honorable James B. Linn, Judge Presiding.



DISPOSITION: Reversed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed the judgment of the Circuit Court of Cook County, Illinois, convicting him of three counts of aggravated unlawful use of a weapon in violation of 720 ILCS 5/24-1.6 (2008), sentencing him to 12 months of probation, and ordering him to pay a $5 court system fee.


OVERVIEW: During the search of defendant's vehicle, the officer recovered a weapon. Because the officer testified that he found the gun in the closed and latched center console, it was "encased" for purposes of 720 ILCS 5/24-1.6(a)(1), (a)(3)(A) (2008); the evidence at trial did not prove that the gun was loaded and immediately accessible. The Appellate Court of Illinois vacated defendant's conviction for a second count of aggravated unlawful use of a weapon, because the evidence showed he had a valid firearm owner's identification card. Defendant's conviction for the third count was vacated, because there was insufficient proof that he had a loaded firearm on a public street. Proof of the corpus delicti could not rest solely on defendant's statement. Besides a hearsay statement from an anonymous witness to the police, no other witness claimed to have seen defendant on the street with the weapon. The $5 court system fee assessed to him pursuant to 55 ILCS 5/5-1101(a) (2006) was vacated, because defendant was not convicted of violating the Illinois Vehicle Code, 625 ILCS 5/1-100 et seq. (2006).


OUTCOME: The judgment was reversed. The $5 court system fee was vacated.
