OVERVIEW: Defendant and a co-worker worked and drank all day, then argued over a crowbar outside the co-worker's trailer. Defendant's vehicle got stuck in a ditch. His co-worker's girlfriend was in the trailer. When she refused to give him a light for a cigarette, he became enraged and bashed the trailer and its windows with a baseball bat, eventually gaining entry. The girlfriend ran out the back door and called police. Defendant contended the trial court erred when it failed to include criminal trespass, La. Rev. Stat. Ann. § 14:63, as a responsive verdict. The trial court's instructions to the jury included responsive verdicts for unauthorized entry of an inhabited dwelling, the attempt offense, and not guilty. Defendant did not object to these instructions or request a charge on the responsive verdict of criminal trespass. Under La. Code Crim. Proc. Ann. art. 801(C), an objection was required to preserve this error for appellate review. In this case, no exception to the objection requirement was warranted because there was no misstatement or error involving the definition of the crime of which defendant was convicted. His maximum sentence did not shock the court's conscience.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed defendant's conviction, habitual offender adjudication, and sentence.

DISPOSITION: SENTENCE AFFIRMED AS AMENDED; REMANDED WITH INSTRUCTIONS.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant pleaded guilty to distribution of crack cocaine in violation of La. Rev. Stat. Ann. § 40:967 in the 13th Judicial District Court, Parish of Evangeline, Louisiana, and two other charges were dismissed. Defendant was sentenced to serve twenty years at hard labor with the first five years of the sentence to be served without diminution of sentence. Defendant appealed.


OVERVIEW: Defendant sold crack cocaine to an undercover agent. In reviewing the record for errors patent, the court held that the trial court erroneously denied the defendant eligibility for diminution of sentence. Under La. Code Crim. Proc. Ann. art. 890.1, the trial court had discretion to deny or place conditions on the defendant's eligibility for diminution of sentence only if defendant was convicted of or pleaded guilty to a crime of violence. The court held that because defendant did not raise the trial court's failure to fully consider mitigating factors at the sentencing hearing or in his motion to reconsider sentence, it could not be raised on appeal under La. Code Crim. Proc. Ann. art. 881.1(E) and La. Ct. App. Unif. R. 1-3. Defendant argued that the sentence was excessive for a non-violent offender suffering from a lifelong drug problem. Distribution of crack cocaine was punishable by imprisonment at hard labor for two to thirty years with the first two years of the sentence to be served without benefit of probation, parole, or suspension of sentence, under La. Rev. Stat. Ann. § 40:967. The court held that this sentence was not an abuse of discretion.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed defendant's sentence. However, the portion of the sentence in which the trial court ordered the first five years of the defendant's sentence to be served without eligibility for diminution of sentence was amended to delete that portion of the sentence.

DISPOSITION: CONVICTIONS AND SIMPLE ESCAPE SENTENCE AFFIRMED; POSSESSION WITH INTENT TO DISTRIBUTE COCAINE SENTENCE AMENDED AND SENTENCE AFFIRMED AS AMENDED.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed a judgment of the 19th Judicial District Court in and for the Parish of East Baton Rouge, Louisiana, that convicted him of simple escape and possession of cocaine with intent to distribute and that sentenced him as a second felony habitual offender, arguing that the trial court erred in imposing an excessive sentence.


OVERVIEW: Defendant was arrested after being stopped for driving a vehicle without a license plate and after he denied possessing his driver's license. He was placed into the police unit, and after the officer searched defendant's car and discovered cocaine, defendant escaped from the police unit and fled. On review, the court held that, under La. Code Crim. Proc. Ann. art. 881.1, because defendant only filed a motion to reconsider the sentence imposed on the possession charge, he was precluded appealing the sentence imposed on the escape conviction. While the trial court did not err in imposing the maximum sentence, it erred in denying good time diminution. Because possession with intent to distribute under La. Rev. Stat. Ann. § 40:967 was not enumerated in La. Rev. Stat. Ann. § 14:2(13) as an offense for which good time credits could be withheld and because the offense did not involve the use or threatened use of physical force against the person or property of another, the offense was not a crime of violence as defined in La. Rev. Stat. Ann. § 14:2(13), and the trial court erred in denying the diminution of sentence.


OUTCOME: Defendant's convictions were affirmed, as was the sentence imposed upon the simple escape conviction. The sentence imposed on the possession conviction was amended by the deletion of the denial of good time diminution; as amended, the sentence was affirmed.
