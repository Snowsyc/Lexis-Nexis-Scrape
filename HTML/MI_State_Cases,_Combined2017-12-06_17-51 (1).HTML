Lincoln v. GMC


No. 113063


SUPREME COURT OF MICHIGAN


461 Mich. 483; 607 N.W.2d 73; 2000 Mich. LEXIS 446


November 8, 1999, Argued  
March 8, 2000, Decided  
March 8, 2000, Filed


SUBSEQUENT HISTORY: Rehearing denied by Lincoln v. GMC, 461 Mich. 1289, 626 N.W.2d 411, 2000 Mich. LEXIS 593 (2000)


PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] Worker's Compensation Appellate Commission Court of Appeals, MACKENZIE and MURPHY, JJ., and WHITBECK, P.J. (Docket No. 204560).
Lincoln v. GMC, 231 Mich. App. 262, 586 N.W.2d 241, 1998 Mich. App. LEXIS 250 (1998)



DISPOSITION: Judgment of the Court of Appeals and decision of the Worker's Compensation Appellate Commission affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant state injury fund appealed from the Michigan Court of Appeals affirming a decision of the Worker's Compensation Appellate Commission, which granted the contested disability benefits.


OVERVIEW: In 1966, plaintiff suffered serious leg injuries in the course of his employment at an automobile plant, leaving him totally and permanently disabled. The issue before the court was the calculation of plaintiff's current disability benefits. Under Mich. Comp. Laws § 418.351(2), totally and permanently disabled employees whose date of injury preceded July 1, 1968, were entitled to the compensation that was payable to the employee immediately before the effective date § 418.351(2). Such persons, referred to as "the Eva King people," had the benefit of the 50-percent benefit floor of Mich. Comp. Laws § 418.351(2), rather than the 25-percent floor found in Mich. Comp. Laws § 418.356(3). Judicial precedent determining that Eva King people had the benefit of the higher calculation was given full retroactive effect in favor of those, such as plaintiff, who did not initially object to the statutory reductions of benefits.


OUTCOME: Judgment affirmed; judicial precedent determining that "the Eva King people," such as plaintiff, had the benefit of statutory provisions permitting greater disability benefits applied retroactively to plaintiff.

American Axle & Mfg., Inc. v. City of Hamtramck


No. 112053


SUPREME COURT OF MICHIGAN


461 Mich. 352; 604 N.W.2d 330; 2000 Mich. LEXIS 234


October 13, 1999, Argued  
February 1, 2000, Decided  
February 1, 2000, Filed


PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] Tax Tribunal Court of Appeals, SAWYER, P.J., and SAAD and GAGE, JJ. 227 Mich. App. 135, 575 N.W.2d 296 (1997) (Docket No. 191628).



DISPOSITION: Judgment of the Court of Appeals and the order of the Michigan Tax Tribunal are reversed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed from the judgment of the Tax Tribunal Court of Appeals (Michigan), which affirmed the judgment of the Michigan Tax Tribunal in favor of plaintiff in a refund action on the ground that adding a judgment levy tax against defendant violated the Headlee Amendment, Mich. Const. art 9, §§ 25-34 (1963).


OVERVIEW: A judgment was entered against defendant city and it assessed the unpaid amount of the judgment on the tax rolls under the Revised Judicature Act, Mich. Comp. Laws § 600.6093 (Mich. Stat. Ann. § 27A.6093). Plaintiff paid the tax and petitioned for a refund. The trial court found for plaintiff on the ground that adding the judgment to the tax rolls violated the Headlee Amendment, Mich. Const. art 9, §§ 25-34 (1963). The appellate court affirmed. Judgment reversed because defendant's authority to assess judgment taxes pre-existed the adoption of the constitutional amendment and therefore the assessment did not constitute a violation of the Headlee Amendment, Mich. Const. art 9, §§ 25-34 (1963), or the home rule cities act or the city charter.


OUTCOME: Judgment in favor of plaintiff was reversed because defendant's authority to assess judgment taxes pre-existed the adoption of the constitutional amendment and therefore the assessment did not constitute a violation of the Headlee Amendment or the home rule cities act or the city charter.
