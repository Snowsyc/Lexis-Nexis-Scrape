OVERVIEW: Defendant was convicted after police found two concealed weapons within the vehicle in which defendant was riding. OUTCOME: The court affirmed the decision of the circuit court.

PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed a judgment from the Circuit Court for Milwaukee County (Wisconsin), which convicted him of carrying a concealed weapon in violation of Wis. OVERVIEW: Defendant owned a grocery store that had been robbed several times. Officers entered the store to conduct a license check and asked defendant if he kept a gun in the store. Following his conviction for carrying a concealed weapon (CWW), defendant argued that his conviction impaired his constitutional rights under Wis. Const. art. I, § 25. The court agreed. OVERVIEW: Petitioner may have desired to resign as judge and run for county executive, and if elected, hold office. The court interpreted the phrase "during the term for which elected" as used in Wis. Const. art. VII, § 10(1). DISPOSITION: Reversed and cause remanded.


CASE SUMMARY:


OVERVIEW: HOLDINGS: [1]-In an action by an asbestos worker's estate against a supplier of asbestos materials to his various job sites, the estate raised a genuine issue of fact as to whether the materials were provided exclusively for a permanent improvement to property or whether they were provided for repair or maintenance; [2]-To the extent the asbestos-containing materials were provided for repair or maintenance, the statute of repose for defects in construction of improvements to real property, Wis. Stat. § 893.89(2) (2013-14) possibly would not apply; [3]-The estate presented deposition testimony of the worker that he was repairing various pipes and existing structures at five sites; it was the jury's province to determine what evidence to believe on the question of whether the work done at these five worksites was repair and maintenance, or was exclusively for new construction.


OUTCOME: Grant of summary judgment reversed and case remanded for trial.
