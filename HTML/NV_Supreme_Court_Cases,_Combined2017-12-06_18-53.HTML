Geddings v. State


No. 61849


SUPREME COURT OF NEVADA


2016 Nev. Unpub. LEXIS 470



February 25, 2016, Filed


NOTICE: AN UNPUBLISHED ORDER SHALL NOT BE REGARDED AS PRECEDENT AND SHALL NOT BE CITED AS LEGAL AUTHORITY. SCR 123.


CASE SUMMARY:


OVERVIEW: HOLDINGS: [1]-State proved that defendant committed murder by child neglect under Nev. Rev. Stat. § 200.070(1) and child neglect with substantial bodily harm under Nev. Rev. Stat. § 0.060(1)-(2) because defendant stated that he placed a large and heavy tank on a "wobbly" chair and then allowed a sixteen-month-old child to play in the area; admitted that he saw the child pushing on the chair; and further stated that after the accident he noticed the child was pale, stiff, and unresponsive, but he failed to get medical attention; [2]-Double jeopardy did not preclude the two charges because each offense contained an element that the other did not; [3]-Court erred in denying the motion to suppress the statements; the court should have ruled that defendant was in custody because he did not reasonably believe that he was free to leave when being interviewed at the police station.


OUTCOME: Reversed and remanded.

Cox v. State


No. 66644


SUPREME COURT OF NEVADA


2016 Nev. Unpub. LEXIS 506



February 2, 2016, Filed


NOTICE: AN UNPUBLISHED ORDER SHALL NOT BE REGARDED AS PRECEDENT AND SHALL NOT BE CITED AS LEGAL AUTHORITY. SCR 123.


CASE SUMMARY:


OVERVIEW: HOLDINGS: [1]-Because the State had to prove that defendant intended to hit the three-year-old victim with his car, the district court erred in allowing the State to argue that he could be found guilty of aggravated battery under Nev. Rev. Stat. § 200.481(1)(a) if he purposely drove in a dangerous manner; hence, the State produced insufficient evidence to support the battery conviction; [2]-The term "forthwith" in Nev. Rev. Stat. § 484E.030(2) (2009) was not unconstitutionally vague and the State presented sufficient evidence to support defendant's conviction for leaving the scene of an accident; he waited for 12 minutes at the scene, he left the scene without providing his information, he was located by the police 34 minutes later after a witness reported his license plate to the police, and when he presented himself to the police, he was not wearing shoes and smoking a cigarette.


OUTCOME: Affirmed in part and reversed in part.
