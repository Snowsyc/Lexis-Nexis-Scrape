2007 Minn. Tax LEXIS 7



January 29, 2007, Decided


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Petitioner taxpayer filed petitions objecting to respondent county's valuation of its property used for a department store, for property taxes assessed on January 2, 2003, January 2, 2004, and January 2, 2005. The county moved to dismiss.


OVERVIEW: The county alleged that, although the taxpayer provided some summary profit and loss statements to the county relative to the January 2, 2003 petition, the information provided by the taxpayer did not contain real estate related expenses required to be paid by the department store under the lease. In addition, the county requested that the January 2, 2004 and January 2, 2005 petitions be dismissed because the taxpayer failed to submit any available income and expense information to the county. The plain language of the 60 Day Rule, Minn. Stat. § 278.05, subd. 6(a). (2003), and prior case law required the production of the information, including the type of information that the taxpayer failed to submit. Further, the arguments concerning reliance (i.e. the taxpayer did not "know" what was to be provided), had no merit. The taxpayer failed to produce the real estate expense/lease information as required by the 60 Day Rule for the January 2, 2003 assessment. In addition, the taxpayer failed to provide any information for the January 2, 2004, and January 2, 2005, assessments. Thus, the taxpayer had not complied with the 60 Day Rule.


OUTCOME: The county's motion to dismiss was granted.
