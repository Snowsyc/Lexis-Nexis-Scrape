CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant was ticketed for operating a personal watercraft after hours, in violation of Minn. Stat. § 86B.313, subd. 1(a)(2) (2000). His motion to dismiss the charge was denied, and after a trial, the Scott County District Court (Minnesota) entered a judgment of conviction. On appeal defendant challenged his conviction, arguing that the statute was unconstitutionally vague and violated U.S. Const. amend. XIV, § 1, and Minn. Const. art. 1, § 2.


OVERVIEW: On appeal from his conviction for operating a personal watercraft within one hour of sunset in violation of Minn. Stat. § 86B.313, subd. 1(a)(2) (2000), defendant claimed that statute was unconstitutionally vague and that there was no rational basis for such treatment of personal watercraft when there were no similar restrictions on other motorized watercraft. The court held that the trial court did not err in holding that § 86B.313, subd. 1(a)(2), did not violate the Equal Protection Clause of the Minnesota or United States Constitutions, and that the statute was not unconstitutionally vague. Personal watercraft were substantially smaller and, arguably, more vulnerable than other motorboats; exceedingly maneuverable; and could be harder to see. Such distinctions were relevant to the public safety concern.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment.
