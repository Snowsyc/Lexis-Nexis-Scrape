OVERVIEW: After a committee was unable to agree on a recommendation on a proposal to boycott Israeli goods and financial investments, the directors adopted a resolution approving the proposed boycott. The Kitsap County Superior Court, Washington, entered a judgment of termination based on a finding that the mother had cognitive impairments that would never allow her to parent the child on her own. The mother appealed.


OVERVIEW: The mother was diagnosed with a cognitive disorder. The trial court terminated parental rights on the basis of a finding that the mother was currently unfit because her cognitive impairments prevented her from making intuitive judgments, grasping child development, perceiving subtle dangers to children, understanding the impact of things on children, or communicating effectively with her child. The court also found that there were no services that could be offered to the mother because her cognitive impairments could not be changed. OUTCOME: The appellate court reversed the judgment of termination and remanded the case for further proceedings.
