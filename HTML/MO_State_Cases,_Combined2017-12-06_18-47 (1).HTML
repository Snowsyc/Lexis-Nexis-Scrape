CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: The relator sought discharge from confinement as a sexually violent predator (SVP) pursuant to Mo. Rev. Stat. § 632.498 (2000), asking that respondent, a judge, be prohibited from applying § 632.498 (Supp. 2006) to his original petition for release. A writ of mandamus had been issued in the matter.


OVERVIEW: The relator sought discharge from confinement as a SVP pursuant to Mo. Rev. Stat. § 632.498 (2000). He asked the supreme court to prohibit the judge from applying § 632.498 (Supp. 2006), the amended version of the statute that became effective in June 2006, to his original petition for release filed in 2002. The supreme court held that § 632.498 (Supp. 2006) applied to the relator's case. Thus, the preliminary writ of mandamus previously issued was quashed. Under the amended statute, the State had the burden to prove by clear and convincing evidence that the committed person's mental abnormality remained such that the person was not safe to be at large and if released was likely to engage in acts of sexual violence. The jury needed to determined if, at the time of retrial, the relator remained a SVP. Contrary to the relator's assertions, the amendments to § 632.498 (Supp. 2006) were prospective in nature, not retrospective.


OUTCOME: The supreme court quashed the preliminary writ of mandamus that had previously issued.
