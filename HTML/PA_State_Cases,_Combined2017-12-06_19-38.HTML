CASE SUMMARY:


OVERVIEW: HOLDINGS: [1]-In support of an affirmance on appeal, the court noted that it properly treated an inmate's petition for a writ of habeas corpus as a second Post Conviction Relief Act (PCRA) petition, as he raised claims that were cognizable under the PCRA, and the way he labeled his filing was not controlling; [2]-Moreover, the inmate's petition was patently untimely on its face, as it was filed more than 10 years after the date that the sentence became final for PCRA purposes under 42 Pa.C.S. § 9545(b)(3) and none of the exceptions to timeliness under § 9545(b)(1)(i)-(iii) were applicable; [3]-The holding of Alleyne was not an applicable exception under § 9545(b)(1)(iii) because it was not to be applied retroactively to secure review of the merits of facially-untimely petitions, and the petition was not filed within 60 days of that decision for purposes of § 9545(b)(2).


OUTCOME: Affirmance recommended.
