PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1]

State Dep't of Corr. v. R.I. Bhd. of Corr. Officers, 2010 R.I. Super. LEXIS 128 (2010)


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: The Rhode Island Department of Corrections (DOC) filed a petition seeking to vacate, or, in the alternative, to modify, an arbitration award for a union in dispute over weapons training for correctional officers (COs) under R.I. Gen. Laws §§ 28-9-18(a) and 28-9-20 (1956). DOC appealed the grant by the Providence County Superior Court, Rhode Island, of the union's motion to confirm the award under R.I. Gen. Laws § 28-9-17 (1956).


OVERVIEW: The state's highest court held that DOC's proposal to abandon yearly on-range weapons qualification after R.I. Gen. Laws § 11-47-17 (1956) was amended was based on budgetary concerns. DOC claimed that under R.I. Gen. Laws § 42-56-10(14) (1956), its director had the authority and duty to establish training programs for COs. However, that duty and authority was not exclusive. The union was entitled under R.I. Gen. Laws § 28-9-1 (1956) to negotiate over matters that directly affected its members' work and welfare, and § 42-56-10(14) did not prevent the DOC director from exercising his power in consultation with the union. The dispute was arbitrable. The arbitrator's reading of § 11-47-17 as meaning that COs had to undergo weapons qualification at least every two years, and prescribing a maximum time period, rather than a minimum or fixed time period, within which COs had to complete weapons qualification was passably plausible.
