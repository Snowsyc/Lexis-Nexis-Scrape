266 S.W.3d 126; 2008 Tex. App. LEXIS 6565



August 26, 2008, Delivered


NOTICE: PUBLISH


PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] 
FROM COUNTY COURT AT LAW NO. 1 OF WICHITA COUNTY.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant challenged a decision from the County Court at Law of Wichita County, Texas, which convicted him of violating a permanent injunction for gang members, issued pursuant to Tex. Penal Code Ann. § 71.021 (2003) and Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code Ann. § 125.065 (2005).


OVERVIEW: Defendant was named as a gang member, and a permanent injunction was issued under Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code Ann. § 125.065, which included a curfew. A police officer recognized defendant after the hours set forth in the curfew. He was arrested, a plea bargain was entered, and this appeal followed. In affirming, the appellate court determined that Tex. Penal Code Ann. § 71.021 (2003) was not overbroad under the First Amendment. It did not restrict a substantial amount of constitutionally protected conduct; the only conduct it restricted was a knowing violation of a court order. Moreover, it was not void for vagueness. The clear language of the statute would have given an individual in defendant's position clear notice that violating the curfew amounted to a criminal offense. Further, it was not arbitrarily applied; instead, it prevented arbitrary enforcement through the establishment of certain guidelines. Also, the separation of powers doctrine was not violated by § 125.065. Finally, an issue relating to the constitutionality of the injunction's curfew provision was not preserved for appellate review.


OUTCOME: The decision was affirmed.

PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiff prisoner appealed an order from the 411th District Court, Polk County (Texas), which dismissed his action, which alleged that defendants state criminal justice employees violated plaintiff's civil rights under color of law by bringing false disciplinary charges against him, for failure to comply with the Texas Civil Practice and Remedies Code requirements for suits filed by inmates.


OVERVIEW: The court of appeals upheld an order dismissing plaintiff prisoner's action, which alleged that defendants state criminal justice employees violated plaintiff's civil rights under color of law by bringing false disciplinary charges against him, for failure to comply with the Texas Civil Practice and Remedies Code requirements for suits filed by inmates. Considering federal law imposed substantially similar requirements on inmates litigating in forma pauperis, the court concluded that Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. OUTCOME: Order, which dismissed plaintiff prisoner's action, which alleged that defendants state criminal justice employees violated plaintiff's civil rights under color of law by bringing false disciplinary charges against him, for failure to comply with the Texas Civil Practice and Remedies Code requirements for suits filed by inmates, affirmed because these requirements neither violated the United States Constitution nor the Texas Constitution.

DISPOSITION: Reversed and Rendered.

FOR APPELLANT: The Honorable John Cornyn, Attorney General. Mr. Joseph A. Pitner, Assistant Attorney General, Austin, TX.

FOR APPELLEE: Mr. J. David Bickham, Jr., Vinson & Elkins, L.L.P., Austin, TX.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellants sought review of an order of the District Court of Travis County, 53rd Judicial District (Texas), which held that Tex. Lab. Code Ann. §§ 403.007(a), 408.184(c) and 28 Tex. Admin. Code §§ 132.10-.12 were unconstitutional to the extent they required appellee's members to contribute to appellant second injury fund.


OVERVIEW: Appellee municipal risk fund was a joint insurance fund consisting of more than 1600 cities that chose to collectively self-insure. Appellant fund was a workers' compensation insurance subsequent injury fund, supported through mandatory contributions from workers' compensation insurance carriers. Appellant fund was administered by appellant commission, the state workers' compensation commission. In 1997, appellant commission ruled that the municipal members of appellee were required to pay into appellant fund any unclaimed death benefits pursuant to Tex. Lab. Code Ann. §§ 403.007, 408.184 and the related regulations, 28 Tex. Admin. Code §§ 132.10-.12. In response, appellee brought an action seeking declaratory relief that §§ 403.007, 408.184 and Admin. Code §§ 132.10-.12 were unconstitutional as applied to the cities participating in appellee. The trial court agreed. Appellants then sought review. The court reversed, and rendered judgment for appellants, because there was no unconstitutional redistribution of municipal funds, and appellee failed to prove that §§ 403.007, 408.184 and §§ 132.10-.12 were unconstitutional as applied.


OUTCOME: The court reversed, and rendered judgment for appellants, because the statutes and regulations requiring the members of appellee to contribute to appellant second injury fund were not unconstitutional, as there was no change in title to the contributed funds, and appellee failed to show that the challenged provisions were unconstitutional as applied.

Bowles v. State


NO. 01-04-00801-CV


COURT OF APPEALS OF TEXAS, FIRST DISTRICT, HOUSTON


2006 Tex. App. LEXIS 7341



August 17, 2006, Opinion Issued


PRIOR HISTORY:  [*1] On Appeal from the 61st District Court. Harris County, Texas. Trial Court Cause No. 2002-44780.
Bowles v. State, 2001 Tex. App. LEXIS 6311 (Tex. App. Houston 14th Dist., Sept. 13, 2001)


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant sought review of an order from the 61st District Court, Harris County (Texas), which awarded summary judgment to appellee, the State of Texas, in an action for a declaration that the Texas Lien Release Law was unconstitutional.


OVERVIEW: Appellant filed three liens against property. It was held that the liens were not valid. When he refused to remove the liens, he was indicted for refusing to execute the release of a fraudulent lien or claim, in violation of Tex. Penal Code Ann. § 32.49(c) (2003). He was convicted of the Class A misdemeanor offense. He filed a petition for declaratory judgment, seeking to have the Texas Lien Release Law declared unconstitutional. The State moved for summary judgment. The trial court granted the motion. On appeal, the court found that appellant did not introduce any evidence of any collateral legal consequences that he had or would suffer as a result of his criminal conviction. He had an opportunity to challenge the validity of the Lien Release Law as a part of his criminal case, which he did not do. Under Tex. Code Crim. Proc. Ann. art. 12.02 (2005), the statute of limitations for a misdemeanor was two years; hence, appellant was no longer subject to prosecution based on the notices to remove the liens. A "naked" declaration that the Lien Release Law was invalid, without a valid request for injunctive relief, was not within the jurisdiction of a district court sitting in equity.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment of the trial court.

On Appeal from the County Criminal Court No. 4, Dallas County, Texas. Trial Court Cause No. MA08-60588-E.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant pleaded guilty in the County Criminal Court No. 4, Dallas County (Texas) to driving while intoxicated. The trial court had denied his motion to suppress. Appellant sought review.


OVERVIEW: Appellant raised various issues on appeal, but the court affirmed. An officer observed appellant violating Tex. Transp. Code Ann. § 545.104(a), and because appellant made a left turn, the officer could have found that it was appellant's intention to make it. The officer was authorized to stop appellant. As the officer was authorized to stop appellant for failing to signal his left turn, whether he was authorized to stop appellant for an additional traffic violation under Tex. Transp. Code Ann. § 545.101(d) was immaterial to whether the trial court erred in denying his motion to suppress. Appellant did not cite to any portion of the record where he asserted that a statute was unconstitutionally vague, and thus the issue was not preserved for review. Because the record did not show that the trial court ever received a request by appellant to file findings of fact and conclusions of law in connection with his motion to suppress, the trial court did not err in failing to file them.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed.
