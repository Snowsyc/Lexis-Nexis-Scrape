Review denied by Enoch v. State, 2013 Fla. LEXIS 82 (Fla., Jan. 18, 2013)


PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] 
An appeal from the Circuit Court for Alachua County. Mark W. Moseley, Judge.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: The Circuit Court for Alachua County (Florida) convicted defendant, upon a plea of no contest, under two statutes enacted to protect the public from crimes committed by criminal gangs, the gang recruitment provision, § 874.05(1), Fla. Stat. (2009), and the electronic communication regulation, § 874.11, Fla. Stat. (2009). Defendant appealed, challenging the constitutionality of each statute.


OVERVIEW: To prove that defendant violated the statutes, the State intended to introduce a DVD containing YouTube videos of defendant. The gang recruitment provision was enacted to protect the public from speech and conduct used to encourage gang membership, where a condition of membership or continued membership was the commission of any crime. The statute passed constitutional muster under U.S. Const. amend. I, and Art. I, § 4, Fla. Const., did not violate the Due Process Clause of Art. I, § 9, Fla. Const., and was not void for vagueness under U.S. Const. amend. V, as applied to defendant. The electronic communication regulation, on the other hand, proscribed the use of electronic communication to intimidate or harass others, or to advertise one's presence in the community, for the purpose of benefiting, promoting, or furthering the interests of a criminal gang. Because § 874.11, Fla. Stat., substantially tread upon protected speech and expressive conduct, associational activity, and other innocent acts, and could not have been suitably narrowed to comport with federal and state constitutional requirements, the court was constrained to reverse the conviction and sentence on that count.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed defendant's conviction and sentence on the gang recruitment count, but reversed the conviction and sentence on the electronic communications count.
