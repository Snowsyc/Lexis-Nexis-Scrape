Subsequent appeal at State v. Beltran, 2007 Haw. LEXIS 341 (Haw., Nov. 16, 2007)


PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] APPEAL FROM THE DISTRICT COURT OF THE FIRST CIRCUIT. HPD Cr. No. 5402729MO. HPD Cr. No. 5426494MO. HPD Cr. No. 5402730MO.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: The District Court of the First Circuit (Hawai'i), separately convicted three defendants of camping without a permit, which violated Honolulu, Haw., Rev. Ordinances § 10-1.3(a)(2) (1990, as am. Ord. 96-58). The trial court sentenced them to pay a $ 20 fine, payment of which was suspended pending appeal. Defendants filed separate appeals, which the intermediate appellate court consolidated.


OVERVIEW: Defendants were each arrested by citation for allegedly camping without a permit at a city park. Defendants moved to dismiss the charges against them. The intermediate appellate court uncovered no evidence of a plainly appearing legislative purpose to impose absolute liability for the camping-without-a-permit offense defined by the camping ordinance. Therefore, pursuant to Haw. Rev. Stat. § 702-213(1) (1993), the camping-without-a-permit offense was reduced to a violation punishable by fine, forfeiture, or other civil penalty unless defendants were specifically charged pursuant to Haw. Rev. Stat. § 702-213(2) (1993) with committing the offense with a negligent state of mind. To determine the state of mine element, the case had to be remanded to the trial court. In addition, the camping ordinance was not unconstitutionally vague because most individuals had a common-sense understanding of what camping involved and the definition of camping provided by the director of the city parks department provided sufficiently definite guidelines and examples to put a person of ordinary intelligence on fair notice of the types of activities that constituted camping.


OUTCOME: The judgments were vacated and the cases were remanded to the trial court to determine the classification of the offense with which defendants were charged. If the charge did not allege a negligent-state-of-mind element, a judgment would be entered against them for camping without a permit as a civil violation. If the charge included a negligent or higher state of mind, they would be convicted of a misdemeanor offense of camping without a permit.

CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Petitioner inmate appealed a decision of the Circuit Court of the First Circuit (Hawai'i) summarily dismissing his Haw. R. Penal P. 40 petition (Rule 40 petition) for post-conviction relief. The inmate was convicted of second-degree murder under Haw. Rev. Stat. §§ 707-701.5 (1985 & Supp. 1990) and 706-656 (Supp. 1990).


OVERVIEW: A mistrial was declared, and the inmate opted to enter into a plea agreement whereby the State would recommend 10 years. The trial court deferred to the Hawai'i Parol Authority (HPA) for sentencing. The inmate's attorney did not appear at the sentencing hearing and the inmate was sentenced to 30 years.
