DePascale v. State


DOCKET NO. A-000897-11, MOTION NO. M-001292-11


SUPERIOR COURT OF NEW JERSEY, APPELLATE DIVISION


2011 N.J. Super. Unpub. LEXIS 3168


November 14, 2011, Submitted 
November 18, 2011, Decided


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant, the State of New Jersey, moved for a stay of the judgment of a trial court (New Jersey), which declared L. 2011, c. 78 unconstitutional as applied to justices and judges and prohibited its further application.


OVERVIEW: The court noted that the polestar in the trial court and on appeal was the issue of judicial independence. The narrower question was whether L. 2011, c. 78 violated N.J. Const. art. VI, § 6, para. 6, which prohibits a diminution of judicial salaries. The court set forth the case law factors to be considered when considering a stay pending an appeal and held that the State failed to meet that burden. The court dismissed the State's contention that judicial independence would not be impacted during the pendency of the appeal. The court held that the public's interest in maintaining a strong and independent judiciary was imperiled by any violation of N.J. Const. art. VI, § 6, para. 6, no matter how extensive and regardless of its duration. As such, the court found that the public interest would be harmed by the stay, which compelled a denial of the State's motion.


OUTCOME: The court denied the State's motion for a stay.
