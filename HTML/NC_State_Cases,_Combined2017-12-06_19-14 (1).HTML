DISPOSITION: Affirmed in part; reversed and remanded in part.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant pharmaceutical companies challenged an order from a trial court in New Hanover County (North Carolina), which certified plaintiff drug buyers' class action lawsuit that asserted various claims against them for allegedly inflating the price of a prescription drug. One of the companies appealed a separate order denying its motion to amend its answer to add a crossclaim.


OVERVIEW: The buyers filed an action alleging that the companies inflated the price of the drug, thus defrauding patients and insurance companies. The buyers brought claims of civil conspiracy, tortious conduct of another, common law fraud, and unfair and deceptive practices in violation of N.C. Gen. Stat. § 75-1.1. They then moved for class certification under N.C. Gen. Stat., N.C. R. Civ. P. 23(a). The trial court granted class certification and the companies appealed. The buyers argued in response that the order was interlocutory and the appeal should be dismissed. The court found, as a result of the significant impact of the lawsuit, the importance of the issues involved, and the need for efficient administration of justice, it would exercise its discretion to reach the merits of the appeal. It then found that, according to the buyers' evidence, differences existed between North Carolina law and the law of the other jurisdictions on each substantive claim, thus, violating due process rights. Certification was improper without findings by the trial court on whether North Carolina's substantive law was sufficiently similar to other states' law to avoid due process problems.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the order denying the one company's request to amend its answer. The court reversed the order certifying the class action and the court remanded the action for findings of fact on whether the state's substantive law was similar to other states' substantive law on the buyers' claims so that choice of the state's law would not violate due process.
