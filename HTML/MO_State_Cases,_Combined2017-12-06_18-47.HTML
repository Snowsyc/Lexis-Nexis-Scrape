OVERVIEW: Appellant argued that §§ 632.480-632.513 violated his right to due process, that the evidence was insufficient to prove he was an SVP, and that the trial court erred in admitting evidence (1) that he suffered from antisocial personality disorder (ASPD) and (2) of actuarial instruments as applied to him. Thus, there was sufficient evidence of the link between ASPD and sexually violent behavior for the jury to find appellant was a SVP. There was no abuse of discretion in admitting evidence of the actuarial instruments, Mo. Rev. Stat. § 632.065 (2000).


OUTCOME: The judgment was affirmed.

The Circuit Court of Cole County, Missouri, ruled in the association's favor, declaring that the three provisions in question were invalid, unenforceable, and severed from the remainder of the bill. The Cole County Circuit Court, Missouri, denied the corporation's motion for summary judgment. The corporation appealed.


OVERVIEW: The corporation was a retail store that sold animal feeds and products. The pharmacy board investigated the corporation for selling animal legend drugs without a pharmacy license, alleging the corporation was in violation of the Missouri Pharmacy Practices Act, Mo. Rev.
