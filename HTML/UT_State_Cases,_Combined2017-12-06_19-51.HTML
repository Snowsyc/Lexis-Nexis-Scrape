OVERVIEW: The court of appeals reversed the trial court's denial of defendant's motion to dismiss, as the State conceded there was insufficient evidence to convict under the corpus delicti rule. As a result, the court abandoned the corpus delicti rule and replaced it with the trustworthiness standard. The standard altered the sufficiency of the evidence that was required to convict under the corpus delicti rule, as it required less or different evidence than required by the corpus delicti rule.


OUTCOME: The judgment was affirmed.
