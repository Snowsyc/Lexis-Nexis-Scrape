The word "shall" in the remedy provision of the anti-SLAPP statute, Wash. Rev. At no point did the trial court differentiate between the movants in its ruling.


OUTCOME: The decision was reversed and remanded with instructions to enter judgment in an amount that included statutory damages of $ 50,000.

SUBSEQUENT HISTORY: Review denied by West v. Wash. Ass'n of Cities, 176 Wn.2d 1012, 297 P.3d 709, 2013 Wash. LEXIS 115 (2013)
Related proceeding at West v. Port of Tacoma, 2014 Wash. App. LEXIS 399 (Wash. Ct. App., Feb. 20, 2014)


PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] 
Appeal from Thurston Superior Court. Docket No: 07-2-02399-0. Judgment or order under review. Date filed: 06/09/2010. Judge signing: Honorable Bruce W. Hilyer.
West v. Thurston County, 168 Wn. App. 162, 275 P.3d 1200, 2012 Wash. App. LEXIS 1075 (2012)


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: The Thurston Superior Court, Washington, dismissed appellant resident's complaints alleging Public Record Act (PRA), Wash. Rev. Code ch. 42.56, violations, conspiracy, fraud, and unconstitutional expenditure of public funds against respondents, county and Port. The resident appealed.


OVERVIEW: The resident argued that the trial court erred in granting the Port's unopposed motion to summarily dismiss the claims against it. The appellate court found that because the resident failed to establish any genuine issues of material fact, the trial court did not err in granting summary judgment to the Port. The resident did not oppose the Port's summary judgment motion and failed to submit any evidentiary support for his claims or allegations. Further, the resident failed to clarify his claims such that the trial court was sufficiently apprised of a justiciable controversy it could rule on. Thus, even if the record before the trial court contained sufficient evidence supporting the resident's allegations--which it did not--the trial court still could not issue a ruling answering the merits of his claims because he failed to present it with a justiciable controversy. The resident's appeal in relation to the Port was frivolous. He presented no debatable point of law and the chance for reversal was nonexistent. Attorney fees were awarded to the Port.


OUTCOME: The judgment was affirmed.

169 Wn. App. 652; 281 P.3d 331; 2012 Wash. App. LEXIS 1752

Appeal from Grant Superior Court. Docket No: 04-1-00557-7. Judgment or order under review. Date filed: 05/27/2011. Judge signing: Honorable Evan E Sperline.



DISPOSITION: Affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed a judgment of the Grant Superior Court (Washington) extending under Wash. Rev. Code § 9.94A.709 one of his 2004 community custody conditions imposed in his first-degree child molestation conviction.


OVERVIEW: The court held that the extension of defendant's community custody restriction against contact with minors did not violate ex post facto or double jeopardy principles because the plain statutory language in effect in both 2004 and 2011, former Wash. Rev. Code § 9.94A.715(5) (2007) and Wash. Rev. Code § 9.94A.709, allowed the trial court to extend community custody conditions up to the maximum allowable sentence. Defendant's judgment and sentence clearly stated that the maximum term was life, and therefore no new punishment or multiple punishments for the same offense were imposed. Wash. Rev. Code § 9.94A.709 did not violate due process because it clearly specified the time frame to extend conditions (prior to the completion of community custody), the criteria (public safety), and the consequence (extension of conditions).


OUTCOME: The judgment was affirmed.

State v. Schenck


No. 41401-5-II


COURT OF APPEALS OF WASHINGTON, DIVISION TWO


169 Wn. App. 633; 281 P.3d 321; 2012 Wash. App. LEXIS 1745


April 13, 2012, Oral Argument 
July 24, 2012, Filed


SUBSEQUENT HISTORY: Subsequent appeal at, Decision reached on appeal by State v. Schenck, 2013 Wash. App. LEXIS 387 (Wash. Ct. App., Feb. 26, 2013)


PRIOR HISTORY:  [***1] 
Appeal from Cowlitz Superior Court. Docket No: 00-1-00414-9. Judgment or order under review. Date filed: 10/28/2010. Judge signing: Honorable James J Stonier, James Warme, Stephen Warning.
State v. Schenck, 119 Wn. App. 1037, 2003 Wash. App. LEXIS 3429 (2003)


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: The Cowlitz Superior Court, Washington, found that defendant violated the terms of his community placement by residing in Cowlitz County instead of Thurston County and failing to report to his community corrections officer in Thurston County. The trial court imposed 20 days' confinement for defendant's violation. Defendant appealed.


OVERVIEW: The appellate court noted that because it dismissed defendant's personal restraint petition (PRP) on ripeness grounds and because the Washington Supreme Court denied discretionary review of that dismissal, there was not a final judgment and collateral estoppel did not apply. Thus, defendant was not barred from bringing the appeal. The 2007 statutory "county of origin" condition on community placement was triggered and properly applied prospectively to him on his 2010 release from prison, Wash. Rev. Code § 72.09.270. Its application did not impair any of defendant's bested rights in preexisting law or change the legal consequences of his past criminal conduct. The precipitating event for the statute was defendant's release from prison in May 2010. Thus, the 2007 statute was prospective as applied to defendant. The 2007 statute neither made a formerly discretionary penalty mandatory nor imposed additional legal obligations on the conditions of defendant's community placement. Applying the statute to defendant did not increase the quantum of punishment to which he was subject and did not violate the prohibition against ex post facto laws.


OUTCOME: The judgment was affirmed.
