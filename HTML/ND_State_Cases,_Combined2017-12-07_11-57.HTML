DISPOSITION: REVERSED AND REMANDED.


CASE SUMMARY:


OVERVIEW: ISSUE: Whether the evidence supported a police officer's determination that he had reasonable suspicion to believe defendant was in violation of N.D.C.C. § 39-21-25(2). HOLDINGS: [1]-The district court erred in suppressing evidence seized after a traffic stop on the ground that § 39-21-25(2) was unconstitutionally vague because the officer's belief that the law prohibited operating a vehicle with more than four illuminated front facing lights was objectively reasonable under the circumstances; [2]-The validity of the stop did not turn on the constitutionality of the statute but on deciding whether reasonable suspicion existed at the time of the traffic stop to believe defendant was in violation of § 39-21-25(2), and the evidence supported a reasonable suspicion he was in violation of the statute.


OUTCOME: Order reversed.

Code § 32-12.2-02(3)(d), barred the husband's claims for money damages. Further, if the husband was unhappy with the judges' decisions, his remedy was to appeal the decisions or petition for rehearing. He failed to use the remedies available to redress any wrongs he might have suffered, and collateral estoppel barred him from relitigating the issues in a second action. Finally, defendants were entitled to appellate attorney fees because the husband's appeal was frivolous under N.D. R. App. P. 38.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment of the trial court. The court held that defendants were entitled to appellate attorney fees. The court remanded the case to the trial court for a determination of reasonable appellate attorney fees.
