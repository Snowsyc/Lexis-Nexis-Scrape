DISPOSITION: Judgment of the Court of Appeals affirming the district court is reversed. Judgment of the district court is reversed and remanded with directions.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: After violating probation, defendant's upward durational departure sentence of 14 months was imposed. The Court of Appeals (Kansas) upheld both the sentence and the probation revocation, and defendant appealed.


OVERVIEW: Defendant argued that her underlying upward departure sentence should have been vacated, as the court previously held that the Kansas scheme for imposing upward departure sentences was unconstitutional on its face and any sentence imposed thereunder was void. State argued that the prior court ruling did not apply to void defendant's sentence, as she pled guilty to her crimes, thus waiving her right to a jury trial, and consequently, her right to have a jury determine beyond a reasonable doubt any aggravating factors supporting her departure sentence. The court determined that State simply failed to present a valid reason for retreating from the courts prior holding.


OUTCOME: The sentence was vacated and was remanded for resentencing.

The police violated the pair's rights by asking questions unrelated to the traffic stop and by running a warrant check not only on the driver but on defendant as well, and while the police may have had a hunch that the driver and defendant were up to something, there was no particularized and objective evidence to support a reasonable suspicion of criminal activity and to detain them any further. Although the pair ultimately consented to a search of the vehicle, the consent was insufficient to remove the taint of the of the unlawful detention under the Fourth Amendment and Kan. Const. Bill Rights § 15 because there was a close proximity between the constitutional violation and consent and because there were no intervening circumstances present to purge the taint of the constitutional violation.


OUTCOME: The district court erred in denying defendant's suppression motion. Because the evidence establishing defendant's guilt was suppressed, his convictions were set aside, and he was discharged from further proceedings.
