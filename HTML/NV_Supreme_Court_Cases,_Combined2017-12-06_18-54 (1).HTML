Butler v. State


No. 68218


COURT OF APPEALS OF NEVADA


2016 Nev. App. Unpub. LEXIS 153



February 18, 2016, Filed


NOTICE: AN UNPUBLISHED ORDER SHALL NOT BE REGARDED AS PRECEDENT AND SHALL NOT BE CITED AS LEGAL AUTHORITY. SCR 123.


CASE SUMMARY:


OVERVIEW: HOLDINGS: [1]-Defendant was denied no good time credit because he was not entitled to Nev. Rev. Stat. § 209.4465(1)(c) credit for probation, as he was not subject to the Department of Corrections, he was not entitled to credit for house arrest or time in a homeless youth center, and he got credit for time in a boot camp; [2]-His sentence was not an abuse of discretion because his original sentence was reduced and within the limits of Nev. Rev. Stat. §§ 193.140, 199.480(1)(a), 200.380(2), and 202.290(2), it was not based on impalpable or highly suspect evidence, he did not allege bias or object to sentencing remarks, and nothing showed bias against him or the court's closed mind; [3]-His sentence was not cruel and unusual because it was within statutory limits and he did not claim the statutes were unconstitutional, it did not shock the conscience, and it was not grossly disproportionate.


OUTCOME: Judgment affirmed.

Howard v. State


No. 68361


COURT OF APPEALS OF NEVADA


2016 Nev. App. Unpub. LEXIS 177



February 17, 2016, Filed


PRIOR HISTORY: Howard v. State, 2014 Nev. Unpub. LEXIS 362 (2014)


CASE SUMMARY:


OVERVIEW: HOLDINGS: [1]-In a case where defendant was convicted of being a felon in possession of an electronic stun device, Nev. Rev. Stat. § 202.357 was not unconstitutionally vague because it was sufficiently definite to allow a person of ordinary intelligence to understand what conduct was prohibited; it provided adequate standards to prevent discriminatory enforcement; and defendant's ignorance of the statute did not render it void for vagueness; [2]- Nev. Rev. Stat. § 202.357 was not unconstitutionally overbroad because it did not improperly interfere with First Amendment rights; and, while it established a strict liability crime that was completed when a felon gained possession of an electronic stun device, defendant did not make a clear showing of its invalidity.


OUTCOME: Judgment and order affirmed.
