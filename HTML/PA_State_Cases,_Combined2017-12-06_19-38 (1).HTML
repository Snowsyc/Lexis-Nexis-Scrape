In support of an affirmance on appeal, it was noted that the second PCR motion was filed nearly two years after the date on which the inmate's appeal from his conviction and sentence became final, such that it was clearly untimely under 42 Pa.C.S. § 9545(b)(1). Further, there was no showing that the motion was within any of the exceptions to timeliness under § 9545(b)(1)(i)-(iii). Lastly, the filing of the second motion was procedurally barred while the first motion was still pending pursuant to § 9545(b)(2). The court noted that the inmate's substantive reliance on a case precedent to support his sentencing claim was erroneous, as it did not apply retroactively to the inmate's matter.


OUTCOME: The court recommended an affirmance on appeal.
