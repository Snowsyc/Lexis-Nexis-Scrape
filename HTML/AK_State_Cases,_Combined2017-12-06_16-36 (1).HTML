DISPOSITION: District court judgment reversed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed from a judgment on jury verdict of the Fourth Judicial District Court, Fairbanks (Alaska), convicting him of violating Alaska Stat. § 11.71.060(a) by possessing less than eight ounces of marijuana. He argued on appeal, among other things, that he had been engaging in conduct protected by Alaska Const. art. I, § 22.


OVERVIEW: Defendant was arrested for marijuana possession after officers found harvested plants in his home. He argued a defense of necessity, but also challenged the constitutionality of the statute. The appellate court agreed that, in the Ravin decision, the state's highest court held that possession of small amounts of marijuana, in the home, for personal use, was protected by the privacy guarantee in the Alaska constitution. After the Ravin decision, the marijuana possession statute had used four ounces as the cutoff point between small amounts and amounts for which a person could be prosecuted, but Alaska Stat. § 11.71.060, enacted by initiative, had re-criminalized possession of any amount of marijuana. The appellate court held that the people, by initiative, could no more enact an unconstitutional law than the legislature could, so, to uphold the constitutionality of § 11.71.060, it interpreted that statute as if the four-ounce dividing line had never been removed and directed a new trial to determine the amount defendant actually possessed. It also held that defendant should not have raised a defense of necessity when a statutory medical use defense was available to him.


OUTCOME: The court reversed the judgment and remanded for a new trial, at which the jury was to determine whether defendant possessed more or less than four ounces of marijuana.
