US Supreme Court certiorari denied by Maher v. Bank One, N.A., 131 S. Ct. 264, 178 L. Ed. 2d 140, 2010 U.S. LEXIS 6310 (U.S., 2010)


PRIOR HISTORY: APPEAL FROM THE SUPERIOR COURT OF GRAHAM COUNTY. Cause No. CV2006-193. Honorable C. Robert Pursley, Judge Pro Tempore.
Bank One, NA v. Maher, 2005 Ariz. App. Unpub. LEXIS 398 (2005)



DISPOSITION: AFFIRMED.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: The Superior Court of Graham County, Arizona, granted summary judgment in favor of appellees, bank and subsequent buyers, on the foreclosure by the bank of a deed of trust on a house owned by appellant homeowner, after the homeowner filed an action seeking to restore title to the house in his name and recover damages. The homeowner appealed.


OVERVIEW: The bank and the buyers argued that, pursuant to Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 33-811(C), the homeowner waived his claims by failing to seek a preliminary injunction before the sale. The appellate court noted that it was within the legislature's power to allow a person to acquire good title to real property based on another's failure to act timely. Any delay in the homeowner's receiving notice of the sale was a result of his intransigence and not the bank's failure to comply with governing law. Because the homeowner waived any defense or objection to the sale under § 33-811(C), he necessarily waived any claim for damages dependent on facts he could have raised as a defense or objection to the sale. Under any reasonable interpretation of the contract, the bank was not required to give the homeowner notice of the merger and could not have breached the contract by failing to do so. The homeowner filed his claim more than two years after his Fair Debt Collection Practices Act, 15 U.S.C.S. §§ 1692 through 1692p, claims had accrued and 15 U.S.C.S. § 1692k(d) barred his claims. The trial court did not err in denying the homeowner's request to delay its ruling on the summary judgment motions.


OUTCOME: The judgment was affirmed.
