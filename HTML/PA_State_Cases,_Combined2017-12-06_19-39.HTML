PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Plaintiff property owners filed suit against defendants, a county and various officers thereof, seeking to compel a county to conduct a countywide reassessment (CR). OVERVIEW: The owners brought suit, alleging that use of the same base year market value for an indefinite number of years violated the UC. OUTCOME: The court denied the owners' request for an interim reassessment, and ordered the county to perform the four-district CR plan that the court set out.

CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant filed an omnibus motion asking that his two convictions, for the unlawful killing of an elk and a deer, 34 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 2307(a), be consolidated for purposes of appeal, and moved for dismissal of the charges.


OVERVIEW: Defendant was convicted of unlawfully killing a deer and an elk in closed season, in violation of 43 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 2307(a). Defendant contended the killings were justified by the protection of his property. Defendant filed timely appeals, contending that the statute was unconstitutional on its face and as applied, and filed an omnibus motion to consolidate the appeals. The Commonwealth argued that under Pa. Cons. Stat. § 2121, defendant could legally kill a deer or elk only if it were damaging crops being cultivated as a primary means of gaining a livelihood. The court held that the provisions allowing regulations for the protection of wildlife were a valid exercise of police power and not unconstitutional, but only to the extent that the provisions were not applied so as to deny any property owner the right to offer the defense of justification in the protection of his property. Therefore the court ordered that the matter proceed to trial, allowing defendant to advance that defense.


OUTCOME: The court denied defendant's omnibus motion and ordered that the matter proceed to trial, with defendant permitted to defend on the basis of justification of his actions in defense of his property. The prohibition on killing of wildlife was not intended to strip all property owners other than farmers of their inherent right to protect their property.
