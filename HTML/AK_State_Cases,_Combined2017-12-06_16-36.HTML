Alaska Action Ctr., Inc. v. Municipality of Anchorage


Supreme Court No. S-11252, No. 5779


SUPREME COURT OF ALASKA


84 P.3d 989; 2004 Alas. LEXIS 19



February 6, 2004, Decided


PRIOR HISTORY: Appeal from the Superior Court of the State of Alaska, Third Judicial District, Anchorage, Mark Rindner, Judge. Superior Court No. 3AN-03-05921 CI.



DISPOSITION:  [**1] Affirmed.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: In an appeal concerning an initiative proposed by appellant citizens, the municipal clerk rejected the initiative petition on the advice of the municipal attorney, who maintained that it would constitute an impermissible appropriation. The citizens appealed the clerk's action to the Superior Court of the State of Alaska, Third Judicial District, Anchorage. The superior court granted judgment to appellee municipality. The citizens appealed.


OVERVIEW: The municipality owned several hundred undeveloped acres in the lower end of the valley. The citizens submitted their initiative petition to the municipal clerk for certification in accordance with Anchorage, Alaska, Mun. Code 2.05.050. The clerk refused to certify the petition, relying on an opinion letter from the municipal attorney, which advised that the petition was legally insufficient because it proposed an appropriation. The initiative designated the use of specified amounts of public assets in a way that encroached on the legislative branch's exclusive control over the allocation of state assets among competing needs. Also, the initiative intruded on decisions reserved by statute and constitution to the assembly by making an appropriation. Thus, the clerk was correct to refuse to place the initiative on the ballot because the clerk was acting within her authority when she rejected the initiative on the ground that it proposed to make an appropriation, which was an action that could not be taken by initiative. Without the impermissible park designation, the rest of the initiative could not go to the voters.


OUTCOME: The supreme court affirmed the superior court's judgment.

CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellants, the State and the Commissioner of Fish and Game, sought review of a judgment of the Superior Court of the State of Alaska, Third Judicial District, Anchorage, which held that a regulation promulgated by the Alaska Joint Boards of Fisheries and Game was invalid because it did not satisfy Alaska Stat. § 16.05.258(c).
