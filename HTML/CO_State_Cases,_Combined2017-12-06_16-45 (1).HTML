PRIOR HISTORY: Boulder County District Court No. 97DR499. Honorable Morris W. Sandstead, Judge.
In re Marriage of Balanson, 25 P.3d 28, 2001 Colo. LEXIS 437 (Colo., 2001)



DISPOSITION: Judgment reversed and case remanded with directions.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: The Boulder County District Court, Colorado, entered a determination that half of a trust would be distributed to appellant wife so long as her father predeceased her. The supreme court granted certiorari. On remand, the trial court conducted hearings. The trial court issued revised permanent orders. The wife appealed.


OVERVIEW: When the wife's mother died, the master trust was divided into Trust A, in which wife had only an expectancy interest, and Trust B, the corpus of which consisted solely of the parents' home. The trial court concluded that wife's vested remainder interest in Trust B constituted property. On appeal, the wife first contended the trial court erred as a matter of law by failing to apply Colo. Rev. Stat. § 14-10-113(7)(b) (2003) in calculating the marital appreciation of her interest in the trust. She maintained that, under the mandatory statutory language, her interest in the trust could not be classified as property until the trust became irrevocable after her mother's death. The appellate court agreed. The supreme court's mandate remanded the case to the trial court for reconsideration of the division of marital property, and it reiterated its earlier rule: a beneficiary's interest in a discretionary trust was not property, but merely an expectancy, as one's enforceable contractual right gave rise to a property interest, while an unenforceable interest constituted a mere expectancy. The appellate court further concluded § 14-10-113(7)(b) was not unconstitutionally retrospective.


OUTCOME: The appellate court reversed the trial court's judgment; the matter was a matter for further consideration.
