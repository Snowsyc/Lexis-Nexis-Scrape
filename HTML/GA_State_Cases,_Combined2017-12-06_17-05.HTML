The court disagreed, finding that the trial court properly concluded that the widower demonstrated a substantial need for the requested documents since they documented past car-to-car crash tests conducted by the auto manufacturer on a line of vehicles that included similar fuel tank locations and performance as the vehicle that was being driven by the wife. The trial court did err by holding that the application of O.C.G.A. § 24-9-67.1 to exclude the testimony of the widower's expert would have been unconstitutional but the admission of the expert's testimony was proper based on the hitch manufacturer's untimely motion seeking the exclusion.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment in favor of the widower as against the auto manufacturer. The court vacated, in part, the trial court's order denying the hitch manufacturer's motion to exclude the widower's expert testimony but, otherwise, affirmed the judgment in favor of the widower against the hitch manufacturer. The court dismissed the widower's cross-appeals.
