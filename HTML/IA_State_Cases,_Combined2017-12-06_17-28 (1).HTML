DISPOSITION: Reversed and remanded.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Decedent husband killed his wife and then killed himself. Appellee executor of the wife's estate requested a determination in the husband's estate that all jointly held property of the couple was property of the wife's estate. Appellant heirs of the husband resisted. The Wapello County District Court (Iowa) held the property passed to the wife's estate pursuant to Iowa Code § 633.535 (1999), Iowa's felonious death statute. The heirs appealed.


OVERVIEW: On appeal, the heirs argued that Iowa Code § 633.535(2) governed the disposition of joint tenancy property, and that under the statute only the husband's rights of survivorship were lost, not his undivided interest in the joint tenancy property. The heirs contended that the statute had the effect of terminating the joint tenancy, causing each tenant's undivided interest to pass through that tenant's estate. The appellate court agreed with the heirs and reversed. The supreme court noted that in § 633.535(2) the legislature expressly eliminated a murderer's right of survivorship, the hallmark characteristic of a joint tenancy; thus, the court held that the legislature intended that one joint tenant's murder of the other joint tenant severed the joint tenancy and created a tenancy in common. Thus, § 633.535(2) meant that the wife's share of the joint tenancy property passed through her estate and the husband's share of the joint tenancy property passed through his estate. Thus, the husband lost his survivorship rights, not his share of the joint tenancy property. The trial court erred in holding that the wife's estate was the owner of all joint tenancy property.


OUTCOME: The judgment of the district court was reversed and remanded.
