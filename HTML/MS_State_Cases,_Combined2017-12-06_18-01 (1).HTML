Nicholson v. State


NO. 1999-KA-01125-COA


COURT OF APPEALS OF MISSISSIPPI


761 So. 2d 924; 2000 Miss. App. LEXIS 268



June 6, 2000, Decided


PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] COURT FROM WHICH APPEALED: WINSTON COUNTY CIRCUIT COURT. DATE OF JUDGMENT: 05/19/1999 TRIAL JUDGE: HON. JOSEPH H. LOPER JR. TRIAL COURT DISPOSITION: CONVICTED OF FELONY DUI: SENTENCED TO A TERM OF FOUR YEARS WITH THE MDOC, TWO YEARS SUSPENDED. FINED $ 2000.



DISPOSITION: THE JUDGMENT OF THE WINSTON COUNTY CIRCUIT COURT OF CONVICTION OF FELONY DRIVING UNDER THE INFLUENCE AND SENTENCE TO FOUR YEARS IN THE CUSTODY OF THE MISSISSIPPI DEPARTMENT OF CORRECTIONS WITH TWO YEARS SUSPENDED AND TWO YEARS PROBATION, AND FINE OF $ 2,000 IS AFFIRMED. ALL COSTS OF THIS APPEAL ARE ASSESSED TO THE APPELLANT.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Appellant sought review of a decision by the Winston County Circuit Court, Mississippi, which convicted him of felony DUI and sentenced him to two years of imprisonment.


OVERVIEW: Appellant, after being stopped for driving erratically, submitted to an intoxilyzer test, which showed that his blood alcohol content was above the legal limit. He was charged for DUI and at his trial, evidence was introduced that he had two prior DUI convictions within five years of the new offense. He was convicted of felony DUI and sentenced to two years of confinement. He sought review and argued that the intoxilyzer results were within the margin of error, that trial court erred in admitting affidavits, and that the trial court erred in sentencing the defendant as a felony. The court affirmed. It found that the margin of error for the intoxilyzer was not properly before the court since it was a new argument and not reflected in the record. The citation and affidavit for running a stop sign were admissible because the officer was cross-examined and the citation was relevant to time of the traffic stop. The court did not err when it sentenced the appellant as a felony DUI because his two prior misdemeanor DUI convictions did not require representation.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the felony DUI conviction and sentence because an uncounseled DUI conviction, for which counsel was not constitutionally required, could be used to enhance punishment upon a subsequent DUI conviction.

Sellers v. State


NO. 1998-KA-01107-COA


COURT OF APPEALS OF MISSISSIPPI


773 So. 2d 350; 2000 Miss. App. LEXIS 156



April 11, 2000, Decided


SUBSEQUENT HISTORY: Petition for Rehearing Denied July 25, 2000.


PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] COURT FROM WHICH APPEALED: JACKSON COUNTY CIRCUIT COURT. DATE OF JUDGMENT: 11/04/1997. TRIAL JUDGE: HON. JAMES W. BACKSTROM. TRIAL COURT DISPOSITION: 11/04/1997: FELONY SHOPLIFTING THIRD OFFENSE AS A HABITUAL OFFENDER SENTENCED TO SERVE A SENTENCE OF 4 YEARS IN CUSTODY OF THE MDOC AS A HABITUAL OFFENDER WITHOUT BENEFIT OF PAROLE OR EARLY RELEASE PURSUANT TO MISS. CODE ANN 99-19-81 (1972). COSTS OF COURT ARE TAXED AGAINST THE DEFENDANT AND THE DEFENDANT IS HEREBY REMANDED TO THE CUSTODY OF THE MDOC.



DISPOSITION: THE JUDGMENT OF THE CIRCUIT COURT OF JACKSON COUNTY OF CONVICTION OF FELONY SHOPLIFTING AND SENTENCE AS A HABITUAL OFFENDER OF FOUR YEARS IN THE CUSTODY OF THE MISSISSIPPI DEPARTMENT OF CORRECTIONS IS AFFIRMED. ALL COSTS OF THIS APPEAL ARE ASSESSED TO JACKSON COUNTY.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed conviction for third offense shoplifting from Jackson County Circuit Court (Mississippi), asserting guard's testimony about items recovered from defendant was insufficient proof of ownership, error in jury instruction improperly shifting burden of proof, and jury was not allowed to pass on validity of prior convictions, a necessary element.


OVERVIEW: After his conviction for third offense shoplifting, defendant appealed, asserting guard's testimony about items recovered from defendant was insufficient proof of ownership, error in jury instruction improperly shifting burden of proof, and jury was not allowed to pass on validity of prior convictions, a necessary element. The court held guard's relationship with business, and comparison work on the items, was competent. Defendant failed to object to instruction at trial. Although not now allowed, the procedure of not having jury pass on prior convictions was at defendant's specific urging, in his motion in limine asking such evidence be excluded during trial, to gain a tactical advantage, to deny the jury knowledge of prior convictions for similar offenses. Any error was provoked by the defendant himself. It did not rise to an unconstitutional denial of the right to trial by jury. The conviction was affirmed.


OUTCOME: The conviction was affirmed. Although not now allowed, the procedure of not having jury pass on prior convictions was at defendant's specific urging, to deny the jury knowledge of prior convictions for similar offenses. It did not rise to an unconstitutional denial of the right to trial by jury.

Tompkins v. State


NO. 1998-KA-01806-COA


COURT OF APPEALS OF MISSISSIPPI


759 So. 2d 471; 2000 Miss. App. LEXIS 117



March 14, 2000, Decided


PRIOR HISTORY:  [**1] COURT FROM WHICH APPEALED: JACKSON COUNTY CIRCUIT COURT. DATE OF JUDGMENT: 09/01/1998. TRIAL JUDGE: HON. JAMES W. BACKSTROM. TRIAL COURT DISPOSITION: 09/01/1998: CAPITAL RAPE: SENTENCED TO LIFE IN THE MDOC.



DISPOSITION: JUDGMENT OF THE JACKSON COUNTY CIRCUIT COURT OF CONVICTION OF CAPITAL RAPE AFFIRMED. SENTENCE OF LIFE IMPRISONMENT VACATED AND CAUSE REMANDED.


CASE SUMMARY:


PROCEDURAL POSTURE: Defendant appealed his conviction of capital rape and sentence of life in prison by the Circuit Court of Jackson County, Mississippi.


OVERVIEW: Defendant was convicted of capital rape and sentenced to life in prison. Evidence of defendant's other acts with the victim was admissible because it tended to show the relationship between the parties. Evidence of acts subsequent to defendant's arrest was not admissible, but the admission was harmless error as all the evidence overwhelmingly supported the conviction. The jury instructions were valid, read as a whole and based on the wide recognition of the meaning of the terms used. Defense counsel's actions did not interfere with the jury reaching the proper verdict as dictated by the evidence, and so was not ineffective. Defendant's sentence was imposed under an outdated statute because the judge did not consider the amendment to the statute that took effect before defendant's sentencing.


OUTCOME: Conviction was affirmed because the challenged evidence relating to other acts was properly admitted, the jury instructions were proper, and defendant was afforded effective assistance of counsel; sentence was vacated and remanded because it was not imposed under the proper statute.
