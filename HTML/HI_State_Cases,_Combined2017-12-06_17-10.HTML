OVERVIEW: Defendant argued that he should have been afforded a jury trial because of the "penalty" of registration, he should not have been required to register, and portions of Haw. Rev. Stat. § 846E-1 were unconstitutionally vague and overbroad. The court held that defendant was not subject to registration as a sex offender, because indecent exposure was not an offense that entailed "criminal sexual conduct." Venue was proper, because the crime was committed in Kona on the island of Hawaii, which constituted the third judicial circuit. Because defendant attempted to prolong his conversation with the minor's mother and moved his chair into a position that allowed him to display his genitalia to the minor, the evidence enabled a person of reasonable caution to conclude that defendant's conduct was intentional under Haw. Rev. Stat. § 702-206. The minor's opinion that defendant acted intentionally met the three criteria of Haw. R. Evid. 701. Moreover, the trial court was accorded greater discretion in asking the minor questions because it held a jury-waived trial.


OUTCOME: The court affirmed the judgment and sentence, except that it vacated the requirement that defendant register as a sex offender.
